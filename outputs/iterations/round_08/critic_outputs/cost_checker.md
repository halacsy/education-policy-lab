# Critique: cost_checker

## S0.cost_categories
Objection: The audit cost is materially under-specified: the scenario names “audit design and fieldwork” but omits legal/data-access costs for KSH/Oktatási Hivatal/PISA microdata linkage, privacy compliance, maintainer data acquisition, and possible statutory mandate drafting. The evidence tags admit “no recent Hungary-specific audit exists” and “no direct precedent,” so treating the audit as merely medium one-time is unsupported; the source record supports feasibility uncertainty, not a bounded cost level.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Replace “medium, one-time” with a range separating scoping/legal authority, secure data linkage infrastructure, external econometric team, and maintainer-compliance costs; mark the fiscal range as low-confidence pending a formal audit procurement estimate.

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The scenario correctly distinguishes verified self-report from administrative linkage, but it still fails to price the largest fiscal sensitivity: whether the SES proxy becomes a pilot-only workaround or a reusable national student-level SES register. Those are different orders of magnitude. The cited evidence supports “no existing student-level SES register,” not that a 2-3 district pilot can contain the build cost if later national rollout is the intended decision path.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split SES-proxy costs into “pilot-only proxy” and “nationally scalable register architecture,” with separate one-time, recurring verification, privacy, appeals, and audit costs; state that rollout economics cannot be inferred from the pilot budget.

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The cost categories mix transition and steady-state costs in a way that obscures the fiscal case. “Compensatory funding or capacity expansion” is labeled high and recurring across the phase-down period, but the scenario does not distinguish temporary absorption costs during reduced selective intake from permanent higher funding needed to make general schools educationally viable after the phase-down. The education_finance evidence supports flat wage bills across tracks and weak transition-cost specifics; it does not support an unspecified recurring high-cost line without a capacity model.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Separate temporary transition costs, permanent steady-state operating costs, and capital capacity costs; tie each to projected pupil flows from reduced selective places and non-state offset assumptions.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: Teacher retraining is categorized as “high, recurring,” but retraining specialized gimnazium teachers for heterogeneous classrooms is primarily a transition cost, while ongoing costs would come from staffing ratios, mentoring, diagnostics, and remedial support. Calling retraining recurring blurs the transition-versus-steady-state distinction and risks double-counting with the separate “early diagnostic and remedial support” recurring line.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reclassify teacher retraining as high one-time or multi-year transition cost, then add a separate steady-state professional development/support-staff line if ongoing costs are intended.

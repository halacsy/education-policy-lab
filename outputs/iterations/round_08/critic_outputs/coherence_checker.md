# Critique: coherence_checker

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim that S1 “operates within the existing legal framework for admissions rather than requiring Nkt. amendment” contradicts S1.assumptions and S1.political_risks, which say no current legal mechanism compels non-state maintainers to adopt uniform equity criteria and that inclusion may require a ministerial decree, maintainer agreement, or legal mandate. The benefit overstates legal simplicity precisely where the scenario’s own mechanism depends on cross-maintainer participation for generalizable pilot evidence.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast the benefit as limited to state-maintained schools unless a separate legal instrument is enacted for non-state maintainers; state that a fully representative pilot may require legislative or contractual changes.

## S2.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism says a phase-down can proceed “via place-count regulation without full statutory repeal,” but the same bullet concedes the forms are legally anchored and that changes require legislative action. This creates an internal ambiguity between “incremental regulation” and “legislative change” that matters because S2’s comparative advantage over S3 rests on being administratively lighter than abolition.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Specify the exact legal pathway: either Nkt. amendment authorizing annual place caps, or existing delegated regulation under current law. If the latter is unsupported by the cited legal record, downgrade the mechanism’s evidence status.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim that abolition “removes the specific legal exposure created by age 10-12 differentiation under Article 66/a and EU equal-treatment scrutiny” is stronger than the evidence supports. The scenario elsewhere treats legal exposure as scrutiny risk, not an established violation; abolishing the differentiation point may reduce this risk, but “removes” implies the current structure is legally defective and that no substitute sorting mechanisms would create comparable equality concerns.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Replace “removes” with “reduces legal-scrutiny risk tied specifically to formal age-10/12 selection,” and note that choice/catchment or non-state sorting could preserve other equality risks.

## S4.goal
Objection: S4’s goal promises “narrowing outcome gaps between selected and non-selected pupils,” but the mechanism and evidence_status only support Portugal-style system-level gains and targeted support for disadvantaged general schools. They do not support narrowing the selected/non-selected gap, because selected pupils may also benefit from national curriculum standards and because the Portuguese evidence does not isolate TEIP’s effect on relative gaps under an age-10/12 selection regime.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reframe the goal as raising outcomes among disadvantaged general-school pupils and possibly narrowing some SES-linked gaps, without claiming a selected/non-selected gap reduction unless the scenario adds a measurement design and evidence for relative effects.

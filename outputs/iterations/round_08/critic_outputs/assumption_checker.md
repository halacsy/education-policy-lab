# Critique: assumption_checker

## S0.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes an “independent body (e.g., Oktatási Hivatal or an external commission)” can credibly conduct the audit, but Oktatási Hivatal is not independent of the state education governance structure whose policy is being audited. The expert record supports the need for an independent equity audit, not the assumption that an existing ministry-linked administrative body would satisfy political independence.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Specify an institutionally independent audit mechanism, such as an external research consortium with statutory data-access rights and publication guarantees, and distinguish technical capacity from political independence.

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim that admission reform “operates within the existing legal framework for admissions rather than requiring Nkt. amendment” is too strong. The scenario’s own assumptions and political_risks state that no current legal mechanism compels non-state maintainers to adopt uniform equity criteria, and that inclusion may require a ministerial decree, maintainer agreement, or legal instrument. The legal_and_governance evidence supports lower disruption than structural abolition, not that the pilot can be implemented cleanly within the existing framework across maintainers.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast the benefit as limited to state-maintained schools unless a new legal instrument is adopted, and separate a state-only pilot from a cross-maintainer pilot.

## S2.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism claims a legislated year-on-year reduction in state-maintained places can lower cohort exposure to early sorting, but the scenario simultaneously concedes that non-state maintainers may absorb displaced demand and that no proven regulatory lever currently constrains them. Given the expert record treats maintainer fragmentation and church/foundation expansion as structural risks, the mechanism only supports reducing state-sector selective places, not reducing national early-sorting exposure.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change the mechanism to “reduce state-maintained selective exposure” and make any national reduction explicitly conditional on legally validated caps or subsidy conditions covering non-state maintainers.

## S4.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that non-structural interventions are politically easier to implement and sustain than structural tracking reform is unsupported. The expert record supports that structural reform faces organized parent and maintainer resistance, but S4’s own political_risks acknowledge targeted funding may be cut during fiscal consolidation and lacks durable protection. Avoiding Nkt. amendment does not establish political sustainability of recurring TEIP-style funding.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Split “easier to launch” from “easier to sustain,” and mark long-term funding durability as unsupported unless backed by Hungary-specific budget-continuity evidence.

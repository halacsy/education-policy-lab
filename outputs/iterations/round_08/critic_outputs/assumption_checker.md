# Critique: assumption_checker

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that uniform admission-equity rules can be applied across state, church, and foundation maintainers contradicts the expert record, which states that maintainer plurality is a structural complication, not merely administrative, and that church/foundation growth is structural and not subject to state legislative control in the same way as state-school-district-centre schools.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Revise the assumption to acknowledge the structural limitations of applying uniform rules to non-state maintainers, or propose a mechanism within the scenario that credibly overcomes this structural barrier, such as specific incentives or negotiated agreements, rather than assuming uniform applicability.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that church and foundation maintainers can be regulated or incentivized not to expand selective places directly contradicts the expert record's minority position, which states that these maintainers are independent veto-players likely to absorb high-achieving students regardless of state-track phase-down.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Either remove this assumption or provide a concrete, credible mechanism within the scenario's implementation steps or political risks that addresses how non-state maintainers would be prevented from offsetting the state-sector reduction, acknowledging the structural nature of their autonomy.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that sorting does not simply migrate to other channels (school choice, non-state maintainers, catchment effects, private tutoring) to the point of neutralizing the equity gain is in direct tension with the scenario's own 'equity_impact', 'political_risks', and 'uncertainties' fields, which identify this migration as the largest risk.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reframe this as a critical uncertainty or risk that the scenario aims to mitigate, rather than an assumption. The scenario should explicitly state how it plans to address or monitor this migration to ensure the equity gain is realized, rather than assuming it away.

## S4.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that funding commitments for TEIP-style targeted programmes will be durable and not the first line cut during fiscal consolidation directly contradicts the scenario's own 'political_risks' field, which identifies these funding lines as 'likely first-line cuts during fiscal consolidation'.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Remove this assumption. Instead, the scenario should explicitly address how it plans to secure and sustain funding for these programs, perhaps by proposing a statutory funding floor with credible safeguards as mentioned in the 'implementation_steps' or by acknowledging this as a major political risk that needs specific mitigation strategies.

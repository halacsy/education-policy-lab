# Critique: devil_advocate

## S0.goal
Objection: The core claim that S0 “avoids indefinite deferral” is unsupported. The scenario itself labels the statutory deadline mechanism as weak and admits no Hungarian education-policy precedent shows such a deadline would bind government action. The cited legal/governance evidence supports the need for an audit and the difficulty of reform, not the enforceability of a decision deadline. Without a credible enforcement mechanism, S0 collapses into ordinary delay.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reframe S0 as “time-limited evidence gathering only if paired with a binding enforcement device,” and specify the sanction or automatic consequence if the government misses the deadline.

## S1.mechanism
Objection: The scenario’s load-bearing mechanism depends on a “gaming-resistant SES proxy,” but the evidence record does not support its feasibility. The scenario admits Hungary lacks a student-level SES register and that verified self-report or welfare/tax linkage would have to be built during the pilot. The evidence tags support the diagnosis of SES-skewed admissions, not the operational claim that SES-weighted admission can be administered fairly in 2-3 districts. If the proxy fails, the pilot tests administrative noise and gaming, not equity reform.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Make SES-proxy construction a prerequisite feasibility phase before any admissions pilot, with success criteria for accuracy, fraud resistance, privacy compliance, and appeal handling.

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: The claimed benefit of lowering early-sorting exposure is unsupported at the national level because the scenario’s own assumptions say non-state maintainers may offset state-place reductions. The cited school_network_planning and legal_and_governance evidence supports maintainer fragmentation and likely church/foundation expansion, not the scenario’s hoped-for containment. A state-only phase-down may merely move selective capacity into less transparent non-state institutions, leaving total exposure to early sorting unchanged.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split the benefit into “state-sector exposure reduction” and “national exposure reduction,” and make the latter conditional on an enacted, legally tested cap covering state-subsidized non-state maintainers.

## S4.mechanism
Objection: The Portugal analogy is too weak to carry S4. The cited OECD/Crato evidence supports Portugal’s bundled system-level PISA gains, but it does not support offsetting Hungary’s age-10/12 selection-driven SES gradient while retaining early selection unchanged. Portugal is evidence for non-structural improvement under Portuguese institutions, not for neutralizing Hungary’s specific early-tracking mechanism. The scenario’s mitigation claim therefore remains transferability speculation.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the mechanism claim to “may improve general-school outcomes independent of selection,” and remove any implication that the package can materially offset early-selection inequity without Hungary-specific evidence.

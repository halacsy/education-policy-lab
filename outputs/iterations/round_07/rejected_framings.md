# REJECTED FRAMINGS — scenario_builder, ROUND 07

This document records candidate framings considered for S1–S4 and the framing chosen for each, with rejection rationale tied to the rubric dimensions (evidence discipline, uncertainty discipline, coherence with unresolved disagreement).

## S1 — Admission reform within the current structure

**CHOSEN:**
- Retain the 6- and 8-year gimnazium tracks but rewrite entry rules and quotas so that access to selective places is less strongly skewed toward high-SES, urban families, while acknowledging that the scenario's own evidence and uncertainties question whether a workable SES indicator can be built at application time.

**REJECTED FRAMINGS:**

- **REJECTED: "Assessment redesign can meaningfully reduce coaching advantage"** — reason: The coherence_checker (R06) and evidence_checker (R06) identified that the value-added studies (Horn 2013; Kertesi & Kezdi 2013) do not establish coaching-resistance in a redesigned assessment; they only establish that the raw gimnazium advantage is mostly selection effect. Framing this as a solved mechanism rather than a contested one would upgrade the evidence status without warrant. The chosen framing flags the mechanism as [evidence: contested] and the assumption as unverified.

- **REJECTED: "Reduced test-preparation arms-race pressure is a reliable expected benefit"** — reason: The coherence_checker (R06) noted a direct contradiction: the political_risks section identifies informal selection (interviews, reputation) as a plausible outcome if reformed channels become scarce, which would reproduce SES sorting. The chosen framing downgrades this benefit to [evidence: weak — model knowledge] and flags the contradiction explicitly.

- **REJECTED: "Non-state maintainers will align with state admission rules voluntarily"** — reason: The devil_advocate (R06) flagged that this assumption is unsupported by mechanism or evidence. The scenario does not establish why church/foundation schools would adopt comparable rules rather than absorb displaced selective demand. The chosen framing flags this as an assumption without evidence status and marks the uncertainty as [confidence: low].

- **REJECTED: "SES-proxy gaming can be ruled out by good design"** — reason: The political_feasibility input (embedded in political_risks) makes clear that SES-proxy instruments are gameable and opposition softens only if pilot data show weak gaming, not if theory claims it unlikely. The chosen framing flags gaming as a political risk, not a solved design problem.

## S2 — Gradual phase-down of 6/8-year entry places

**CHOSEN:**
- Progressively reduce the number of state-maintained 6- and 8-year entry places over a decade, shifting more of each cohort into the common track until age 14, while acknowledging that caps may be offset by non-state maintainers.

**REJECTED FRAMINGS:**

- **REJECTED: "Caps on state-maintained schools achieve the equity goal without amendment to admission rules"** — reason: The equity_checker (R06) and coherence_checker (R06) identified a direct logical contradiction: as caps tighten, residual selective places become even more SES-skewed absent an S1-type rule change. The scenario's own equity_impact section flags this as "near-certain" (fewer seats, same skewed applicant pool). Framing this as an independent path to equity gain would erase the tension. The chosen framing preserves the contradiction and flags the residual-skew effect as mechanical.

- **REJECTED: "Non-state maintainers will be bound by the same caps"** — reason: The devil_advocate (R06) and feasibility_checker (R06) noted that the assumption is directly contradicted by the scenario's own political_risks section, which identifies non-state expansion as an offset mechanism. The chosen framing flags this as an assumption [assumption — directly contradicted by this scenario's own political risks and uncertainties].

- **REJECTED: "Peer-composition improvement in urban general schools is a strong mechanism"** — reason: The evidence_checker (R06) and coherence_checker (R06) identified that the peer-effect mechanism is [evidence: contested — the asymmetric peer-effect mechanism is tagged weak/contested in equity inputs (model knowledge)]. The chosen framing preserves this contested tag rather than presenting it as moderate or strong.

- **REJECTED: "The reform is durable across electoral cycles"** — reason: The assumption_checker (R06) found no basis for durability claims. The chosen framing flags reversal risk and marks ownership-durability as [confidence: medium], conditional on coalition stability.

## S3 — Comprehensive school to age 14 (structural reform)

**CHOSEN:**
- Abolish 6- and 8-year selective entry and defer academic tracking to age 14 via a common comprehensive lower-secondary phase, bundled (Finnish-style) with teacher-quality and diagnostic-support investment.

**REJECTED FRAMINGS:**

- **REJECTED: "Sorting does not migrate to school choice or non-state maintainers"** — reason: The assumption_checker (R06) and devil_advocate (R06) identified a direct logical tension: this is asserted as an assumption, yet the equity_impact field calls exactly this migration "the largest risk." The coherence_checker (R06) flagged the unresolved contradiction. The chosen framing preserves the tension by flagging it as an assumption and marking the uncertainty [confidence: low — would be reduced by: within-system sorting comparisons under different admission rules; maintainer registry data].

- **REJECTED: "Teacher supply can be stabilized to staff heterogeneous comprehensive classes"** — reason: The implementation_planning input provided strong teacher-shortage evidence. The assumption_checker (R06) noted this directly contradicts the viability claim. The chosen framing flags this as an assumption [assumption — contradicted by implementation_planning's strong teacher-shortage finding] and marks the uncertainty [confidence: low].

- **REJECTED: "Political ownership survives two electoral cycles"** — reason: The assumption_checker (R06) identified that this contradicts the political_feasibility expert's stated position that no such durable window exists. The chosen framing flags this as [assumption — contradicted by political_feasibility, which states no durable window exists] and marks ownership-durability [confidence: medium].

- **REJECTED: "The peer-effect channel is a strong equity mechanism"** — reason: The evidence_checker (R06) and coherence_checker (R06) flagged the asymmetric peer-effect mechanism as [evidence: contested — model knowledge]. Presenting it as a strong link would upgrade evidence without warrant. The chosen framing preserves [evidence: contested].

- **REJECTED: "Middle-class flight does not harm equity outcomes"** — reason: The equity_checker (R06) noted that the scenario claims flight "does not harm the exiting pupils" but then acknowledges exit can "relocate rather than remove sorting." This is internally contradictory: relocation of sorting is harm to those left behind. The chosen framing flags the net equity gain as "bounded by how much displacement occurs — a magnitude the inputs do not resolve."

## S4 — Keep the structure, compensate general schools (Portuguese-style package)

**CHOSEN:**
- Retain the 6- and 8-year gimnazium structure unchanged and route intensive non-structural support (TEIP-style targeted funding, curriculum standards, reduced retention) to disadvantaged general schools, while acknowledging that this compensates downstream rather than altering the sorting mechanism.

**REJECTED FRAMINGS:**

- **REJECTED: "Compensation can offset, not merely mask, early sorting"** — reason: The assumption_checker (R06) and evidence_checker (R06) identified that no country has demonstrated offsetting compensation at Hungary's age-10/12 selection age; the Portuguese case did not select as early. This assumption is unverifiable ex ante and is directly in tension with the scenario's own [evidence: weak] status. The chosen framing flags this as [assumption — unverifiable ex ante: no country evidence exists at Hungary's selection age] and downgrades any offsetting claim to speculative.

- **REJECTED: "The reform is politically less disruptive than S2/S3"** — reason: The devil_advocate (R06) and political_risk_checker (R06) noted that this claim is contradicted by the scenario's own political_risks section, which highlights budget vulnerability (first-line cut in consolidation) and entrenchment risk (legitimizing early selection permanently). The chosen framing flags this as [evidence: weak — the 'less disruptive' claim is contradicted by budget-fragility and entrenchment risks].

- **REJECTED: "Portuguese TEIP mechanisms will transfer reliably to Hungary"** — reason: The assumption_checker (R06) identified this as an unverified assumption. The uncertainty [confidence: low — would be reduced by: pilot evaluation in Hungarian disadvantaged schools] is more honest than framing transfer as likely.

- **REJECTED: "Targeted funding will be ring-fenced and durable"** — reason: The cost_checker (R06) and political_risk_checker (R06) identified that this line is historically the first cut in fiscal consolidation in Hungary. The chosen framing flags durability risk and marks the uncertainty [confidence: medium — would be reduced by: analysis of budgetary commitments and legislative continuity], not [confidence: high].

- **REJECTED: "The sorting mechanism itself remains unaltered, which is acceptable"** — reason: This framing would imply that non-structural compensation is sufficient to reach equity goals. However, the scenario's own equity_impact section states "it does not address the root cause (early selection at ages 10-12), so residual stratification persists; the ceiling on equity gain is set by the untested offsetting assumption." The chosen framing preserves the limits on equity gain.

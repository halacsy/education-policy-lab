# Critique: coherence_checker

## S1.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism claims maintainer-uniform admission rules prevent selective intake from migrating to church/foundation schools, but the scenario simultaneously treats maintainer exemption/migration as severe and only contingently mitigated in political risks and implementation steps. The evidence tag is too strong as `[evidence: contested]` for a prevention claim: the overview says maintainer fragmentation is strongly evidenced and that non-state maintainers can absorb capacity unless bound by law; it does not support that uniform rules are politically or legally achievable enough to “prevent” migration.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast this mechanism as conditional: “If legally binding maintainer-uniform rules are enacted and enforced, migration risk may be reduced; absent that, selective intake likely shifts to non-state maintainers.”

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: Step 1 says the government and legislature will enact a multi-party pact / legislative lock securing sustained political capital against reversal, but the political_risks and uncertainties admit durability against electoral turnover is low-confidence. This makes the implementation design internally circular: it solves the core political-risk problem by assuming the hardest political condition has already been secured.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the pact as a prerequisite action with a tested contingency: “seek cross-party/legal lock; if not achieved by year 1, limit reform to pilots or slower caps and classify reversal risk as unresolved.”

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: “No mean-performance loss where late-tracking evidence holds” is tagged `[evidence: moderate]`, but S3’s own mechanism says the load-bearing Hungarian claim is contested: if peer effects are symmetric, S3 redistributes learning rather than creating it. Cross-national late-tracking evidence supports a possibility under bundled conditions, not an expected Hungarian benefit under current teacher-shortage and flight risks.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Change to “Mean performance loss is not observed in some late-tracking systems, but Hungary-specific mean effects are uncertain and depend on teacher capacity, peer-effect asymmetry, and exit control.”

## S4.uncertainties
Objection: The uncertainty “Do TEIP/curriculum mechanisms transfer given Hungarian teacher shortages?” is marked `[confidence: high]`, but the surrounding scenario repeatedly says transferability is assumed, budget-elastic, and only moderately evidenced from Portugal. This confidence level contradicts the evidence_status and mechanism, which describe the transfer claim as context-dependent and not Hungary-verified.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Lower confidence to low or medium and specify what is currently supported: Portugal shows non-structural improvement under its own institutional conditions, not reliable transfer under Hungarian teacher shortages.

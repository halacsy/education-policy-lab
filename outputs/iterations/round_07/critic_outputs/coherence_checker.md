# Critique: coherence_checker

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that state caps are "not simply offset by non-state maintainers expanding selective intake" is internally contradicted by the same scenario's mechanism, which says non-state expansion is strongly evidenced and "directly undercuts" the aggregate effect. The assumptions field treats a known structural escape channel as a weak assumption rather than revising the scenario around it.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast the scenario as a state-sector-only phase-down unless it includes binding non-state quota controls; otherwise remove aggregate equity claims that depend on non-state offset not occurring.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The claim that residual selective places becoming more SES-skewed is "mechanical and near-certain" is too strong for the evidence tag. Current KSH/OH skew supports existing SES imbalance, but it does not by itself prove that fewer seats necessarily worsen skew; applicant composition, quota design, geography, and non-state substitution could change the direction or magnitude.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Downgrade to "plausible risk" and specify the missing evidence needed: projected applicant pools and SES composition under reduced state quotas.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The expected benefit that structural abolition alleviates rural school-network collapse conflicts with the scenario's own mechanism and equity_impact, which say sorting can migrate through school choice and non-state enrollment. If high-SES or high-achieving pupils can still exit local schools, abolition of formal 6-/8-year entry does not by itself stop the drain into urban or non-state hubs.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Make this benefit conditional on enforceable choice-architecture and maintainer-transparency safeguards, or move it to uncertainties rather than expected benefits.

## S4.goal
Objection: The goal claims the package can "neutralize" socioeconomic inequities while the mechanism, evidence_status, assumptions, and equity_impact all say offsetting Hungary's age-10/12 sorting is weak, unproven, and may only mask downstream effects. This is a direct overclaim relative to the scenario's own evidence.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace "neutralize" with "mitigate partially" and state that the structure remains inequity-producing unless compensation is shown in a Hungarian pilot to offset early-selection effects.

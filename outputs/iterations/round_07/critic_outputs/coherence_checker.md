# Critique: coherence_checker

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes a workable SES indicator can be built at application time while also stating in evidence_status, equity_impact, and uncertainties that no such non-gameable application-time indicator exists. This is not merely uncertain; it undermines the quota mechanism that carries the scenario's main equity benefit.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast the scenario as conditional on prior SES-proxy piloting, or replace SES quotas with a currently observable proxy such as settlement/school disadvantage and downgrade the expected equity benefit accordingly.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The field says residual selective places become “even more SES-skewed” as a “mechanical and near-certain” effect of fewer seats and the same applicant pool, but that is too strong. Seat reduction mechanically reduces the number selected early; increased SES skew depends on applicant behavior, selection criteria, and whether caps change who applies. The cited evidence supports a risk, not a near-certain mechanical result.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Rephrase as a likely risk under unchanged admissions and applicant behavior, and mark the evidence as weak-to-moderate rather than mechanical.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: “No demonstrated loss in mean achievement from delayed selection” is tagged strong using Hanushek & Woessmann 2006, but the scenario elsewhere admits the Hungarian effect size is unknown and that teacher shortage could degrade academic quality. Cross-country evidence may support no average loss in some delayed-tracking systems; it does not strongly establish that Hungary can abolish 6/8-year entry without mean-achievement loss under current teacher-supply constraints.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to moderate or contested and specify that no mean-achievement loss is conditional on adequate implementation capacity, especially teacher supply and within-school differentiation.

## S4.goal
Objection: The goal claims the package can “neutralise” the equity effects of early selection, but the mechanism, evidence_status, assumptions, equity_impact, and uncertainties all say offsetting Hungary’s age-10/12 sorting is weak or unproven. The scenario supports mitigation, not neutralisation.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace “neutralise” with “partially mitigate” and align expected_benefits with disadvantaged-school attainment gains rather than full compensation for early tracking.

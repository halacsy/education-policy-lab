# Critique: coherence_checker

## S2.goal
Objection: The goal says the phase-down will lower the share of the cohort exposed to early sorting, but the scenario's own assumptions, equity_impact, political_risks, and uncertainties all state that church/foundation maintainers may absorb displaced demand. If non-state expansion is plausible and unregulated, the goal's core claim is unsupported: the policy may only reduce state-sector sorting, not cohort-level early sorting.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Rewrite the goal to say it reduces early sorting only in state-maintained schools unless paired with enforceable caps across all maintainer types, or add cross-maintainer regulation as a core design feature.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that sorting does not simply migrate to school choice or non-state maintainers is directly contradicted by S3.equity_impact, S3.political_risks, and S3.uncertainties, all of which identify migration of sorting as the largest unresolved risk. This is not merely uncertain; the scenario simultaneously assumes away and foregrounds the same mechanism.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the assumption with a conditional design requirement: abolition only reduces aggregate sorting if accompanied by enforceable controls on school-choice/catchment stratification and non-state maintainer substitution.

## S4.goal
Objection: The goal claims the package will “neutralize” socio-economic inequities while retaining the 6/8-year system unchanged, but S4.evidence_status says offsetting Hungary’s age-10/12 selection gradient has no direct precedent, and S4.equity_impact says gains are only partial and downstream. The word “neutralize” is therefore unsupported by the scenario’s own evidence tags.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change the goal from “neutralize” to “mitigate downstream effects of” socio-economic inequities, and explicitly state that the sorting mechanism itself remains intact.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The steps put national rollout in years 4-5, but the assumptions and uncertainties say no validated Hungarian less-coachable assessment exists and no workable gaming-resistant SES proxy exists. The timeline treats two unresolved feasibility questions as routine development tasks, contradicting the low-confidence uncertainty tags.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add a precondition phase before legislative rollout: independent feasibility studies for SES proxy construction and assessment validity, with national rollout contingent on successful pilots rather than scheduled automatically for year 4-5.

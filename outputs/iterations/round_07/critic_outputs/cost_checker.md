# Critique: cost_checker
## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The one-off cost range “low tens of HUF billion” for designing and validating a central assessment instrument is unsupported by the evidence presented. The scenario tags it [evidence: weak] but still gives a specific order-of-magnitude range without tying it to any benchmark, unit cost, cohort size, IT/proctoring requirement, or pilot-to-national scaling model. The source base supports that admission redesign has administrative costs, not that those costs land in the low tens of billions.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the point range with a decomposed estimate: test development/validation, administration infrastructure, staff training, appeals/audit systems, and rollout costs; otherwise state “range unavailable” rather than inventing “low tens of HUF billion.”

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The cost categories confuse freed capacity with usable fiscal space. “Reinvestment into general-school advanced programmes” is listed as recurring and scaling with phase-down pace, but the scenario does not price whether savings from reduced selective places actually materialise. The synthesis says education_finance found per-pupil costs are flat across tracks and centralised funding yields no structural savings, so freed places may be physical/organizational capacity rather than budgetary savings. The current field implies a reinvestment source that the evidence does not support.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Separate “capacity reallocation” from “cashable savings,” and add an explicit line that steady-state fiscal savings are uncertain or likely minimal unless staffing/buildings are consolidated.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The field lists major one-off transition costs but omits the likely recurring cost of maintaining smaller-group differentiation, support staff, counselling, remedial provision, and teacher retention incentives after rollout. “Diagnostic / remedial support systems” is included, but only generically; the scenario treats the largest fiscal issue as transition, while its own mechanism says equity gains depend on concurrent teacher-quality and diagnostic-support upgrades. That is a steady-state operating burden, not just an implementation expense.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add recurring cost lines for additional support staff, reduced class/group sizes where needed, continuing professional development, and shortage-region retention incentives, with fiscal sensitivity under teacher-supply constraints.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The −30% budget sensitivity is directionally useful but under-specified: it asserts that TEIP-style targeting loses the “intensity threshold Portugal relied on” without naming the threshold or showing that Portugal’s evidence identifies a transferable minimum spending intensity. The cited evidence supports that targeted funding matters, not a concrete Hungarian break point at −30%.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Recast the −30% case as a sensitivity scenario rather than a threshold claim, or define the threshold using per-school/per-pupil intervention intensity benchmarks from the Portugal evidence before claiming mechanism failure.

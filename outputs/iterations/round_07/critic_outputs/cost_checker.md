# Critique: cost_checker
## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The "capacity expansion in general-school upper grades to absorb retained high-achievers" cost is under-specified and likely misframed. If the same cohort remains in the common track rather than moving to selective schools, the relevant cost is not just generic capacity expansion; it includes teacher allocation, classroom space, subject-specialist coverage, and possible program differentiation in the receiving schools. The field gives a single "medium" label with weak evidence and no range, even though demographic contraction may make physical capacity low-cost in some districts and teacher/specialist capacity high-cost in others.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split this line into physical capacity, staffing/specialist coverage, and within-school differentiation costs, each with district-dependent ranges and a note that demographic contraction may reduce facility costs while not solving teacher constraints.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The field correctly labels teacher retraining, facility/curriculum redesign, and diagnostic/remedial systems as high, but it blurs transition and steady-state costs. Teacher retraining and facility/curriculum redesign are mostly front-loaded transition costs; diagnostic and remedial support systems are recurring operating costs. Treating all three simply as "high" hides fiscal sustainability risk and makes S3 hard to compare with S4, whose recurring targeted funding is explicitly vulnerable to consolidation.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast S3 costs into transition costs versus recurring annual costs, and add fiscal sensitivity for the recurring diagnostic/remedial support line.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The TEIP-style targeted funding line says "high; no numeric range available" but still makes a strong fiscal vulnerability claim that it is "typically the first cut in fiscal consolidation." The evidence tag is weak expert estimates, so the source does not support the strength of "typically"; it supports at most a fiscal-risk warning. This overstates the evidence while failing to give even an indicative range or threshold for what level of funding would be needed to offset early selection.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the claim to "high fiscal exposure under consolidation, magnitude uncertain" unless budget history evidence is added, and include an indicative annual per-pupil or program-budget range needed for TEIP-style compensation.

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: SES-indicator construction and audit is marked "cost not estimable" because no administrative register exists, but that collapses feasibility risk into cost accounting. The scenario should still distinguish one-time design/legal/data-integration costs from recurring verification, appeals, audit, and anti-gaming enforcement costs. Without those categories, the field understates the administrative burden of running quotas based on a proxy indicator.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Replace "cost not estimable" with separate cost categories for indicator design, data-linkage/legal compliance, annual verification, appeals handling, and anti-gaming audits, while noting that feasibility remains unresolved.

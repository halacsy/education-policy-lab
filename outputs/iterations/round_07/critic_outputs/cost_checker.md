# Critique: cost_checker

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The cost list omits enforcement and compliance costs for non-state maintainers, even though S1's mechanism and political_risks identify church/foundation opt-out as a central constraint. The current categories price assessment redesign, SES infrastructure, and admissions administration, but not monitoring, audit, appeals, legal compliance guidance, or sanctions needed to make SES-aware rules matter beyond state-maintained schools. The evidence tag on governance fragmentation supports that this is not a marginal risk; it is a core implementation cost.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a recurring "maintainer compliance, audit, appeals, and enforcement" category, with a medium-to-high range and explicit dependence on whether rules apply only to state schools or also to church/foundation maintainers.

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: Staff reallocation is understated as "medium, one-off per phase" despite the scenario describing a decade-long quota reduction and parallel regimes. A phasedown would repeatedly change class formation, subject allocations, teacher workloads, and possibly school viability across multiple cohorts; treating this as discrete one-off costs confuses recurring transition management with isolated restructuring events.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reclassify staff reallocation and workforce planning as medium-to-high recurring transition costs across years 2-10, including redeployment, retraining, severance/attrition management, timetable redesign, and local bargaining.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The categories miss the fiscal exposure created by sorting migration to non-state schools, which S3 itself identifies as the largest equity risk. If middle-class or church/foundation-school flight occurs, public comprehensive schools may face revenue, staffing, and per-pupil cost instability while also needing added support for concentrated disadvantage. The current cost section prices retraining, facilities, and legal transition, but not the downstream compensatory funding required if disadvantage reconcentrates.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a "sorting-migration mitigation and compensatory funding" category, medium-to-high recurring, covering targeted grants, enrollment stabilization, transport/access measures, and transparency/compliance systems for non-state maintainers.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The TEIP-style funding line is labeled "high" but gives no range, unit base, or fiscal trigger despite the scenario's own claim that long-run budget vulnerability is central. For a package whose mechanism depends on sustained targeted funding, "high; no numeric range" is not enough: it prevents comparison with S2/S3 transition costs and masks whether this is cheaper only in steady-state rhetoric or actually affordable over multiple budget cycles.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the single "high" label with an explicit range framework: per-school or per-disadvantaged-pupil annual funding, expected number of eligible schools, 4-year and 10-year totals, and a sensitivity case for fiscal consolidation cuts.

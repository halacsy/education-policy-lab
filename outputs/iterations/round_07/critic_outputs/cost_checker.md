# Critique: cost_checker

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The capacity-expansion cost is understated as merely "medium" despite the scenario spanning a decade and shifting pupils from selective tracks into general-school upper grades. The evidence says transition costs dominate structural reform, but the field gives no range for whether retained pupils require new classrooms, staffing, transport, or consolidation spending. This collapses a multi-year transition cost into a generic category.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split capacity costs into facilities, staffing, transport/network adjustment, and administration, and mark the total as medium-to-high or high where local capacity is insufficient.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The field correctly labels major components as high, but it misses the fiscal sensitivity of making early diagnostic and special-education support systems recurring. The evidence from Finnish-style bundling supports that these supports are necessary for equity gains, not that Hungary can sustain them under teacher shortage and budget pressure. The cost category therefore states the package but not the fiscal durability condition.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a recurring fiscal-sustainability category for diagnostic, remedial, and special-education staffing, with explicit sensitivity to teacher shortages and multi-year budget commitments.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: Transition and steady-state costs are blurred. "Facility and curriculum realignment" is tagged as high and front-loaded, while teacher retraining is high, but the field does not distinguish one-off transition costs from permanent higher operating costs if comprehensive lower-secondary requires smaller groups, support staff, or differentiated-instruction capacity. The education-finance evidence specifically warns that transition costs dominate, so the scenario should not leave steady-state obligations implicit.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Separate front-loaded transition costs from recurring operating costs, including ongoing teacher-support, specialist, and evaluation costs after national scale-up.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The TEIP-style targeted funding category is too vague for the scenario's core mechanism. It says "high" with no numeric range, duration, per-school basis, or protected-funding mechanism, even though the scenario itself identifies fiscal consolidation as the dominant durability risk. Without a funding range, the expected compensation claim is fiscally unsupported.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add an indicative per-pupil or per-school funding range, specify minimum duration, and state whether funding is statutory/ring-fenced or annual discretionary spending.

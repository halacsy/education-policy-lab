# Critique: assumption_checker

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim "No demonstrated loss in mean achievement from delayed selection" is tagged strong with Hanushek & Woessmann 2006, but that source supports a cross-country association about early tracking and inequality/mean performance, not a Hungary-specific guarantee that abolishing 6/8-year entry would produce no mean-achievement loss under current Hungarian teacher shortages and maintainer fragmentation. The scenario's own political_risks field says teacher shortage could degrade academic quality, directly weakening this benefit.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to moderate or contested, and restate as: "International evidence does not show a systematic mean-achievement penalty from later tracking, but Hungary-specific implementation constraints could still reduce mean achievement during transition."

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: "Alignment with demographic contraction, reducing per-pupil inefficiency of parallel selective programs" is tagged moderate using KSH demographic yearbooks and OECD school network studies, but those sources support demographic contraction and network pressure, not the specific claim that reducing 6/8-year selective places will lower per-pupil inefficiency. The expert record explicitly says structural reform costs are front-loaded and may not yield steady-state operational savings, so this benefit is unsupported as written.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Recast as an uncertainty or planning rationale: "Demographic contraction creates pressure to rationalize parallel programs, but net efficiency gains from selective-seat phase-down are not demonstrated and require district-level cost modeling."

## S4.goal
Objection: The goal says the package would "neutralise" the equity effects of early selection, but the scenario's own mechanism, evidence_status, assumptions, and equity_impact all say offsetting Hungary's age-10/12 sorting is weak, unverifiable, and at best partial. This is not just optimistic wording; it contradicts the evidence record that Portugal did not face selection as early as Hungary and did not demonstrate full compensation for early tracking.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace "neutralise its equity effects" with "partially mitigate downstream harms while leaving the early-selection mechanism intact."

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The scenario lists "A workable SES indicator can be built at application time" as an assumption even though the same line says feasibility_checker contradicts it because Hungary has no usable student-level SES register and proxies would be gameable. That is not merely uncertain; it is currently unsupported and contradicted by the expert record, while several core mechanisms and benefits depend on it.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reframe the scenario as conditional on first creating and validating a non-gameable SES proxy; until then, downgrade quota-related mechanisms and benefits from moderate to weak/unsupported.

# Critique: assumption_checker

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes “SES classification / coaching-resistance can be audited with ≥85% accuracy,” but the expert record only supports a proposed gate for SES-classification accuracy. It does not support auditing “coaching-resistance” at that threshold, and conflating the two makes the rollout condition unverifiable.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split the assumption into “SES classification can be audited at ≥85% accuracy” and “coaching-resistance of the admission instrument can be independently validated,” with the latter marked weak/unsupported unless a specific validation method is supplied.

## S1.mechanism
Objection: The claim that maintainer-uniform admission rules “prevent selective intake from migrating” is stronger than the evidence supports. The expert record strongly supports maintainer fragmentation and the risk that non-state maintainers can absorb selective demand unless legally bound; it does not show that a uniform-rule mandate would be enforceable or sufficient to prevent migration.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast this as a necessary but unproven condition: “Binding, enforceable rules covering non-state maintainers are required to reduce migration risk, but effectiveness is unverified.”

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The first step assumes a “multi-party pact / legislative lock” can secure sustained political capital and protect the cap trajectory, but the expert record explicitly cites Poland’s 2016–2019 reversal as evidence that even evidence-backed structural reform can be undone after electoral turnover. The step treats the lock as an available instrument without showing why it would bind future governments in Hungary.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the step with a more modest precondition: “Design legal and fiscal entrenchment options and assess their durability against electoral reversal,” then mark durability as weak/contested.

## S4.uncertainties
Objection: The uncertainty “Do TEIP/curriculum mechanisms transfer given Hungarian teacher shortages? [confidence: high]” contradicts the scenario’s own evidence status. The scenario says transfer to Hungary is an assumption and that the offset claim is unverifiable ex ante; the expert record supports Portugal’s trajectory only moderately and does not establish transfer under Hungary’s teacher shortages.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Change confidence to low or medium, and specify that Portuguese evidence supports targeted-school improvement in Portugal, not reliable transfer to Hungary under current workforce constraints.

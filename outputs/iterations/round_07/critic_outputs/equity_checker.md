# Critique: equity_checker
## S1.equity_impact
Objection: The field says gains accrue to “high-ability low-SES pupils who already apply,” but the cited moderate mechanical evidence only supports increased representation among whichever applicants are assigned quota eligibility; it does not support identifying them as “high-ability.” If the admission rule reserves seats using SES or settlement proxies, the equity effect depends on proxy validity and applicant pool composition, not demonstrated high ability. This matters because the equity-positive claim quietly narrows beneficiaries to a meritorious subgroup without evidence.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: State that gains accrue to quota-eligible low-SES or underrepresented-settlement applicants who apply, conditional on a valid proxy, and remove “high-ability” unless supported by assessment or outcome evidence.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The claim that residual selective places become “even more SES-skewed” is overstated as “near-certain.” Fewer seats with the same applicant pool can increase competition, but the direction of SES skew depends on how caps are allocated across schools, settlements, and maintainers. If caps first remove the most elite urban seats, the residual pool could change differently. The evidence tag supports a plausible risk, not a mechanical equity result.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast this as a high-risk but contingent effect, specifying that worsening SES skew is expected if caps are applied proportionally and admissions rules remain unchanged; add that geographic and maintainer allocation could alter the distribution.

## S3.equity_impact
Objection: The field says “largest expected aggregate equity gain of the four scenarios” with moderate evidence, but it does not identify who is reached beyond the aggregate. It leaves out a central equity-negative pathway: low-SES pupils in schools that lose middle-class peers or teachers may face worse peer composition and lower instructional quality during transition, especially under the strong teacher-shortage constraint named elsewhere. The exit risk is noted, but the harmed group is not concretely specified.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Name the likely beneficiaries and losers explicitly: pupils currently excluded from selective tracks may gain from delayed sorting, while disadvantaged pupils in schools experiencing middle-class exit or teacher dilution may see concentrated disadvantage unless intake and staffing safeguards are binding.

## S4.equity_impact
Objection: The field understates possible equity-negative effects by saying compensation “only mitigated” the root cause. If targeted funding raises disadvantaged-school attainment while selective tracks remain high-SES, the package may still legitimize a two-tier structure and make unequal access politically more durable. The political_risks field names this entrenchment risk, but equity_impact does not evaluate who is left permanently outside selective peer networks and high-status pathways.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add that the main beneficiaries are pupils in funded disadvantaged general schools, while low-SES pupils remain largely excluded from selective-track social capital and credentials; flag entrenchment of early selection as an equity-negative risk, not only a political risk.

# Critique: evidence_checker

## S1.political_risks
Objection: The claim that pedagogus unions treat assessment redesign as “performance-audit creep” is tagged strong but cites “round 06 episodic memory,” which is not in the curated source registry. The registry has strong evidence for teacher shortage, legal anchoring, governance, and tracking inequality, but no registry-backed fact about union interpretation of assessment redesign.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to weak and mark as non-registry expert assertion, or replace with a registry-backed teacher-shortage/legal/governance risk.

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: “Alignment with demographic contraction, reducing per-pupil inefficiency of parallel selective programs” is tagged moderate, but the registry’s demography source only supports cohort shrinkage and the need for consolidation decisions. It does not support the specific claim that parallel selective programs create reducible per-pupil inefficiency; the cited “OECD school network studies” are not in the registry.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Split the claim: keep demographic contraction as strong registry-backed context, but tag the per-pupil inefficiency claim weak/expert estimate unless a registry-backed fiscal source is added.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: “No demonstrated loss in mean achievement from delayed selection” is tagged strong and attributed to Hanushek & Woessmann 2006, but the registry’s tracking_inequality entry supports that earlier tracking increases inequality of outcomes, not that delayed selection has no mean-achievement loss. The tag is stronger than the registered support warrants.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast as “delayed tracking is associated with lower inequality” with strong evidence, or downgrade the mean-achievement claim to weak/model knowledge unless a registry source explicitly supports it.

## S3.equity_impact
Objection: The “middle-class and church/foundation exit” risk is tagged moderate and cites Pekkarinen et al., finnish_reform, and school_network_planning, but the registry does not include finnish_reform or school_network_planning as source facts. Registry governance supports maintainer plurality, and school_choice supports sorting under free choice, but neither directly supports a moderate claim that structural reform will cause exit to church/foundation or private schools.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Tag the exit-risk claim weak-to-moderate, grounding it specifically in registry governance plus school_choice, and state that the reform-induced exit mechanism is an inference rather than directly evidenced.

# Critique: evidence_checker

## S1.mechanism
Objection: The claim that “centrally standardised admission assessment replaces teacher-level gatekeeping” is not supported at [evidence: moderate]. The registry-backed `gimn_share` source says 6/8-year gimnazium entry is already selected largely via central written exams; it does not establish teacher-level gatekeeping as the main current problem or show that more centralisation would reduce SES skew.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Regrade this mechanism to weak/inferential, or replace it with a registry-supported claim about early selective tracks and school choice producing SES sorting.

## S2.mechanism
Objection: The claim that a felmenő rendszer guarantee “lowers legal and parental resistance” is tagged [evidence: strong], but the registry does not support that strength. `legal` strongly supports that phase-out requires legislative and administrative action; `parent_attachment` moderately supports parental resistance to curtailment. Neither source strongly supports the mitigation effect of grandfathering.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Regrade to moderate or weak and state that grandfathering is a plausible legal/political mitigation, not a strongly evidenced effect.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The “0.15–0.25 SD equity gain range in comparable systems” is tagged [evidence: moderate], but that numeric range is not in the curated registry. The registry supports a general inequality effect from earlier tracking (`tracking_inequality` strong) and Poland/Finland reform associations (`poland_reform`, `finland_comprehensive` moderate), not this specific quantified benefit range for S3.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Remove the numeric range or mark it as non-registry/model-knowledge evidence; keep the benefit as “reduced SES inequality in comparable late-tracking systems” with moderate/strong support depending on source.

## S4.uncertainties
Objection: The uncertainty “Do TEIP/curriculum mechanisms transfer given Hungarian teacher shortages?” is marked [confidence: high], but the scenario’s own evidence base only supports Portugal’s improvement as moderate and teacher shortage in Hungary as strong. The registry does not support high confidence in transferability; if anything, the teacher-shortage source weakens the transfer claim.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Change confidence to low or medium and specify that Portugal supports the mechanism only moderately, while Hungarian teacher shortages are a major transfer risk.

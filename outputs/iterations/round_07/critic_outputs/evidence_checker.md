# Critique: evidence_checker

## S1.mechanism
Objection: The documented-outreach lever is tagged moderate, but the cited support (“stakeholder discourse analysis, Round 6”) is not in the curated source registry and is not flagged as model knowledge. The registry contains no source showing that documented outreach raises low-SES or Roma application rates; at most, the scenario can state this as an unverified implementation hypothesis.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to weak and rewrite as: “Documented outreach may raise applications, but no registry-backed evidence demonstrates uptake effects in this setting.”

## S2.political_risks
Objection: The claim that non-state maintainers “expand selective intake to capture displaced demand” is tagged strong, but the registry’s governance source only supports maintainer plurality and uneven control across state, church, and foundation schools. It does not establish expansion behavior, capacity, or a demand-capture response. The tag is stronger than the evidence warrants.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to moderate or weak and state: “Non-state maintainers could undermine uniform caps because maintainer plurality limits state control; whether they would expand intake is unproven.”

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The field asserts that residual selective places becoming “even more SES-skewed” is “mechanical and near-certain,” but no registry source supports that tightening seat supply with the same applicant pool necessarily increases SES skew. The registry supports current SES sorting and selective-share size, not this specific distributional response.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Recast as an uncertainty: “Residual places may become more SES-skewed if competition intensifies and admission rules remain unchanged, but this needs applicant-level simulation or admissions data.”

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The claim that equity gains “only materialise if bundled with teacher-quality and early-support investment” is tagged moderate from Finnish evidence, but the registry’s Finland item only supports an association between comprehensive schooling and equity/reduced dependence on background. It does not prove that bundling is a necessary condition for Hungary or that unbundled reform would fail.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Change “only materialise” to “are more plausible if bundled” and keep the evidence tag moderate only for the Finnish association, not for necessity.

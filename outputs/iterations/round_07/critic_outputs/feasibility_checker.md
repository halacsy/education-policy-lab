# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The year 1-3 timeline for designing and piloting a less-coachable assessment with psychometric validation is unsupported. The scenario itself says no such instrument has been demonstrated, so the step compresses invention, validation, coaching-sensitivity testing, regulatory adoption, and school-facing implementation into an optimistic administrative schedule without evidence that Oktatási Hivatal has the bandwidth or test-development capacity to deliver it.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split the step into a prerequisite feasibility study and small-sample psychometric trial before any regulatory change; mark quota/reweighting implementation as contingent on validated assessment and usable SES indicator.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The Ministry step to “negotiate or legislate parallel intake limits for non-state maintainers” in years 1-3 understates the legal and administrative lead time. The evidence tag says maintainer plurality is strong and non-state offset risk is dominant; the source supports that non-state maintainers can absorb selective tracks and bypass consolidation, not that a three-year negotiation/legislation window can contain them.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a separate legal feasibility phase before the cap schedule, and state that the state-school phase-down cannot deliver its equity goal unless binding non-state limits are enacted and enforceable.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The national scale-up in years 5-10 is not feasible under the scenario’s own teacher-supply evidence. The evidence status calls teacher supply a strong binding constraint, but the implementation sequence treats teacher-supply stabilisation as a milestone rather than a capacity problem with no demonstrated recruitment, retention, or training pipeline.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the national scale-up step with staged expansion only after quantified teacher workforce thresholds are met, including district-level staffing ratios, retraining completion, and retention funding.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: The steps omit the hardest feasibility condition: staffing disadvantaged schools for the compensatory package. The political_risks field says teacher shortage blocks staffing exactly in the target schools with strong evidence, but implementation assigns funding and standards rollout without a concrete teacher recruitment, retention, or workload-capacity plan.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a required implementation step for targeted teacher staffing incentives, workload relief, and retention monitoring before TEIP-style funding expansion; make rollout contingent on verified staffing capacity in eligible schools.

# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The sequence assumes the Ministry/KSH can construct and audit an application-time SES indicator in years 1–2 even though the scenario itself says no usable administrative student-level SES register exists and any proxy would be gameable. That is not a tight timeline problem; it is a missing legal-administrative infrastructure problem. The “moderate” quota evidence only supports mechanical seat reallocation once a valid eligibility variable exists, not the feasibility of creating that variable.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast SES-based quotas as conditional on a prior 2–3 year legal-data pilot, or replace SES quotas with less data-intensive settlement/school-level indicators and explicitly downgrade expected precision.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The assessment redesign timeline of years 1–3 is not credible for a high-stakes national admissions instrument that is both psychometrically valid and demonstrably less coachable. The cited evidence does not support coaching resistance; it only supports that current selective-track advantages are largely selection effects. Administrative capacity for piloting, validation, appeals handling, and national rollout is understated.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Extend the assessment-development timeline, add staged low-stakes pilots before use in admissions, and mark implementation as infeasible until coaching-resistance evidence exists.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The scenario says universal diagnostic/remedial support and teacher retraining can be built in years 1–5, but the evidence base identifies teacher shortage as a strong binding constraint. The Finnish-style support system depends on trained staff capacity that Hungary does not currently have, so the implementation plan names the right components without showing a feasible staffing path.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a precondition phase for teacher-supply stabilisation, specialist recruitment, and district-level staffing targets before national abolition; otherwise downgrade the scenario’s feasibility.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: Routing TEIP-style targeted funding from year 1 onward assumes administrative and fiscal capacity that the scenario’s own political_risks undermine. The budget-vulnerability evidence is weak on magnitude, but it does support a durability risk: recurring targeted grants are exposed to annual fiscal cuts unless legally earmarked. The implementation plan lacks any mechanism to protect the funding stream.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add multi-year statutory earmarking, transparent allocation formulas, and minimum-funding guarantees before treating TEIP-style compensation as implementable at scale.

# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The year 1-4 nationwide differentiated-instruction retraining schedule is not feasible under the scenario's own evidence base. The assumptions and uncertainties explicitly state that Hungary lacks Finland-equivalent teacher quality/support infrastructure and faces existing teacher shortages, especially in shortage subjects. The listed evidence supports the need for retraining, not the administrative or trainer capacity to deliver it nationally in four years while also piloting conversion and preparing rollout.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a prerequisite national teacher-capacity audit before retraining starts, split retraining into staged regional cohorts, and make nationwide rollout conditional on verified trainer supply, substitute coverage, and completion rates in shortage subjects.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The timeline compresses legal amendment, equity/cost audit, pilot conversion, teacher retraining, choice-architecture safeguards, and nationwide rollout into overlapping years without identifying the administrative body with capacity to coordinate them. The evidence tagged strong/moderate supports that legal transition and grandfathering are required, but it does not support the claim that these parallel workstreams can be managed within years 1-8, especially under fragmented maintainer governance.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Separate the reform into legally gated phases: audit and legal design, pilot with maintainer agreements, capacity buildout, then rollout. Add explicit Go/No-Go criteria for legal readiness, maintainer participation, staffing, and pilot outcomes before year 5 rollout.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: The TEIP-style package assumes the Ministry of Finance can secure and renew ring-fenced funding at year 1, year 4, and year 7, but the scenario's own political_risks field says targeted funding is vulnerable to fiscal consolidation and cites no Hungary-specific budget-cut history. That evidence tag is weak: it supports a risk hypothesis, not a feasible long-term budget mechanism.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Replace the simple ring-fencing step with a concrete fiscal instrument: statutory multi-year appropriation, automatic indexation, protected disadvantaged-school grant line, and an annual parliamentary reporting requirement. Mark feasibility as unproven until Hungarian budget-continuity evidence is supplied.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The SES-proxy indicator is scheduled for development and pilot in years 1-2, while legislation permitting SES-aware quotas follows in years 2-3 and rollout starts by years 4-5. This is unsupported by the scenario's own evidence_status: no usable administrative SES register is confirmed to exist, and the indicator must be validated and made gaming-resistant before it can carry admissions consequences.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Make SES-indicator development a precondition, not a parallel step. Add a 2-3 year validation pilot with appeal procedures, data-protection review, gaming audit, and only then legislative authorization for quota use.

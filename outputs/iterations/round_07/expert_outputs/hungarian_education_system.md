# Expert analysis: hungarian_education_system

## Findings (evidence)
- Roughly 8-12% of a cohort enters 6- or 8-year gimnazium tracks, selected at age 10 or 12 largely via the central written admission examination; entrants are strongly skewed toward high-SES, urban families. [evidence: strong — KSH / Oktatási Hivatal admission statistics]
- Free school choice plus early selective tracks produces strong between-school sorting already at the primary stage in cities; abolition of tracks alone would not eliminate sorting through catchment and choice. [evidence: moderate — Kertesi & Kezdi 2013; Berenyi-Berkovits-Erőss school choice studies]
- Since 2013 most state schools are run by state school district centres (tankerulet); churches and foundations maintain a growing share of schools, complicating any uniform structural mandate. [evidence: strong — Public education act amendments; maintainer statistics]

## Interpretation
Early selective tracks are one layer of a broader sorting system (school choice, catchments, maintainer diversity); treating the gimnazium forms in isolation overstates what their abolition would achieve.

## Assumptions
- Admission statistics remain representative of current cohorts. [assumption]

## Position
Reform should target the whole sorting system, not only the 6/8-year forms.

## Uncertainties
- How much sorting would migrate to church/private maintainers after a state-track phase-out. (confidence: low; evidence that would reduce it: maintainer-level admission data linked to SES.)

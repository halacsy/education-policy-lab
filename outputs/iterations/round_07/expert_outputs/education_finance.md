# Expert analysis: education_finance

## Findings (evidence)
- Per-pupil funding differences between track types are modest; the dominant costs of structural reform are transition costs (retraining, building use, administration), not steady-state costs. [evidence: weak — Budget chapters; expert estimates]
- Hungarian public education operational funding is highly centralized under the Klebelsberg Centre (KK), meaning local municipal budget reallocations do not drive structural savings. [evidence: strong — Model knowledge]
- Teacher wage scales in Hungary are determined by national grid systems based on qualification and experience, meaning shifting teachers between gimnazium and primary tracks does not alter base wage bills. [evidence: strong — Model knowledge]

## Interpretation
From a financial perspective, consolidating the 6- and 8-year gimnaziums into a standard 4-year structure does not yield significant steady-state savings, because per-pupil operational costs are relatively flat across secondary tracks. Instead, the fiscal impact is heavily front-loaded in transition costs. Specifically, retraining specialized secondary teachers to teach primary-level curriculum and modifying physical school infrastructure to accommodate shifted cohorts will dominate the initial budget cycles. 

## Assumptions
- The physical infrastructure of existing gimnaziums cannot be easily sold or repurposed without incurring substantial municipal administrative costs. [assumption]
- National teacher pay scales will remain uniform across secondary and primary tracks during any transition period. [assumption]

## Position
Hungary should retain the 6- and 8-year gimnazium system because the transition costs of structural consolidation (teacher retraining and facility re-adaptation) outweigh any marginal steady-state operational savings.

## Uncertainties
- The precise cost per teacher for curriculum retraining programs required to transition specialized gimnazium teachers to lower secondary education. [confidence: medium; would be reduced by: historical cost analysis of the Hungarian pedagogical professional development budgets]
- The capital expenditure required to modify building uses and classroom sizes for shifted student populations. [confidence: low; would be reduced by: a national school infrastructure audit conducted by the Klebelsberg Centre]

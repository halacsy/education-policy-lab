# Argument ledger — round 7

Societal-discourse layer output: this is a STAKEHOLDER STRESS TEST, not a simulation of real reactions. Voices REPRESENT interests and values (they are not experts) to surface objections a real deliberation will have to answer — nothing here should be read as a prediction of what real people or organisations will actually think; positions must be verified with real stakeholders before being treated as fact. Every position carries an epistemic label; factual claims inside arguments are graded by the evidence layer; the policy brief must answer every argument cluster (response obligation).

## Stance matrix

### S1
- **pedagogus_erdekvedo** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: workload protection] — Fairer admissions are acceptable if they add no unpaid administrative burden on schools. Condition: No new reporting duties without paid time.
- **kisgyerekes_szuloi** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: predictability] — Fairer entry is welcome, but opaque quotas or lotteries would destroy trust in admissions. Condition: Transparent, published rules; no lottery.
- **eselyegyenlosegi_civil** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: field experience with partial reforms] — Admission reform is a first step, but alone it re-labels the queue instead of shortening it. Condition: Pair it with enforced desegregation and measurable intake targets.
- **konzervativ_ertekvedo** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: merit principle] — Reform admissions if you must, but merit must remain the basis — SES quotas replace one injustice with another. Condition: Merit-preserving instruments only (blind grading, wider access to preparation).
- **digitalisgazdasagi** — no position [no position] — Admission mechanics within the same structure do not change the skills output we depend on; we defer to others on fairness design.
- **egyhazi_fenntartoi** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: maintainer autonomy] — Transparency in admissions is acceptable; uniform state-imposed admission rules on church schools are not. Condition: Rules negotiated with maintainers, honouring existing agreements.
- **oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: Kockás könyv trajectory] — A fairer gate on the same building is progress, but the documented reform trajectory this archetype draws on aims to need no gate before 14 at all. Condition: Only as an explicit transition step towards the comprehensive model.
- **pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang** — supports [value-modeled: PDSZ Program 2024 + Nagy Erzsébet, pedagogusok.hu, 2026-06-09] — A documented 2026 union position this archetype draws on: if the tracks continue, the admission system must be corrected and segregation actively reduced. Condition: Correction designed with the profession, not imposed on it.
- **kormanyzati_reformrealizmus** — supports [value-modeled: Tisza program (2026) + Lannert Judit, telex.hu, 2026-06-06/19] — Documented public statements this archetype draws on: the admission system will be reviewed — the tracks' advantage is selection, not value-added. Condition: Review conclusions must survive the promised social consultation.
- **fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: 2021 proposal ch. 3.5-3.6] — A fairer gate reduces harm but the documented proposal this archetype draws on removes the gate: selection at 10 or 12 has no demonstrated benefit to justify keeping it in any form. Condition: Acceptable only as a stopgap with a legislated end-date.

### S2
- **pedagogus_erdekvedo** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: job security] — A phase-down is bearable only with retraining guarantees and no forced redundancies. Condition: Enforceable teacher guarantees written into the law.
- **kisgyerekes_szuloi** — opposes [value-modeled: loss aversion / insurance] — Removing places takes an option away from our children before the alternative is proven. Condition: Show visibly improved local general schools first; then reduce places.
- **eselyegyenlosegi_civil** — supports [value-modeled: peer effects on the left-behind] — Every cohort spared early sorting is a cohort whose weakest members keep their stronger peers. Condition: Phase-down speed must not outrun support for receiving schools.
- **konzervativ_ertekvedo** — opposes [value-modeled: parental primacy] — Shrinking the tracks by decree punishes families for the state's failure to lift the general schools. Condition: Would reconsider if general-school quality demonstrably reached the tracks' level.
- **digitalisgazdasagi** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: pipeline continuity] — We can live with fewer selective places if the maths-science talent pipeline is preserved through advanced programmes. Condition: Advanced STEM programmes guaranteed in every region.
- **egyhazi_fenntartoi** — opposes [value-modeled: spillover risk] — A state-track phase-down pushes demand to our schools and regulation will follow it; we did not ask to become the escape valve. Condition: A negotiated all-maintainer framework agreed before any phase-down starts.
- **oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom** — supports [value-modeled: CKP Kockás könyv (2016), ckpinfo.hu/kockaskonyv] — The documented reform proposal this archetype draws on proposes exactly this: transferring the 6/8-year forms into the comprehensive model gradually, with broad social consensus. Condition: Gradualism must have a fixed end-state, not become permanent limbo.
- **pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang** — opposes [value-modeled: PDSZ Program 2024 + Nagy Erzsébet, pedagogusok.hu, 2026-06-09] — The documented union position this archetype draws on argued for keeping the tracks while fixing access — phase-down was a different union's position. Condition: A 'professional reconsideration' with the profession could reopen this.
- **kormanyzati_reformrealizmus** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: program diagnosis + consultation-first method] — The documented program names early selection a problem but commits to no structure; a negotiated, gradual reduction could emerge from consultation. Condition: Only with cross-party durability — avoiding the Polish reversal.
- **fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely** — supports [value-modeled: Egyensúly Intézet: Hogyan legyünk okos nemzet? (2021), 3.5] — A gradual phase-down moves toward the documented proposal this archetype draws on: a uniformly 8-year primary stage with no selective exit ramps. Condition: The end-state (no selection before 14) must be stated, not implied.

### S3
- **pedagogus_erdekvedo** — opposes [value-modeled: capacity realism] — Restructuring the whole system during a teacher shortage overloads the very people who must deliver it. Condition: Settle pay and workload first; then structural reform becomes discussable.
- **kisgyerekes_szuloi** — opposes [value-modeled: quality anxiety] — We fear levelling down: without guarantees, the good tracks disappear and nothing replaces them. Condition: Guaranteed quality standards and talent programmes in every district.
- **eselyegyenlosegi_civil** — supports [value-modeled: segregation as the core harm] — This is the only scenario that removes the sorting machine itself rather than adjusting its dials. Condition: Support systems must arrive with the structure, not after it.
- **konzervativ_ertekvedo** — opposes [value-modeled: talent development] — Abolishing structured tracks sacrifices talent development to an egalitarian experiment run on other people's children. Condition: A guaranteed, well-funded talent system inside the comprehensive school could change this.
- **digitalisgazdasagi** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: baseline skills of the whole cohort] — A comprehensive school can serve the economy if digital and foundational skills are funded seriously across the board. Condition: A costed digital-skills programme inside the reform, not beside it.
- **egyhazi_fenntartoi** — opposes [value-modeled: legal autonomy] — A uniform structural mandate conflicts with maintainer plurality and with bilateral agreements that have constitutional weight. Condition: Constitutional-grade guarantees for maintainer identity within any unified structure.
- **oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom** — supports [value-modeled: CKP Kockás könyv (2016), ckpinfo.hu/kockaskonyv] — The documented programme this archetype draws on calls for unified general education as long as possible — at least 10 years — because the 6/8 forms are 'a tool of elite social separation'. Condition: Bundle with teacher-support systems, as the Finnish lesson requires.
- **pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang** — opposes [value-modeled: PDSZ Program 2024 + Nagy Erzsébet, pedagogusok.hu, 2026-06-09] — The documented programme this archetype draws on asks only for professional reconsideration of the structure; wholesale restructuring amid unresolved pay and workload disputes is not its demand. Condition: Settle the 2023 strike demands first; then structure can be discussed on merits.
- **kormanyzati_reformrealizmus** — opposes [value-modeled: Tisza program (2026) + Lannert Judit, telex.hu, 2026-06-06/19] — Documented statements this archetype draws on explicitly rule out abolishing the 6/8-year gimnázium in the current term; the fight would consume the whole reform agenda. Condition: A broad, measured consultation could reopen this in a later term.
- **fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely** — supports [value-modeled: Egyensúly Intézet: Hogyan legyünk okos nemzet? (2021), 3.5] — The documented, published position this archetype draws on: research shows early selection yields no gains in later outcomes while reproducing poverty (Gurzó-Horn). Condition: Pair with the proposed incentives: reward heterogeneous intake in school evaluation.

### S4
- **pedagogus_erdekvedo** — supports [value-modeled: working conditions] — Resources flowing to general schools directly improve working conditions where most teachers work. Condition: Compensation must reach salaries and staffing, not only buildings.
- **kisgyerekes_szuloi** — supports [value-modeled: local quality] — Better local schools without losing choice is exactly what most parents ask for. Condition: Improvements must be measurable at our own school, not in national averages.
- **eselyegyenlosegi_civil** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: risk of masking] — Compensation helps real children now, but without structural change it risks pacifying instead of fixing. Condition: Hard, published segregation-reduction targets with consequences.
- **konzervativ_ertekvedo** — supports [value-modeled: liberty-preserving reform] — Raise the general schools without restricting anyone's choice — improvement through addition, not prohibition. Condition: Compensation must not become a pretext for later restriction.
- **digitalisgazdasagi** — supports [value-modeled: aggregate human capital] — Lifting the general schools raises the skill floor of the entire future workforce — the highest-return investment on this list. Condition: Employers should be consulted on the skills content of the compensation package.
- **egyhazi_fenntartoi** — supports [value-modeled: plurality-preserving improvement] — Strengthening general schools without structural mandates respects plurality while helping pupils everywhere. Condition: Compensation must be maintainer-neutral.
- **oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: 2026 package (workload, equity) + Kockás könyv goal] — This archetype also calls for stronger general schools — but as the floor of structural reform, not its substitute. Condition: Acceptable only alongside a stated structural trajectory.
- **pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang** — supports [value-modeled: Program 2024, ch. 5 and 12] — Priority funding for disadvantaged schools and lower workloads are literally in the documented programme this archetype draws on. Condition: The package must include the NOKS staff, not only teachers.
- **kormanyzati_reformrealizmus** — supports [value-modeled: program: esélyteremtő minimumok, iskolarangsor] — Mandatory equity minimums, an equity-based school ranking and targeted support are in the documented program — this scenario largely overlaps with it. Condition: Fiscal room decides the pace; promises will not outrun the budget.
- **fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely** — conditionally supports [value-modeled: 2021 proposal: standards + equity ranking as complements] — The documented proposal this archetype draws on also proposed standards and targeted support — but as complements; compensation alone leaves the selection machine running. Condition: Acceptable as phase one if selection reform is scheduled behind it.

## Argument clusters

- **A1** (S3, fact, con): The teacher shortage makes a near-term structural reorganisation infeasible. [evidence: strong — OECD Education at a Glance; PSZ/PDSZ surveys] registry fact teacher_shortage — raised by: pedagogus_erdekvedo, pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang, digitalisgazdasagi
  - interest: teachers' staffing security · value: feasibility/pragmatism vs. reform ambition · fear: reform collapses into classroom chaos under staffing pressure · relevance: high
  - affected: teachers, students in reorganised schools, school administrators · assumption: the teacher shortage will not be resolved before implementation begins · empirical uncertainty: settled — the shortage is well documented (strong evidence)
- **A2** (S3, fact, pro): Early selective tracks sort by social status more than by ability. [evidence: strong — KSH / Oktatási Hivatal admission statistics] registry fact gimn_share — raised by: eselyegyenlosegi_civil, oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom, fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely
  - interest: disadvantaged families and their children · value: equality of opportunity vs. sorting-as-merit · fear: talented but disadvantaged children lose out to family background, not ability · relevance: high
  - affected: disadvantaged and Roma pupils, families without tutoring resources · assumption: current admission practice proxies status rather than ability · empirical uncertainty: settled — well documented (strong evidence)
- **A3** (S3, value, con): Parents have the right to choose an academically intensive track for their child. — raised by: konzervativ_ertekvedo, kisgyerekes_szuloi
  - interest: parents seeking an academically intensive track · value: parental choice/subsidiarity vs. equity · fear: losing the ability to shape their child's education · relevance: high
  - affected: parents of academically strong children, private/church school sector · assumption: parental choice and equity cannot both be maximised in the current structure · empirical uncertainty: not an empirical claim — a value question
- **A4** (S2, fact, con): Restricting state selective tracks shifts demand to church and private schools. [evidence: moderate — Kertesi & Kezdi 2013; Berenyi-Berkovits-Erőss school choice studies] registry fact school_choice — raised by: egyhazi_fenntartoi, konzervativ_ertekvedo, pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang
  - interest: church/private maintainers and families who can pay · value: maintainer plurality vs. equal access to selective education · fear: state disinvestment pushes selection into fee-paying schools, worsening equity · relevance: medium
  - affected: church-maintained schools, families who cannot afford private alternatives · assumption: private/church capacity can absorb the shifted demand · empirical uncertainty: moderate evidence for the shift
- **A5** (S4, mixed, pro): Investing in general schools lifts the skills of the whole cohort, as Portugal showed. [evidence: moderate — OECD PISA trend reports; Crato 2020] registry fact portugal_improvement — raised by: digitalisgazdasagi, kormanyzati_reformrealizmus
  - interest: general-school students/teachers and employers wanting broad skills · value: universalism vs. targeted excellence · fear: without a visible flagship result, the investment path is politically fragile and gets cut · relevance: high
  - affected: general school students, general school teachers, employers · assumption: Portugal's non-structural path transfers to the Hungarian institutional context · empirical uncertainty: moderate — the Portugal effect is documented, transferability is contested
- **A6** (S1, value, pro): Admission reform is a low-risk, reversible first step that builds trust for larger reform. — raised by: kormanyzati_reformrealizmus, pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang, pedagogus_erdekvedo
  - interest: policymakers wanting incremental, low-conflict change · value: caution/reversibility vs. urgency of reform · fear: claiming progress while avoiding the structural question entirely · relevance: low
  - affected: current admission-track applicants, policymakers' credibility · assumption: a small first step actually builds trust rather than just delaying larger reform · empirical uncertainty: a strategic/value judgment, not directly measurable
- **A7** (S1, fact, con): The central written exam measures tutoring-driven preparation more than ability. [not registry-backed — treat as model knowledge] no curated source supports this claim — raised by: eselyegyenlosegi_civil, oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom
  - interest: families without resources for tutoring · value: merit vs. purchased advantage · fear: children without private tutoring lose out systematically · relevance: high
  - affected: low-income families, rural families with less tutoring access · assumption: tutoring access strongly predicts exam performance independent of ability · empirical uncertainty: unknown/contested — not registry-backed
- **A8** (S3, fact, con): Finland's equity gains came from a bundle (structure + teacher policy + support), not from structure alone. [evidence: moderate — Pekkarinen, Uusitalo & Kerr 2009] registry fact finland_comprehensive — raised by: konzervativ_ertekvedo, kisgyerekes_szuloi
  - interest: those skeptical of copying Finland wholesale · value: caution about international transfer vs. urgency for structural reform · fear: adopting only the structural piece wastes the reform and damages trust in reform generally · relevance: high
  - affected: pupils under a partial reform, teachers implementing an incomplete bundle · assumption: the structural component can be usefully separated from teacher-policy and support · empirical uncertainty: moderate — the bundling effect is documented, decomposability is contested
- **A9** (S2, value, conditional): A phase-down is acceptable only with enforceable teacher guarantees. — raised by: pedagogus_erdekvedo, pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang
  - interest: teachers facing job/role disruption from phase-down · value: reform ambition vs. protecting the workforce that must carry it out · fear: teachers lose jobs, seniority or working conditions in the transition · relevance: high
  - affected: teachers in phased-down schools, teacher unions · assumption: enforceable guarantees are politically and legally achievable within the timeline · empirical uncertainty: not empirical — a condition-setting political claim
- **A10** (S4, value, con): Compensation without structural change entrenches a two-tier system while appearing to fix it. — raised by: eselyegyenlosegi_civil, fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely, oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom
  - interest: equity advocates who see compensation as avoiding real reform · value: symbolic fairness vs. removing the structural cause of inequality · fear: the two-tier system becomes permanent because visible fixing relieves reform pressure · relevance: high
  - affected: pupils remaining in the non-selective track, equity advocates' credibility · assumption: compensation and structural preservation are politically substitutable · empirical uncertainty: not directly measurable — a prediction about political dynamics

## Reciprocity round

- **pedagogus_erdekvedo** → A2: We do not deny the SES-sorting evidence; our objection is sequencing — reform carried by an exhausted profession fails regardless of its design. (outcome: maintain)
- **kisgyerekes_szuloi** → A2: If sorting is by wealth rather than ability, that hurts our children too; we would accept need-blind admission reform that keeps the tracks predictable. (outcome: revise)
- **eselyegyenlosegi_civil** → A3: Parental rights are real, but they end where they are exercised through other children's diminished classrooms; choice must not require someone else's exclusion. (outcome: maintain)
- **konzervativ_ertekvedo** → A2: The SES-sorting data deserves an answer: our answer is widening access to the tracks, not closing them — talent is present in every class of society. (outcome: revise)
- **digitalisgazdasagi** → A1: The teacher-shortage constraint is real and we feel it first in maths and IT; any scenario must include a credible plan for those subjects or the pipeline breaks. (outcome: maintain)
- **egyhazi_fenntartoi** → A4: Yes, demand would shift to us — which is why we ask for a negotiated framework rather than exemption: unregulated spillover harms us too. (outcome: revise)
- **oktataspolitikai_reformmozgalom** → A1: The shortage argument cuts the other way: a fragmented, selective structure wastes scarce teachers on parallel small classes; consolidation economises them. (outcome: maintain)
- **pedagogus_szakszervezeti_hang** → A2: This archetype accepts the sorting evidence; the documented remedy it draws on is admission correction and desegregation within the structure — evidence of harm does not by itself pick the remedy. (outcome: maintain)
- **kormanyzati_reformrealizmus** → A10: The masking risk is fair; that is why the documented program pairs compensation with a published equity ranking — if the gap does not close, the data will say so and policy must answer. (outcome: revise)
- **fuggetlen_szakpolitikai_kutatomuhely** → A8: Correct: Finland bundled structure with teacher policy — which is an argument for bundling, not for keeping selection; the documented proposal this archetype draws on includes the support components. (outcome: maintain)

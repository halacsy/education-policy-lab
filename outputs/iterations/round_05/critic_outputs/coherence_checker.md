# Critique: coherence_checker

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit "Reduced test-preparation arms race pressure on age-10 children" [evidence: weak] directly contradicts the political_risks field, which states scarcer/reformed selection channels invite informal selection, and contradicts uncertainty 3, which admits it is unknown "whether reduced coachability survives the test-prep industry's adaptation" (confidence: low). Claiming a benefit whose own mechanism tag is weak and whose supporting uncertainty is low-confidence is internally incoherent — the field asserts as an expected benefit what the uncertainties field concedes may not occur.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Downgrade the benefit to a conditional ("possible reduction in arms-race pressure, contingent on coachability gains surviving industry adaptation") or delete it, aligning it with uncertainty 3.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The equity_impact concedes that without S1-type admission reform the residual selective places become "even more SES-skewed," yet the goal and mechanism claim equity gains from capping alone. This is an internal contradiction: the scenario's own equity field admits the mechanism worsens SES-skew for the remaining selective gate while the mechanism field credits caps with "mechanically lowering early sorting" [evidence: strong] without acknowledging the redistribution of skew it creates.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reconcile by stating the net equity effect explicitly (majority gains via peer composition vs. worsened skew at the residual gate) and note the dependency on bundling with S1.

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism relies on "asymmetric peer effects" tagged [evidence: contested], but the expected_benefits field asserts a "Weaker SES-outcome gradient within a decade" [evidence: moderate] as though the contested link holds. The benefit's moderate tag is stronger than the contested mechanism it depends on — the evidence chain is inconsistent, since the benefit cannot be better-supported than its own load-bearing mechanism.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the expected_benefit to [evidence: contested] or split it so that the structural (later-selection) component keeps a moderate tag while the peer-effect-dependent component is tagged contested.

## S4.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit "Politically inexpensive; implementable under any government" [evidence: strong] contradicts the political_risks field, which identifies "Budget vulnerability: compensation lines are the first cut in fiscal consolidation," and uncertainty on "Durability of the budget line across fiscal cycles" (confidence: low). A strong-tagged claim of durable implementability cannot coexist with a low-confidence uncertainty about that very durability.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Split the claim — retain "low political capital to enact" as strong, but downgrade "durable/implementable under any government" to weak, cross-referencing the budget-vulnerability risk and durability uncertainty.

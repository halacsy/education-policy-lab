# Critique: coherence_checker

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit "More balanced intake into selective tracks within 2-3 admission cycles [evidence: moderate]" contradicts the goal and equity_impact framing. The equity_impact concedes the reform "may relabel rather than reduce sorting if compliance is weak," and the assumptions flag that a workable SES indicator and elite-school compliance are open. A benefit tagged moderate on a 2-3 cycle horizon presupposes exactly the compliance the scenario itself treats as its most uncertain input (uncertainties: elite-school channels, confidence low). The tag overstates what a design contingent on an unresolved assumption can support.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Downgrade the benefit tag to weak, or condition it explicitly: "conditional on verified compliance (see uncertainties); moderate only where the SES indicator functions."

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: Internal contradiction between mechanism and equity_impact. The mechanism claims caps benefit the non-selected majority via "stronger peer composition in urban general-school upper grades," but equity_impact concedes that residual selective places become "even more SES-skewed" absent S1-type reform. The scenario nowhere reconciles that its own headline mechanism (retention of ambitious families, evidence weak) is the load-bearing link that, if it fails, converts the policy into pure intensification of skew — the equity_impact and the weak-tagged retention mechanism describe opposite outcomes with no stated tipping condition.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add an explicit condition stating that net equity depends on the retention mechanism holding; specify the observable (family exit rate) that distinguishes the benign from the skew-intensifying regime.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The evidence_status field self-rates as "contested" and identifies peer-effect asymmetry as genuinely disputed, yet the mechanism tags the asymmetric peer-effect claim "contested" while expected_benefits assumes a net positive gradient effect at "moderate." A benefit that rests on a mechanism the scenario itself labels contested cannot carry a higher evidence tag than the mechanism supporting it. The source (cross-country tracking) supports later-selection direction of effect but not the asymmetric-peer magnitude the benefit implicitly requires.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split the benefit: keep "weaker SES gradient (direction)" at moderate but tag any magnitude claim depending on peer asymmetry as contested, mirroring the mechanism.

## S4.expected_benefits
Objection: Contradiction between expected_benefits and uncertainties. The benefit "Improved outcomes in disadvantaged general schools within 5 years (Portuguese trajectory) [evidence: moderate]" is directly undercut by the scenario's own first uncertainty: "no country has demonstrated it at Hungary's selection age (confidence: low)." The Portuguese source supports gains where selection was not this early; transposing it to Hungary's age-10 selection is precisely the untested step. The moderate tag imports external evidence across the very boundary the scenario flags as undemonstrated.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Retag to weak for the Hungarian context, and restate the benefit as "outcome gains demonstrated in Portugal; unverified at Hungary's earlier selection age."

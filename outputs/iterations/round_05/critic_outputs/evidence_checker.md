# Critique: evidence_checker

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: "Stronger peer composition in urban general-school upper grades [evidence: moderate]" is graded above what the mechanism supports. The peer-composition improvement depends on the retention link that the scenario's own evidence_status labels "the unevidenced link" and the mechanism tags [weak]. A downstream benefit cannot be graded moderate when its precondition (families staying in the common track) is weak; if high-achievers exit, upper-grade peer composition does not improve. The moderate tag conflates the mechanical cap effect with the demand-retention effect.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Regrade to [weak], or split into two claims: mechanical intake reduction [strong] and retention-dependent peer improvement [weak, conditional on advanced-programme uptake].

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The tag "[evidence: strong]" on "Later between-school selection weakens the SES-performance link, per cross-country tracking evidence" overstates what cross-country tracking studies establish. That literature (e.g. PISA-based tracking-age correlations) supports a direction of association, not a strong causal effect transferable to Hungary; the scenario's own evidence_status calls Hungarian implementation capacity "genuinely disputed" and benefits are only tagged [moderate]. A [strong] mechanism tag feeding [moderate] expected_benefits is internally inconsistent.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Regrade the mechanism to [moderate] with a note that the evidence is associational/cross-country and its Hungarian transferability is contested, aligning it with the [moderate] benefit tag.

## S4.mechanism
Objection: "Targeted resources and retention reduction improved outcomes in Portugal without changing selection age [evidence: moderate]" cites Portugal (TEIP), but Portugal did not have Hungary's age-10/12 between-school selection — so the source does not support the claim as applied to offsetting selection this early. The scenario itself concedes in uncertainties that "no country has demonstrated it at Hungary's selection age." The [moderate] tag imports evidence from a non-analogous selection context.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to [weak] for the Hungarian application, or restrict the [moderate] tag to the narrow claim that TEIP-style targeting improved outcomes in Portugal, explicitly separating it from the offset-early-selection claim.

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: "More balanced intake into selective tracks within 2-3 admission cycles [evidence: moderate]" attaches a specific timeframe (2-3 cycles) that the cited abroad admission-rule evidence does not calibrate to Hungarian elite-gimnazium compliance. The evidence_status notes effects on intake composition are documented abroad, but the assumptions flag that compliance may route selection "through informal channels" — an unresolved risk that directly governs the speed and size of the intake shift. The moderate grade covers the direction but not the 2-3-cycle timing.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Keep [moderate] for direction of effect but drop or hedge the "2-3 admission cycles" quantifier to [weak], conditional on the pilot compliance-audit results named in uncertainties.

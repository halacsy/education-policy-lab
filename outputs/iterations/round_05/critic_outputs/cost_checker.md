# Critique: cost_checker

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The cost_categories omit the recurring cost of the SES indicator/verification infrastructure that the assumptions and mechanism both require ("A workable SES indicator exists at application time"). Building and maintaining an SES-verification system at application time — data collection, fraud-checking against gaming, appeals handling — is a non-trivial recurring administrative cost, not the "low" flat administration line stated. The scenario categorizes admission-process administration as "low" without accounting for the per-applicant SES verification burden that the equity mechanism depends on.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a recurring line: "SES-indicator verification and appeals administration (medium; scales with applicant volume; includes anti-gaming audit)" and downgrade the confidence that total recurring cost is "low."

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The advanced-programme funding is tagged "medium, formula-based" but this conflates transition and steady-state costs. During the decade-long phase-down there is a period where the state funds both the still-existing selective places AND the ramping general-school advanced programmes — a double-running cost peak that the "medium, recurring" framing hides. The cost profile is not flat; it bulges in the transition years before selective places are fully reduced.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split the line into "transition-peak (medium-high, years 1-5 while selective places co-exist with new programmes)" and "steady-state recurring (medium)"; state the peak-year fiscal exposure explicitly.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The one-off teacher-retraining cost is tagged "high" but there is no recurring cost line for sustaining differentiation capacity — teacher turnover means retraining is not a one-off. At national scale with normal attrition, a substantial share of teachers needs the differentiation training on an ongoing basis, plus in-service refreshers. Treating national differentiation capacity as a purely one-off cost understates the steady-state budget and mislabels a recurring commitment as transitional.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add "Recurring: differentiation training for new-entrant and re-assigned teachers, plus in-service refreshers (medium, ongoing)"; reclassify part of the "high one-off" as sustained recurring cost.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The cost_categories list teacher premia as "medium-high" recurring but omit the ratchet/escalation dynamic that the uncertainties section itself flags ("teacher premium size needed to actually move experienced teachers" is unknown, confidence low). If the initial premium fails to move teachers, the fiscally rational response is to raise it — so the cost is not a fixed medium-high line but an open-ended commitment whose eventual size is unbounded until the mobility response materializes. The stated cost understates this contingent escalation.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add a note to the premia line: "cost is contingent and may escalate — budget must anticipate upward adjustment if initial premium fails the mobility threshold (see uncertainties)"; present a plausible high-end scenario, not a single point estimate.

# Critique: cost_checker

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The cost list omits the compliance-audit and monitoring costs that the scenario's own uncertainties section says are needed ("would be reduced by: compliance audit design in the pilot") and that political_risks flags as a real threat (informal selection channels, maintainer exemption claims). Auditing elite-school compliance and adjudicating exemption claims is a recurring administrative and potentially legal cost, not covered by "administration of new admission process (low)." As written, the cost line implicitly assumes full compliance, which contradicts the scenario's own assumptions section ("Elite gimnaziums comply rather than route selection through informal channels").
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a recurring line "Compliance monitoring and exemption adjudication (low-medium, ministry + legal)" and explicitly flag it as contingent on the compliance assumption failing.

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: "Recurring: advanced-programme funding in general schools (medium, formula-based)" gives no range and no time horizon, conflating a transition-period cost (ramping up programmes as caps bite over a decade) with the steady-state cost once caps are fully phased in and demand has (hypothetically) shifted. Since the mechanism itself concedes the demand-retention effect is only weakly evidenced, the funding requirement could either shrink (if families don't materialize) or need to scale up sharply (if it works) — the single "medium" figure hides this fiscal uncertainty entirely, and there is no line for the cost of the year-5 evaluation/adjustment mechanism referenced in implementation_steps.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split into "transition-period programme funding (medium, years 1-5, scaling with cap trajectory)" and "steady-state programme funding (uncertain range, contingent on year-5 evaluation)", and add a one-off line for the evaluator/adjustment mechanism cost.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: Both one-off items ("nationwide teacher retraining," "network and building reconfiguration") are labelled only "(high)" with no range, no per-cohort or per-district unit cost, and — more consequentially — there is no recurring line for sustaining differentiation quality after the initial retraining (coaching, refresher training, materials), even though the uncertainties section itself doubts "whether in-school differentiation reaches acceptable quality at national scale" over time, not just at launch. This is a transition-vs-steady-state gap: the cost model prices the training event but not the ongoing capability it claims to build.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add "Recurring: differentiation coaching/refresher training and materials (medium, ongoing post-launch)" and require a per-district cost range for retraining rather than a single "(high)" tag.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: "Political capital: low" is inconsistent with the scenario's own political_risks line stating compensation budgets are "the first cut in fiscal consolidation" — a package that is fiscally this exposed should carry at least medium political capital cost to defend recurring funding across budget cycles, and the cost table has no line at all for the statutory earmarking effort that uncertainties says would be needed to secure durability ("would be reduced by: statutory earmarking attempt"). Labeling political capital "low" understates the fight required to keep a "high" recurring funding line alive, which is precisely the scenario's central fiscal-sensitivity risk.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reclassify political capital as "low upfront, medium-high to sustain," and add a one-off line "Statutory earmarking / budget-protection effort (low-medium, legislative)."

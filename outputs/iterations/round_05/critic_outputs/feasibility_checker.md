# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "teacher capacity for differentiated instruction can be built at national scale within the transition window" is unsupported by any capacity estimate. Nationwide retraining of the differentiation workforce is the binding constraint, yet no scenario field quantifies the annual throughput of teacher-education universities, the number of teachers needing retraining, or whether the pipeline can supply them within a 6-10 year window. The synthesis itself flags teacher supply as a binding constraint, but the assumption is stated as a hopeful precondition rather than tested against real training capacity.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a capacity sub-analysis: estimate the stock of teachers requiring differentiation retraining, the current annual output/retraining capacity of teacher-education institutions, and derive the minimum feasible transition window from that ratio; make the assumption conditional on hitting that throughput.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "launch the general-school advanced-programme fund" assumes administrative bandwidth to design, disburse and monitor a formula-based fund simultaneously with running annual caps across three maintainer types, but no step addresses the administrative lead time to build the funding formula and the intake-composition reporting system before cap year 1. If the fund lags the caps, the retention mechanism (already tagged weak) fails precisely in the transition years when the arms race intensifies.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Sequence the steps: mandate that the advanced-programme fund and reporting infrastructure be operational and evaluated for at least one cycle before the first binding cap year, and add a lead-time estimate for standing up the formula and monitoring system.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The steps compress "design and pilot the new assessment," "amend admission decree," and "run first reformed admission cycle" without acknowledging the legal lead time for decree amendment plus the multi-year cycle needed to validate a new age-appropriate assessment instrument. A less-coachable assessment that is deployed before psychometric validation risks either legal challenge or reverting to coachable formats — undermining the core mechanism.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Insert an explicit validation gate: require at least two pilot cycles of psychometric and coachability validation before the decree mandates system-wide use, and state the decree's minimum legal notice period ahead of the first live cycle.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The teacher-premia cost is tagged "medium-high," but the scenario's own uncertainty admits the premium size needed to move experienced teachers is unknown (confidence: medium, resolvable only by a discrete-choice study). A cost band cannot be credibly assigned before that elasticity is known; if the premium required to actually relocate experienced teachers is large, the "politically inexpensive" benefit claim collapses.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Replace the fixed "medium-high" band with a range conditioned on the teacher-mobility elasticity study, and flag that the "politically inexpensive / low political capital" claim holds only at the low end of that range.

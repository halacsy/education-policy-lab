# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim of "more balanced intake into selective tracks within 2-3 admission cycles" ignores the assessment-agency lead time named in the scenario's own mechanism. Building and validating a "less preparation-sensitive assessment" is a psychometric development task that typically requires multi-year item-banking, trialling and bias-testing before it can be used for a real admission decision — not something that fits inside cycle 1, which the implementation_steps themselves reserve for "design and pilot." The 2-3 cycle horizon therefore only leaves 1-2 cycles for the reformed instrument to actually run and show a composition effect, which is too short a base for the "moderate" evidence tag to support a confident timeline claim.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Separate "assessment validated" from "assessment shows composition effect" as two distinct milestones, and revise the benefit timeline to 4-5 cycles (validation cycle + 2-3 operating cycles) unless a fast-track validation protocol is specified.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "enact the cap trajectory with felmenő rendszer guarantees for enrolled pupils" commits every maintainer to running two parallel admission/place-allocation regimes (old uncapped rules for already-enrolled cohorts, new capped rules for entrants) for the 6-8 years it takes an enrolled cohort to graduate. The scenario names no administrative mechanism or staffing for this dual-track bookkeeping, and it is least plausible for church and private maintainers, who lack the centralized tankerület reporting infrastructure that state schools have. Without this, "annual entry-place caps" is a legal instruction, not an administratively executed one.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add an explicit implementation step assigning parallel-cohort tracking to a named administrative body (e.g., KIR/statistical office) with a reporting format mandatory for all maintainer types, before the cap trajectory is enacted.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The steps list "launch differentiation retraining at scale" and "pilot comprehensive model in 2-3 districts" as concurrent actions under the same ministry+university actor line. This contradicts ordinary rollout logic (pilot before scale) and, more consequentially, ignores that teacher-education universities have a fixed annual training capacity — nationwide retraining "at scale" for differentiated instruction cannot be launched before the pilot has even generated the training curriculum and observed outcomes that the uncertainties section says are needed ("whether in-school differentiation reaches acceptable quality at national scale... would be reduced by pilot-district classroom observation"). As written, the scale-up step has no capacity ceiling and no dependency on pilot results, which is the single largest feasibility risk in the whole scenario set given S3's own admission that this is the most capacity-constrained option.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Re-sequence into two dependent steps: (1) pilot-district retraining and evaluation (fixed 2-3 year window), (2) scale-up gated on a named teacher-training-capacity figure (seats/year at teacher-ed universities) and on pilot evaluation results, not launched in parallel.

# Expert analysis: finnish_reform
## Findings (evidence)
- The Finnish peruskoulu reform, implemented in the 1970s, abolished early academic selection and streaming in basic education [evidence: strong — Finnish National Agency for Education].
- The reform aimed to provide all students with a common, high-quality education regardless of socioeconomic background or location [evidence: strong — Finnish National Agency for Education].
- Key elements included a unified nine-year comprehensive school, a focus on individualized learning, and a reduction in school-level autonomy in curriculum design initially, later balanced with increased local freedom [evidence: moderate — OECD].
- The equity effect of the reform is widely attributed to the elimination of early tracking and the provision of equal opportunities and resources across all schools [evidence: strong — OECD].
- Finland's PISA results have shown high performance and low variation between schools, often cited as a consequence of this equitable system [evidence: strong — PISA reports].
## Interpretation
The Finnish peruskoulu reform represents a deliberate policy choice to postpone academic selection until after the comprehensive school stage. This approach prioritizes equity by ensuring all students receive a common educational foundation, thereby mitigating the impact of socioeconomic disparities on educational outcomes. The success of this model, as indicated by international assessments, suggests that delaying selection and focusing on comprehensive, high-quality instruction for all can lead to both high achievement and equitable results. The transferability of this model hinges on understanding the specific societal and historical context in which it was implemented, including the strong social consensus and investment in public education.
## Assumptions
- The Hungarian education system currently practices early academic selection. [assumption]
- The goal of Hungarian educational policy is to improve both equity and academic outcomes. [assumption]
- International comparisons of educational systems are valid and informative for policy recommendations. [assumption]
## Position
Hungary should consider abolishing early academic selection and adopting a comprehensive, equity-focused basic education model similar to Finland's peruskoulu to improve educational outcomes and reduce inequality.

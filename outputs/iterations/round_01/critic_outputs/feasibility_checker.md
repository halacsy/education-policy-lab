# Critique: feasibility_checker
## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The implementation steps assume the Ministry of Education can unilaterally "negotiate equity thresholds with church and foundation maintainers." Given the "fragmented maintainers (state centres, churches, foundations)" and the "legal/contractual barriers" mentioned in the `legal_and_governance` expert output, negotiation alone is unlikely to be sufficient for binding rule changes. This step underestimates the legal and administrative lead times required to enforce such mandates across diverse maintainers, especially if they resist, as noted in `S1.political_risks`.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "Ministry — front-load teacher recruitment/retraining during the 2025–2035 retirement window" is presented as a feasible action, but `S2.uncertainties` explicitly states: "No published estimate of cost/timeline for retraining ~5,000–8,000 teachers in mixed-ability pedagogy exists for Hungary." Without this critical information, the feasibility of front-loading such a large-scale, complex retraining effort within a specific timeframe, especially given the existing "teacher shortage" mentioned in the overview, is highly questionable and unsupported.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "Ministry — reform free-choice rules and catchment boundaries simultaneously to curb between-school sorting" is a significant undertaking that is likely to face substantial administrative and political resistance. The scenario acknowledges "Strong, organised middle-class opposition that has blocked prior reform attempts" (`S3.political_risks`) and that "choice, catchment, and maintainer diversity fragment the system independently of track existence" (`hungarian_education_system` expert output). Reforming these deeply entrenched mechanisms simultaneously with a major structural reform (integrating 6/8-year forms) is an enormous administrative burden and political risk, potentially overwhelming the capacity for effective implementation.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "Ministry — pilot intensive TEIP-style support in underperforming vocational and lower-track schools in selected regions" is presented without acknowledging the significant fiscal commitment required for such a program. `S4.assumptions` notes that "Hungary's political appetite for targeted school funding matches Portugal's 2006–2015 fiscal commitment," and `S4.political_risks` states that "Sustained targeted funding requires fiscal commitment that may not match Portugal's; funding could lapse across cycles." Piloting requires not just initial funding but a credible commitment to sustained, intensive funding, which is a major feasibility challenge given the political and fiscal uncertainties highlighted.

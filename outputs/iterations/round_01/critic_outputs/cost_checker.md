# Critique: cost_checker

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The advanced-programme funding in general schools is tagged "medium, formula-based" recurring, but this cost is treated as if it terminates or stabilizes. The scenario reallocates "freed capacity" from shrinking selective places, yet the cost_categories omit the transition-period double-running cost: for a decade, the system funds both the still-operating (capped but not closed) selective places under felmenő rendszer guarantees AND the ramping general-school programmes. This concurrent-funding hump is the largest cost of the scenario and is missing entirely.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The one-off teacher-retraining cost is tagged "high" but given no range or magnitude anchor, despite assumptions.teacher-capacity being the pivotal feasibility question flagged in evidence_status and the synthesis. "Nationwide teacher retraining for differentiation" is a recurring cost during the decade-long phase-in (successive cohorts of teachers, backfill for released teachers, sustained coaching), not a one-off — mislabeling it one-off understates both magnitude and duration, exactly the transition-vs-steady-state confusion that determines whether the reform is fundable.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: Both major lines (targeted funding formula "high"; teacher premia "medium-high") are tagged recurring but carry no fiscal-sensitivity range, while political_risks and uncertainties both identify budget durability across fiscal cycles as the decisive failure mode. A cost_categories block whose central claim is that this package is "the package" (high recurring spend) must state the annual magnitude band; without it, the "politically inexpensive" claim in expected_benefits (tagged strong) is uncosted and contradicts the high recurring commitment.

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: Recurring administration of the new admission process is tagged "low," but the mechanism introduces SES-aware rules requiring a "workable SES indicator at application time" (per assumptions). Building, verifying, and adjudicating individual SES status each admission cycle — including appeals and fraud checks — is a recurring administrative and data-governance cost that is neither low nor accounted for; the "low" tag omits the ongoing cost created by the scenario's own SES-verification requirement.

# Critique: devil_advocate

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes elite gimnaziums "will not bypass" reformed admissions via oral exams or alternative pathways, but this is stated as a fragile precondition without any mechanism preventing exactly this bypass — and the synthesis itself notes fragmented governance between state and non-state maintainers, who retain selection autonomy. The scenario's own "Political risks" section admits non-state maintainers may litigate over autonomy, which directly undermines the assumption rather than merely risking it. The mechanism claim that quotas "limit siphoning" is contradicted by the scenario's own uncertainty about voluntary maintainer alignment — this is not a side risk, it is the load-bearing assumption failing.

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit "reduced concentration of privilege... without incurring high transition costs" is tagged [evidence: weak], yet it is listed as a primary expected benefit rather than flagged as speculative in the equity_impact or uncertainties section. Given the synthesis's own admission that primary-stage sorting (residential segregation, feeder-school effects) is the dominant driver of SES skew (per the "highly unequal outcomes" consensus item), an admissions-only reform cannot plausibly deliver "reduced concentration of privilege" system-wide — at most it reduces concentration within the narrow admitted cohort. The benefit as phrased overstates scope relative to the weak tag.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that felmenő rendszer "satisfies constitutional principles of legal certainty and acquired rights" is asserted as a fact-checkable legal claim but carries no evidence tag at all, while the synthesis's legal_and_governance minority view treats this as actively contested and requiring negotiation over 8+ years — i.e., the experts do not agree this assumption holds, they propose it as a hedge against constitutional risk. Presenting it as a satisfied assumption rather than an open legal uncertainty is unsupported (not merely speculative) given the Constitutional Court practice citation belongs to a different, more cautious scenario position.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The claim that the reform "significantly improves equity... reducing between-school sorting" ignores the scenario's own uncertainty that "middle-class parents will bypass the reform by moving children to private or church schools that resist state mandates." If bypass is a live uncertainty, the equity_impact section cannot assert "significant" improvement as if settled — the two sections contradict each other, and the equity claim should be downgraded to conditional on non-state maintainer compliance, which S2 itself elsewhere treats as legally contestable.

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism relies on Hanushek & Woessmann 2006 (cross-country tracking-age analysis) and Jakubowski et al. 2016 (Poland-specific difference-in-differences) to justify a "strong" evidence tag, but the scenario's own assumptions section admits "Polish and Finnish... outcomes are applicable to the Hungarian institutional and socio-political context" is itself an unverified assumption (speculative). The evidence tag "strong" describes the international literature's internal validity, not its transportability to Hungary — conflating these is the actual load-bearing flaw, since the synthesis elsewhere notes a Polish-style political reversal as a live risk, meaning the Polish case may equally support the opposite conclusion (reform fragility) as it supports the achievement-gain mechanism cited here.

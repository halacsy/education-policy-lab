# Critique: devil_advocate
## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim of "Reduced SES concentration at entry without dismantling institutional structures [evidence: contested]" is directly contradicted by the `Evidence status` field, which states "the effect of admission-rule changes on composition is contested and not directly quantified for Hungary." This makes the benefit speculative and unsupported by the provided evidence.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "Staged reform reduces reversal risk relative to abrupt change (untested — may merely extend conflict)" is presented as an assumption, but the parenthetical "untested — may merely extend conflict" directly undermines its validity. If it's untested and potentially counterproductive, it's a weak foundation for a gradual phase-down strategy.

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim of "Consistent reduction of the SES-score gradient, which in Hungary is elevated at 19–25% vs 12–14% OECD average [evidence: strong]" is too strong. While the high SES gradient in Hungary is strongly evidenced, the `Evidence status` for S3 states "the Hungary-specific magnitude and the bundled-supports requirement introduce substantial conditionality and unquantified risk." This means a *consistent reduction* of a specific magnitude for Hungary is not strongly supported, only the general direction.

## S4.equity_impact
Objection: The statement "Aims to raise the floor for non-selected pupils rather than remove the age-10–12 gatekeeper; leaves the early-selection sorting mechanism and its high-SES skew intact, so it mitigates rather than reverses stratification and does not address the loss of political voice/resources that follows departed top students" is not an objection to a flaw but rather a description of the scenario's inherent limitations. The task is to find a concrete flaw in the scenario's claims, not to summarize its design.

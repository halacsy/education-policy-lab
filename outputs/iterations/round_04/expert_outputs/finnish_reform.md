# Expert analysis: finnish_reform

## Findings (evidence)
- Finland's peruskoulu (comprehensive school to age 16, phased in 1972-1977) is associated with high equity and reduced dependence of outcomes on family background; effects are entangled with teacher policy and support systems. [evidence: moderate — Pekkarinen, Uusitalo & Kerr 2009]

## Interpretation
Finland shows a comprehensive school can deliver equity, but the reform bundled structure with teacher-education overhaul and pupil support; structure alone is not the treatment.

## Assumptions
- 1970s Nordic evidence remains informative for a 2020s CEE system. [assumption]

## Position
Do not copy the structure without the support system.

## Uncertainties
- Which components of peruskoulu carried the equity effect. (confidence: low; evidence that would reduce it: decomposition studies; Norwegian/Swedish comparisons.)

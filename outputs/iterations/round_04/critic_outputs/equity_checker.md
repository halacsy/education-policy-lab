# Critique: equity_checker

## S1.equity_impact
Objection: The claim that "gains accrue to high-ability low-SES pupils" overstates who is reached. The mechanism admits the SES indicator must exist "at application time" (assumptions), but low-SES families are systematically less able to document eligibility (under-registration, informal employment, reluctance to self-identify). Without a take-up analysis, the reform can predictably reach the *already-connected* low-SES segment while missing the most disadvantaged — meaning the equity gain is concentrated among the least-disadvantaged of the target group. This equity-negative filtering is unacknowledged.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add that the SES weighting depends on documentable eligibility and flag differential take-up as an equity risk; commit the pilot to measure take-up by disadvantage stratum, not just intake composition overall.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The field concedes residual selective places become "even more SES-skewed" but treats this as a footnote. During the decade-long phase-down, the scarcer the places, the more the admission arms race (named in political_risks) advantages high-SES families — so the transition years are plausibly equity-*negative* for each cohort still competing for shrinking elite entry, while the peer-composition benefit accrues only gradually. The net sign for transition-era disadvantaged high-achievers is unassessed.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: State the transition-period equity trade-off explicitly and either bundle S1-type admission reform into the cap trajectory (to protect the shrinking places) or acknowledge S2-alone is equity-negative for disadvantaged high-achievers during transition.

## S3.equity_impact
Objection: The field frames middle-class exit to non-state schools as the sole equity risk, but omits an intra-school one: the mechanism relies on in-school differentiation (setting/enrichment), which is itself a documented channel of SES-based within-school sorting. If differentiation quality is uneven (a stated uncertainty), low-SES pupils can be concentrated in lower sets inside the common school — reproducing the gradient the reform aims to remove, without any between-school exit. This within-school equity risk is unaddressed.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add within-school setting/tracking as a distinct equity risk alongside exit; commit pilot classroom observation to monitor SES composition of sets, not just overall NABC results.

## S4.equity_impact
Objection: The claim that the package "helps the disadvantaged majority where they actually are" assumes targeting reaches them, but the assumptions field itself flags a stigma risk and the evidence that no country has offset selection this early. More consequentially, the equity_impact omits that compensation directed only at *disadvantaged general schools* does nothing for disadvantaged pupils dispersed in mixed or advantaged general schools — who fall below the targeting threshold. Threshold-based targeting predictably leaves out isolated disadvantaged pupils.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Acknowledge that school-level targeting misses disadvantaged pupils outside targeted schools; consider a pupil-level component or lower threshold, and measure coverage of disadvantaged pupils system-wide, not just funded-school outcomes.

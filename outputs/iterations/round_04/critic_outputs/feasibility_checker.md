# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "Teacher capacity for differentiated instruction can be built at national scale within the transition window" is asserted without engaging the supply arithmetic that the synthesis itself flags as a binding constraint. The synthesis states transition capacity binds any structural variant to a 6-12 year horizon and that teacher supply is a hard limiter; nationwide retraining of the entire lower-secondary teaching force in differentiated pedagogy is the single largest bandwidth demand in any scenario, yet the scenario provides no throughput estimate (trainers available, teachers retrainable per year, attrition). Without that arithmetic the whole reform's central substitution mechanism (in-school differentiation replaces between-school tracks) is unfeasible-until-proven, not merely uncertain.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add an explicit retraining throughput model to assumptions or implementation_steps: number of lower-secondary teachers requiring retraining, annual trainer/course capacity, and the resulting minimum years to reach adequate coverage — and reconcile that number against the named phase-in window.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "Maintainers (state, church, private) — apply annual caps; report intake composition" assumes an enforcement lever over non-state maintainers that the scenario's own political_risks and assumptions concede may not exist ("Caps are enforceable across state, church and private maintainers" / "Church maintainers may litigate or negotiate exemptions"). The implementation plan lists no legal instrument, lead time, or contingency for the case where church/private maintainers refuse or litigate caps — which, on Hungarian church-maintainer autonomy, is the most likely failure path. An implementation step that presumes compliance it cannot compel is not implementable as written.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a legal-instrument step specifying the statutory basis for imposing caps on non-state maintainers, the required legislative lead time, and an explicit contingency (e.g. differential cap applying only to state maintainers) if exemptions are won.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The cost_categories label recurring targeted funding and teacher premia as "high" and "medium-high" but the implementation_steps and political_risks treat this as "politically inexpensive" and "low" political capital. This is internally inconsistent on feasibility grounds: a high, permanent, formula-based funding line is precisely the kind of commitment that "the first cut in fiscal consolidation" targets (as the scenario's own political_risks note). Labelling the political capital "low" understates the sustained fiscal-defence bandwidth the package requires across budget cycles.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reclassify political capital as "low to enact, medium-high to sustain," and add an administrative-bandwidth cost line for annual defence/renewal of the earmarked budget against consolidation pressure.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The step sequence assumes an assessment redesign can be piloted and validated ("design and pilot the new assessment in volunteer districts") within an unstated timeframe, but developing a genuinely less-coachable, age-appropriate assessment with demonstrated predictive validity is a multi-year psychometric undertaking. The scenario gives no lead time for instrument development, and the uncertainty "whether reduced coachability survives the test-prep industry's adaptation" (confidence: low) implies validation must span at least two admission cycles before the instrument can be trusted — which the linear step list does not accommodate.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Insert an explicit instrument-development and validation phase with a stated duration (multi-year) before the "first reformed admission cycle," and make the decree amendment contingent on the pilot demonstrating reduced coachability.

# Critique: cost_checker

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The advanced-programme funding is tagged "medium, formula-based," but this is a permanent recurring cost scaling with the number of general schools that must run advanced programmes to retain ambitious families nationally — the same mechanism the scenario itself rates [evidence: weak]. If retention requires programmes in most general schools (not just urban ones), "medium" understates a large, open-ended annual liability. The cost estimate also omits the transition-year cost of running scarcer-selective-place admission churn the scenario flags as a political risk.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split into "recurring — advanced-programme fund (medium if concentrated in high-demand districts; high if universal)" and add a line-item for transition-period admission administration during the phase-down.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: This scenario conflates a large one-off transition cost with the steady-state cost and thereby understates the latter. "One-off: nationwide teacher retraining (high)" treats differentiation capacity as a one-time build, but the mechanism [evidence: moderate] depends on sustained differentiation quality at national scale — which requires recurring in-service training, replacement of attrition, and induction of new teachers. There is no recurring teacher-capability line; only a one-off item and a "recurring: in-school enrichment (medium)" line that does not cover ongoing pedagogical capability maintenance.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add "Recurring: ongoing differentiation in-service training and new-teacher induction (medium-high)" and re-label the retraining item as the initial build only.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The teacher premia are tagged "medium-high," but the scenario's own uncertainty section concedes the premium size needed to actually move experienced teachers is unknown (confidence: medium, requiring a discrete-choice study). A cost band cannot be asserted as "medium-high" while the key parameter determining it is admittedly unquantified — the range is either unsupported or should be wide enough to reflect that ignorance.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Replace "medium-high" with an explicit range flagged as parameter-dependent, e.g. "medium to high — depends on premium size (unquantified pending discrete-choice study)," linking it to the stated uncertainty.

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: Both the one-off (assessment redesign/piloting) and recurring (administration) are tagged "low," but a new "age-appropriate, less preparation-sensitive assessment" administered system-wide, plus SES-indicator verification at application time (which the assumptions concede requires "a workable SES indicator"), is not plausibly low-cost recurring. Building and auditing an SES indicator and running weighted-lottery/quota logic across all maintainers is a recurring administrative and data-governance cost the categories omit entirely.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add "Recurring: SES-indicator data collection, verification and audit (medium)" and reassess the administration line upward to reflect per-cycle SES-verification workload.

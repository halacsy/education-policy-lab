# Critique: coherence_checker

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit "Stronger peer composition in urban general-school upper grades [evidence: moderate]" contradicts the mechanism, which tags the retention of ambitious families in the common track as [evidence: weak] and the evidence_status field explicitly names "the retention of demand in general schools is the unevidenced link." Improved peer composition depends entirely on that unevidenced retention: if ambitious families are not retained, peer composition does not improve. A benefit cannot carry a higher evidence tag (moderate) than the causal link it strictly depends on (weak).
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the peer-composition benefit to [evidence: weak], or restrict the "moderate" claim to the mechanical intake reduction and separate out the peer-composition effect as contingent on retention.

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism asserts asymmetric peer effects [evidence: contested] as a load-bearing element, yet the equity_impact and expected_benefits fields treat the "largest expected equity gain of all scenarios" as the headline. If the asymmetric-peer-effect premise is genuinely contested (i.e., advantaged pupils may lose as much as disadvantaged gain), the claim of a net equity gain is not established — it rests on the disputed asymmetry. The scenario presents a contested premise as if it underwrites a confident benefit ranking.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: State the equity-gain ranking as conditional on the asymmetric-peer-effect assumption holding, and present a downside variant (symmetric effects → smaller or neutral net gain) explicitly in equity_impact.

## S4.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit "Politically inexpensive; implementable under any government [evidence: strong]" contradicts the cost_categories field, which lists targeted school funding as "high — this is the package" and teacher premia as "medium-high" recurring costs. A package whose core is high recurring expenditure is not "politically inexpensive" in any durable sense; the political_risks field confirms this by naming budget vulnerability as the first cut in consolidation. "Strong" evidence for cheap durability is inconsistent with the scenario's own cost and risk analysis.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split "politically inexpensive" (low political capital to enact) from "fiscally cheap" (false — high recurring cost), and downgrade the durability claim to [evidence: weak] given the stated budget-vulnerability risk.

# rejected_framings.md

## S1 — Admission reform within the current structure

- CHOSEN: Admission reform within the current structure — rebalance who gets in via redesigned entry examination, regional equity quotas, and non-state maintainer regulation, without abolishing tracks.

- REJECTED: "Targeted selection using refined SES proxy indicators" — reason: The framing obscured the load-bearing assumption that a workable SES indicator exists at application time. The chosen framing makes the assumption explicit and flags it as unverified in the Hungarian administrative context.

- REJECTED: "Levelling the playing field through fairer assessment" — reason: This framing implied the redesigned instrument would be neutrally fairer, masking the political reality that scarcer/reformed channels invite informal selection and off-exam sorting (flagged in political_risks). The chosen framing acknowledges both the mechanism and the offsetting risk.

- REJECTED: "Equity-quota reweighting without structural change" — reason: This framing emphasized the quota mechanism but elided the three separate assumptions (workable SES indicator; church/foundation consent; outreach-driven applicant-pool broadening), any of which failing would narrow the gain. The chosen framing names the goal and mechanism but makes assumptions explicit.

- REJECTED: "Assessment-led reform with maintainer coordination" — reason: This framing led the framing with instrument design, which is only the first mechanism element. It underrepresented the political and administrative difficulty of extending equity rules across state and non-state maintainers via renegotiation or conditionality. The chosen framing foregrounds the goal and mechanism equally.

## S2 — Gradual phase-down of 6/8-year entry places

- CHOSEN: Gradual phase-down of 6/8-year entry places — reduce early-entry places on a published trajectory, protecting enrolled pupils, while funding advanced programmes in general schools to retain ambitious families.

- REJECTED: "Phased reduction of selective-track intake" — reason: This framing treated the cap as the sole mechanism, obscuring that the expected equity gain depends on a secondary, weakly evidenced retention mechanism (advanced-programme funding retaining ambitious families). The chosen framing names both the cap and the retention link, making the dependency explicit.

- REJECTED: "Timing reform without structural abolition" — reason: This framing foregrounded the timing-sensitivity literature (moderate evidence), but obscured that the cap's downstream effect on the SES gradient is not measured in the registry and depends on families not migrating to uncapped non-state places. The chosen framing names the timing goal and flags the migration uncertainty.

- REJECTED: "Protective transition to later selection" — reason: This framing emphasized the felmenő rendszer protection mechanism, but underrepresented the reversal risk explicitly flagged in political_risks (Polish precedent). The chosen framing keeps the mechanism but adds the political-risk transparency and the entrenchment step.

- REJECTED: "Gradual track phase-down with absorption guarantees" — reason: This framing promised that general schools would absorb displaced demand, which is asserted as an assumption, not evidenced. The chosen framing keeps the absorption link explicit in the mechanism but flags it as an assumption with low confidence.

- REJECTED: "Equity via timing without immediate structural change" — reason: This framing implied S2 was structurally neutral, eliding that the gap between cap reduction and abolition creates transition-cohort harm: as caps tighten, better-informed higher-SES families capture the shrinking remaining places first. The chosen framing flags this harm in equity_impact.

## S3 — Comprehensive school to age 14 (structural reform)

- CHOSEN: Comprehensive school to age 14 (structural reform) — abolish early selection by moving tracking to age 14, bundled with teacher-quality and diagnostic-support investment, on the Finnish/Polish model.

- REJECTED: "Delayed selection with peer-effect amplification" — reason: This framing foregrounded the asymmetric-peer-effect mechanism, which is tagged [evidence: contested] in the scenario itself. The chosen framing names the structural change as the primary mechanism and flags the peer-effect channel as contested in evidence_status.

- REJECTED: "Comprehensive lower-secondary without structural amendment to upper-secondary" — reason: This framing obscured that the policy abolishes the 6/8-year entry forms, which is a comprehensive structural amendment. The chosen framing names the full scope: moving tracking from age 10/12 to age 14.

- REJECTED: "Finnish-style comprehensive model transplanted to Hungary" — reason: This framing treated Finnish evidence as directly transferable, obscuring the load-bearing assumption that teacher supply can staff a fully comprehensive system (contradicted by documented Hungarian shortage) and that sorting does not simply migrate to school choice and non-state schools. The chosen framing flags both assumptions as unresolved.

- REJECTED: "Peer-composition improvement via heterogeneous grouping" — reason: This framing foregrounded the pedagogical mechanism (heterogeneous grouping enabling asymmetric peer effects), but the scenario's own equity_impact concedes the central risk: if middle-class families exit to non-state schools, the downside concentrates on lower-SES pupils left in the general track. The chosen framing flags this distributional-equity blind spot.

- REJECTED: "Structural reform with optional legislative protection for pilot rollout" — reason: This framing treated the pact attempt and fallback to S2 as optional garnish. The chosen framing integrates them into implementation_steps, making clear that the scenario includes a contingency (S2 phase-down) if cross-party consensus fails.

- REJECTED: "Teacher retraining and diagnostic support as co-benefits" — reason: This framing positioned teacher investment as enabling the structural change. The scenario's own implementation_steps correct this: retraining must be sequenced *after* pilot evaluation, not concurrently, because the teacher shortage forbids scaling retraining while piloting. The chosen framing makes sequencing explicit.

## S4 — Keep the structure, compensate general schools (Portuguese-style package)

- CHOSEN: Keep the structure, compensate general schools (Portuguese-style package) — retain the 6/8-year structure unchanged and neutralise its equity effects through targeted funding, curriculum standards, and school clustering, on the Portuguese TEIP model.

- REJECTED: "Non-structural compensation to offset sorting" — reason: This framing implied the package offsets sorting; S4's own uncertainties and evidence_status concede this is undemonstrated. The chosen framing says "neutralise" (which is what the goal claims) but flags in evidence_status that whether compensation can offset rather than mask sorting is unresolved (low confidence).

- REJECTED: "Rapid, low-disruption equity improvement via TEIP replication" — reason: This framing implied the Portuguese effect size transfers directly to Hungary and that disruption is avoided. The chosen framing acknowledges the Portuguese trajectory is reasonable evidence for Portugal but flags the Hungarian context is different (earlier selection age, different teacher-supply conditions) and notes the dynamic equity cost: sustained compensation risks legitimising early selection permanently.

- REJECTED: "Curriculum standards and targeted funding without track restructuring" — reason: This framing emphasized the standards and funding mechanisms separately, obscuring that they are bundled with school clustering and must coordinate to produce the claimed outcome. The chosen framing keeps all three mechanisms visible and flags that all depend on the undemonstrated assumption that compensation can offset sorting.

- REJECTED: "Politically inexpensive structural preservation with equity gains" — reason: This framing treated political inexpensiveness as a strong benefit, but S4's own political_risks section and cost_categories flatly contradict it: compensation lines are the first cut in fiscal consolidation, and teacher-salary premia require political capital to defend. The chosen framing downgrades political inexpensiveness to weak evidence and flags the budget vulnerability.

- REJECTED: "Teacher-support and standards-driven improvement across general schools" — reason: This framing led with the pedagogical mechanism (standards, teacher support, grade-retention reduction), but obscures that the expected-benefits timeline (5 years) is borrowed from the Portuguese trajectory without adjustment for Hungary's earlier selection age or teacher-supply constraints. The chosen framing keeps the mechanisms but flags the transfer assumption as unresolved.

- REJECTED: "Compensation as sorting-mitigation without structural change" — reason: This framing implied the package mitigates sorting. S4's own equity_impact and political_risks concede the risk is the opposite: sustained compensation may entrench the sorting structure rather than dissolve it. The chosen framing makes this dynamic-equity cost explicit.

## Evidence and assumption handling across rejected framings

Every rejected framing was set aside because it either:

1. **Obscured load-bearing assumptions** (e.g., "Levelling the playing field" in S1 masked the assumption that informal selection would not reappear; "Finnish-style comprehensive" in S3 masked the teacher-supply assumption).

2. **Inflated evidence status** (e.g., "Rapid, low-disruption equity improvement" in S4 treated undemonstrated transfer of the Portuguese effect size as established; "Peer-effect amplification" in S3 foregrounded a [contested] mechanism as the primary driver).

3. **Suppressed internal contradictions** (e.g., "Politically inexpensive" in S4 contradicted the scenario's own political_risks field; "Delayed selection with peer effects" in S3 relied on the contested peer mechanism while the equity_impact identified middle-class exit as the central risk).

4. **Simplified away distributional inequity** (e.g., "Comprehensive lower-secondary" in S3 missed that if exit occurs, the least-mobile disadvantaged pupils absorb the loss).

5. **Conflated mechanism with outcome** (e.g., "Absorption guarantees" in S2 treated the assumption of absorption as a mechanism feature).

The chosen framings preserve these tensions, flag assumptions and low-confidence links explicitly, and avoid consensus laundering. Every unresolved objection from episodic memory is addressed in the expected_benefits, assumptions, evidence_status, and political_risks fields of the scenarios themselves.

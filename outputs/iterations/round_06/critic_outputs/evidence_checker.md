# Critique: evidence_checker
## S1.mechanism
Objection: The evidence tag for "Regulating non-state (church/foundation) maintainer admissions in parallel prevents demand from migrating wholesale to the unregulated non-state selective sector" is graded as 'weak', citing "no Hungarian precedent for enforced uniform admissions across maintainer types; hungarian_education_system interpretation only". This is a misrepresentation of the source registry. The registry entry for 'governance' is graded 'strong' and states: "Since 2013 most state schools are run by state school district centres (tankerulet); churches and foundations maintain a...". This strong evidence indicates a clear distinction in governance, which directly supports the difficulty of regulating non-state maintainers. The current tag understates the strength of the evidence against the claim.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Change the evidence tag to 'strong' and update the citation to include 'governance' from the registry, as the registry entry directly supports the difficulty of uniform regulation across maintainer types.

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim "Falling early-sorting share of each cohort, measurable annually as a direct statistical consequence of the binding cap [evidence: strong for the mechanical share reduction; evidence: weak for any claim that this reduction translates into reduced segregation, since no registry source measures whether displaced demand moves to non-state selective tracks instead]" has a split evidence tag. The 'strong' part is correctly attributed to the mechanical effect. However, the 'weak' part regarding reduced segregation is not just weak, but directly contradicted by the 'Equity impact' section of S2 itself, which states: "Residual selective places become more SES-skewed as caps tighten...". This internal contradiction means the claim of "reduced segregation" is not merely weakly evidenced, but actively undermined by another part of the scenario.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Revise the 'weak' evidence tag to 'contested' or 'contradicted by scenario's own equity impact analysis' and explicitly acknowledge the internal contradiction. The claim should be rephrased to reflect that reduced segregation is not an expected benefit, or at least not a direct one, and that the opposite effect is more likely in the short term.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "Sorting does not simply migrate to school choice between general schools and to non-state maintainers [evidence: contested — see mechanism above; this is the load-bearing assumption on which S3's entire equity case rests, and the scenario's own equity_impact field concedes this migration is the 'largest risk,' so the assumption and the risk assessment are in direct tension rather than the assumption being a settled premise]" is tagged as 'contested'. While the explanation correctly identifies the tension, the registry entry 'school_choice' is graded 'moderate' and states: "Free school choice plus early selective tracks produces strong between-school sorting already at the primary stage in ci...". This registry entry directly supports the likelihood of sorting migrating to school choice, making the assumption not just 'contested' but actively 'unsupported' or 'contradicted' by existing evidence on school choice in Hungary.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change the evidence tag to 'contradicted by registry evidence' and update the citation to include 'school_choice'. The assumption should be rephrased to acknowledge the strong evidence for sorting migration.

## S4.mechanism
Objection: The claim "Compensation can meaningfully offset, not just mask, the effects of early sorting on disadvantaged pupils in general schools [evidence: contested — this is the load-bearing assumption of the entire scenario, and it directly conflicts with this scenario's own uncertainties below, which state that no country in the source evidence has demonstrated compensation offsetting selection effects at Hungary's selection age of 10-12; Portugal's comparator did not have an early selection point of this kind to compensate for]" is tagged as 'contested'. This is an understatement. The scenario's own 'Uncertainties' section explicitly states "no country in the reviewed evidence has demonstrated this at so early an age", which means the claim is not merely contested but directly contradicted by the available evidence base for the specific context of Hungary's selection age.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change the evidence tag to 'contradicted by scenario's own uncertainties' or 'unsupported by evidence' and explicitly highlight the direct conflict with the 'Uncertainties' section.

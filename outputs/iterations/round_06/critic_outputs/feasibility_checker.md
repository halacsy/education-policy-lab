# Critique: feasibility_checker
## S1.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that a workable SES indicator exists at application time is contradicted by the scenario's own admission that Hungary has no administrative student-level SES register usable at application, and any proxy would be imperfect and gameable. This makes the core mechanism of regional equity quotas, which relies on this indicator, fundamentally unworkable as described.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Either propose a concrete method for creating and validating a usable SES indicator, or acknowledge that the regional equity quotas mechanism is not feasible without this prerequisite.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The timeline for the Oktatási Hivatal to design and pilot a new, genuinely less-coachable assessment, including psychometric validation, is stated as year 1-3. However, the scenario's own assumptions and cost categories indicate that such an assessment requires multi-year psychometric development, making a 3-year timeline for a novel, validated instrument highly optimistic and likely infeasible.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Extend the timeline for assessment redesign and validation to reflect the complexity and time required for robust psychometric development, or acknowledge that a truly "less-coachable" assessment may not be ready within the proposed timeframe.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that caps on state-maintained schools are not simply offset by non-state maintainers expanding selective intake is presented as an assumption, but the scenario's political risks and uncertainties directly contradict this by stating that church/foundation maintainers may expand selective intake to absorb capped state demand. This creates a direct conflict where the mechanism's effectiveness is undermined by a foreseeable consequence.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Either provide evidence or a mechanism for how non-state maintainers will be prevented from offsetting the caps, or acknowledge that this assumption is highly questionable and the mechanism may fail to achieve its goal.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that sorting does not simply migrate to school choice between general schools and to non-state maintainers is directly contradicted by the scenario's own equity impact assessment, which states that middle-class exit to non-state or private schools is the largest risk and that this migration directly undercuts the assumption. This makes the core premise of the reform's equity gains highly uncertain and potentially illusory.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Either provide a robust mechanism to prevent sorting migration to non-state maintainers and school choice, or revise the equity impact to reflect the high probability of this migration and its negative consequences for disadvantaged pupils.

# Critique: political_risk_checker
## S1.political_risks
Objection: The scenario states that the private tutoring and test-prep industry "can mobilize against a redesign that reduces the returns to paid preparation" but rates the evidence as "weak — no direct study of this actor's mobilization capacity in Hungary". This is an unsupported claim of mobilization capacity. While their commercial stake is clear, the leap from "commercial stake" to "can mobilize" without any evidence of past or present mobilization, or even organizational structure, is speculative.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Rephrase the risk to focus on the *incentive* for opposition rather than an unevidenced *capacity* for mobilization, or explicitly mark it as speculative. For example: "Private tutoring and test-prep industry has a direct commercial stake in the current exam format and would have a strong incentive to oppose a redesign that reduces the returns to paid preparation (speculative: no direct study of this actor's mobilization capacity in Hungary)."

## S2.political_risks
Objection: The scenario acknowledges the Polish precedent of reversal and includes an entrenchment mechanism in implementation_steps, but then rates the legal robustness of this mechanism as "weak". This creates a contradiction: if the legal robustness is weak, the entrenchment mechanism is unlikely to effectively mitigate the risk of reversal, making the risk itself higher than implied by the "moderate" rating for the overall risk of reversal. The scenario identifies the problem (Polish reversal) and proposes a solution (entrenchment), but then immediately undermines the solution's effectiveness without adjusting the overall risk assessment.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Either provide stronger evidence for the entrenchment mechanism's robustness or raise the overall political risk rating for reversal to high, explicitly stating that the proposed entrenchment mechanism is unlikely to be effective. For example: "A successor government can freeze or reverse the trajectory cheaply if no robust entrenchment mechanism exists in law... This scenario's implementation_steps now include an explicit entrenchment step, but its legal robustness against a determined future legislature is untested [evidence: weak], making the risk of reversal high."

## S3.political_risks
Objection: The scenario states "No described fallback exists if the cross-party pact attempt fails before legislation — the scenario currently proceeds to pilot and scale-up regardless, leaving no contingency plan..." and rates the evidence as "weak — this is a structural gap in the plan rather than an evidenced risk". This is a critical flaw for a political risk assessment. A structural gap in contingency planning for a high-intensity opposition scenario *is* a significant political risk, as it implies a high likelihood of the entire reform failing if the initial, difficult condition (cross-party pact) is not met. The "weak" evidence tag here refers to the *lack* of a plan, not the weakness of the risk itself.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Rephrase to clearly state that the absence of a fallback plan constitutes a high political risk. For example: "The absence of a described fallback if the cross-party pact attempt fails before legislation constitutes a high political risk, as the scenario currently proceeds to pilot and scale-up regardless, leaving no contingency plan for a likely failure point. [evidence: structural gap in plan]."

## S4.political_risks
Objection: The scenario claims "Budget vulnerability: compensation lines are the first cut in fiscal consolidation, since they lack the legal permanence of structural changes to the public education act" but then rates the evidence for the protection mechanism's effectiveness as "weak". This directly contradicts the "politically inexpensive" benefit claimed in expected_benefits, which implies durability. If the budget line is vulnerable and the protection mechanism is weak, the long-term political risk of defunding is high, undermining the core premise of this scenario's political feasibility.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Explicitly state that the budget vulnerability makes the long-term durability of the compensation package a high political risk, and that this undermines the "politically inexpensive" claim. For example: "Budget vulnerability: compensation lines are highly susceptible to cuts in fiscal consolidation, as they lack the legal permanence of structural changes. The proposed multi-year budget line's legal robustness against a determined future budget process is untested [evidence: weak], making the long-term durability of the compensation package a high political risk and directly contradicting the 'politically inexpensive' benefit claimed elsewhere."

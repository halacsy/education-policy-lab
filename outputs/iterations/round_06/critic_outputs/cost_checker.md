# Critique: cost_checker
## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The cost category "Recurring: compliance-audit and monitoring mechanism" is listed as recurring, but its description "to verify quota adherence and detect informal circumvention" suggests it's a steady-state cost, not a transition cost. The scenario also flags it as "omitted from the original cost list despite being flagged as necessary in the uncertainties below," which implies it was a missing item, not just miscategorized. The current description doesn't clarify if this is a new, ongoing cost or a temporary one.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Clarify whether the compliance-audit and monitoring mechanism is a permanent, ongoing recurring cost (steady-state) or a recurring cost for a defined transition period. If it's a new, permanent cost, explicitly state that it's a new recurring cost for the steady state.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The cost category "One-off: nationwide teacher retraining for differentiated instruction in comprehensive classrooms" is marked as "high" but explicitly states "no numeric range or per-cohort/per-district unit cost currently available." This lack of a numeric estimate or even a plausible range makes the "high" severity subjective and unhelpful for comparison or budgeting.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Provide a plausible numeric range or a unit cost estimate (e.g., per teacher, per district) for the nationwide teacher retraining, even if it's an order-of-magnitude estimate based on similar reforms or expert judgment.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The cost category "Recurring: TEIP-style targeted funding for disadvantaged general schools" is listed as "tentatively high (double-digit percentage uplift for targeted schools based on the Portuguese TEIP model, unverified for Hungary)" but lacks a numeric range or a share-of-education-budget estimate. This makes it difficult to assess the actual fiscal impact, especially given the political risk of budget vulnerability.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Provide a more concrete numeric range for the "double-digit percentage uplift" (e.g., 10-20% or 20-30%) and, if possible, an estimate of what this would represent as a share of the total general-school operating budget in Hungary. This would make the cost more actionable and comparable.

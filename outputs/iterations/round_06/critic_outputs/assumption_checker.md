# Critique: assumption_checker
## S1.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "Church and private maintainers can be brought under the same admissions constraints as state schools, or their share is small enough that leakage does not offset state-side gains" is directly contradicted by the "Disagreement map" in the expert record. The "hungarian_education_system vs school_network_planning" section explicitly states this is an "unresolved contradiction" and that "whether the state actually has leverage to bind them is disputed rather than settled by either expert." The scenario presents this as a mere assumption, when the expert record indicates it's a fundamental, unresolved point of contention among experts, making it a highly uncertain premise for the scenario's success.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change the assumption to reflect the expert disagreement, e.g., "It is assumed that church and private maintainers *can* be brought under the same admissions constraints, though experts disagree on the state's leverage to achieve this, and if not, their share is small enough that leakage does not offset state-side gains." Also, elevate this to a "Uncertainty" with low confidence.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "General schools can absorb and challenge high-ability students if adequately funded" is listed with "evidence: contested" and a cross-reference to the mechanism field, which states "this is 'the unevidenced link' per the underlying analysis: no Hungarian study demonstrates general-school capacity to replicate the value-added or peer environment of selective tracks." An assumption should ideally be a premise taken as true for the scenario's logic, even if unproven. Here, it's explicitly stated as contested and unevidenced, making it less of an assumption and more of a critical, unresolved uncertainty that underpins the entire scenario's expected benefits.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Move this claim from "Assumptions" to "Uncertainties" and assign a low confidence level, as it is explicitly stated as "unevidenced" in the Hungarian context.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "Sorting does not simply migrate to school choice between general schools and to non-state maintainers" is listed with "evidence: contested" and a note that it is in "direct tension" with the scenario's own equity_impact field. The equity_impact field calls this migration the "largest risk." An assumption should be a premise that the scenario proceeds from, even if unproven. When the scenario itself identifies this as its "largest risk" and "load-bearing but empirically unresolved claim," it ceases to be a functional assumption and becomes a critical, acknowledged vulnerability.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Rephrase this as an "Uncertainty" with low confidence, explicitly acknowledging the internal tension with the equity_impact field.

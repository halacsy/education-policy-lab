# Critique: devil_advocate
## S1.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "Church and private maintainers can be brought under the same admissions constraints as state schools, or their share is small enough that leakage does not offset state-side gains" is directly contradicted by the "Disagreement map" and "What the experts agree on" sections of the overview, which state that the ability to regulate non-state maintainers is "disputed rather than settled by either expert" and that they operate under "independently-governed share of schools, which complicates any uniform structural mandate." This is a load-bearing assumption for the scenario's equity goals, and its contested nature makes it an unreliable premise.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Rephrase the assumption to acknowledge the high uncertainty and dispute, or move it to the "Uncertainties" section. The scenario should also explicitly address the implications if this assumption fails, as its equity impact would be severely muted.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "General schools can absorb and challenge high-ability students if adequately funded" is listed as "contested" and refers back to the mechanism and expected benefits, which also state it is "contested" and "unevidenced." This circular referencing means the assumption is not supported, but rather is a restatement of an unresolved claim. The scenario's core benefits depend on this unproven capacity.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: This assumption should be moved to the "Uncertainties" section, as it is a critical unknown. The scenario should also outline a contingency plan or research pathway to address this fundamental lack of evidence.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "Sorting does not simply migrate to school choice between general schools and to non-state maintainers" is listed as "contested" and the explanation explicitly states it is "in direct tension" with the scenario's own equity_impact field, which identifies this migration as the "largest risk." An assumption should ideally be a premise the scenario builds upon, not a contested risk that undermines its core goal.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: This should be reclassified as a major uncertainty or risk, not an assumption. The scenario needs to either provide stronger evidence to support this claim or develop explicit mitigation strategies for this acknowledged "largest risk."

## S4.mechanisms
Objection: The mechanism "Compensation can meaningfully offset, not just mask, the effects of early sorting on disadvantaged pupils in general schools" is immediately followed by an evidence tag stating it is "contested" and "directly conflicts with this scenario's own uncertainties below, which state that no country in the source evidence has demonstrated compensation offsetting selection effects at Hungary's selection age of 10-12." A mechanism should describe how the policy works, but here the core mechanism is presented as unproven and contradictory to the scenario's own assessment of evidence.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: This should be moved to the "Uncertainties" section. A mechanism should be a plausible causal link, not a contested claim that the scenario itself admits is unsupported by evidence for the specific context. The scenario needs to acknowledge that its core premise is unproven for Hungary's early selection age.

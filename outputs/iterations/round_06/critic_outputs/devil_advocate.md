## S1.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that a workable SES indicator can be built at application time is contradicted by the scenario's own evidence and uncertainties.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Either remove the assumption or explicitly state that it is a major unresolved challenge that must be addressed before the scenario can be considered viable. The scenario states that Hungary has no administrative SES register usable at application, and that any proxy would be imperfect and gameable. The uncertainties section also flags this as having low confidence.

## S1.expected_benefits
Objection: The claim that reduced test-preparation pressure is an expected benefit is undermined by the political risks section, which states that reformed/scarcer channels may invite informal selection.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reframe this benefit to acknowledge the potential for informal selection, or remove it as a clear benefit. The scenario notes that the new assessment "resists coaching" but this is contradicted by the political risks which suggest that "scarcer/reformed channels may invite informal selection".

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that caps on state-maintained schools will not be offset by non-state maintainers expanding selective intake is directly contradicted by the political risks and uncertainties sections.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Acknowledge that this is a critical assumption that is highly uncertain and likely to be a major obstacle. The scenario states that "church/foundation maintainers expand selective intake to absorb capped state demand, defeating the goal" as a political risk, and that it is uncertain "Whether non-state maintainers offset the cap" with medium confidence.

## S2.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit of stronger peer composition in urban general-school upper grades is based on a contested and weak link, as noted in the mechanism and evidence status.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Downgrade the confidence in this benefit and explicitly state that it is contingent on the contested peer-effect mechanism. The scenario states that this benefit is "contested — downstream of the asymmetric peer-effect and retention links, both of which are contested/weak in the sources".

## S3.assumptions

Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reframe this as a critical, unaddressed risk rather than an assumption. The scenario states that "this is asserted here yet the equity_impact field calls exactly this migration the largest risk; it is load-bearing and unevidenced."

## S3.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit of a weaker SES-outcome gradient within a decade is presented with moderate evidence, but the mechanism itself is tagged as contested and weak.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Qualify this benefit more strongly, acknowledging the contested nature of the primary mechanism. The scenario states that this benefit "rests on the asymmetric-peer-effects mechanism, which the mechanism field itself tags contested; the moderate-tagged cross-country equity result does not by itself deliver the decade-scale Hungarian gradient".

## S4.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that compensation can meaningfully offset, not just mask, the effects of early sorting is directly contradicted by the scenario's own uncertainties and evidence status.
Severity: high

The scenario states that "this contradicts the scenario's own uncertainties, which concede no country has demonstrated it at Hungary's selection age" and that the "pivotal uncertainty" is whether compensation can offset early sorting.

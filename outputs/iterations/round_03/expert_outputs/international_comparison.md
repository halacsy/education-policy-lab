# Expert analysis: international_comparison

## Findings (evidence)
- Cross-country difference-in-differences evidence indicates earlier between-school tracking increases inequality of outcomes without raising mean performance. [evidence: strong — Hanushek & Woessmann 2006, Economic Journal]
- In PISA, the share of reading/maths score variance explained by socio-economic status is among the highest in the OECD for Hungary (~19-25% across cycles vs ~12-14% OECD average). [evidence: strong — OECD PISA 2012-2022 country notes]

## Interpretation
Hungary combines one of the OECD's earliest selection points with one of its strongest SES-performance gradients; the correlation is consistent with, but does not by itself prove, a causal link at the country level.

## Assumptions
- PISA ESCS gradients are comparable across cycles. [assumption]

## Position
Later selection is the international default for equity-oriented systems.

## Uncertainties
- Transferability of cross-country estimates to the Hungarian institutional mix. (confidence: medium; evidence that would reduce it: within-Hungary quasi-experimental studies.)

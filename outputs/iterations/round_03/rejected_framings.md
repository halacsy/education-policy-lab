# Rejected Framings

## S1 — Admissions Reform Within Current Structure
- CHOSEN: Regulating admissions processes and establishing equity-based quotas within the existing 6- and 8-year gimnazium structure.
- REJECTED: Mandatory homogenization of admissions across all sectors — reason: The government lacks immediate political or legal leverage to enforce uniform admissions policies on church and private maintainers without triggering severe constitutional litigation.
- REJECTED: Complete elimination of early admissions criteria — reason: Banning selective criteria entirely within the existing structure would lead to immediate middle-class flight to unregulated private providers, undermining public school system stability.

## S2 — Gradual Phase-down of 6- and 8-year Entry Places
- CHOSEN: Gradual reduction of enrollment capacity in early selective tracks combined with restriction of these tracks to regional urban hubs.
- REJECTED: Immediate administrative termination of 6- and 8-year tracks — reason: Violates the constitutional protection of acquired rights and triggers unmanageable legal disputes with public and non-state maintainers.
- REJECTED: Freezing enrollment capacity at current levels — reason: Fails to address the ongoing socio-demographic sorting and exacerbates geographic disparities as rural cohorts shrink.

## S3 — Comprehensive School to Age 14 (Structural Reform)
- CHOSEN: Abolishing all academic tracking before age 14 to establish a comprehensive lower-secondary school system.
- REJECTED: Phased implementation starting with voluntary municipal mergers — reason: Voluntary structural changes fail to prevent early sorting as long as prestigious gimnaziums continue to offer early-admission tracks in neighboring municipalities.
- REJECTED: Delayed tracking combined with immediate streaming within schools — reason: Internal tracking replicates the equity-depressing sorting effects of early selection, failing to achieve the comprehensive school benefits observed in Finland.

## S4 — Compensate General Schools (Portuguese-style Package)
- CHOSEN: Retaining the early selective structure while deploying targeted resources and rigorous standards to low-performing general schools.
- REJECTED: Structural merging of low-performing primary schools into elite complexes — reason: Triggers intense opposition from elite schools and middle-class parents, while failing to address teacher shortages in marginalized areas.
- REJECTED: Universal funding increases across all primary schools without targeting — reason: Fails to close the achievement gap for disadvantaged students and dilutes the fiscal resources needed for critical regional teacher incentives.

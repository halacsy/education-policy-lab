# Critique: cost_checker

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The cost list omits the transition-year cost of the intensified admission arms race that the scenario's own political_risks section names ("Scarcer selective places intensify the admission arms race in the transition years"). If scarcer places drive families to more private test-prep and cross-district applications, that imposes real administrative load on maintainers processing surging applications for shrinking cohorts — an unbudgeted recurring transition cost distinct from the steady-state "reallocation planning per district (low-medium)."
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add a transition-only line, e.g. "Transition (years 1-5): elevated admission-processing load from over-subscription to shrinking selective places (medium, tapering)", explicitly flagged as distinct from steady-state costs.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The teacher-retraining cost is tagged "one-off (high)" but the mechanism relies on building "teacher capacity for differentiated instruction... at national scale" and the assumptions concede this capacity must be sustained through and beyond the transition. National-scale pedagogical capacity is not a one-off purchase: it requires recurring in-service renewal, replacement of attrition, and ongoing coaching. Labelling it one-off understates the fiscal commitment and creates a cliff where funding lapses once initial retraining is "done."
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split into "One-off: initial differentiation retraining rollout (high)" and "Recurring: sustained in-service renewal, new-teacher differentiation training, and instructional coaching (medium, ongoing)".

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The cost categories tag "Political capital: low," but this contradicts the scenario's own political_risks and uncertainties, which flag that the two large recurring funding lines are "the first cut in fiscal consolidation" and of uncertain "durability... across fiscal cycles." A policy whose entire mechanism is two high/medium-high recurring budget lines that must survive multiple fiscal cycles carries substantial ongoing political-capital cost to defend at each budget round — not low. The "low" tag captures only enactment, ignoring the recurring defense cost the scenario elsewhere admits is the binding risk.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change to "Political capital: low to enact, recurring-moderate to defend the budget lines each fiscal cycle" so the cost tag reflects the fiscal-sensitivity risk the scenario itself identifies as binding.

# Critique: coherence_checker

## S2.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism tags "Annual entry-place caps reduce the share of a cohort selected at 10/12, mechanically lowering early sorting [evidence: strong]" as strong, but the goal and equity_impact fields concede the reform only works if freed capacity produces "strong programmes inside general schools" that retain demand — which the evidence_status field itself calls "the unevidenced link." The strong tag applies only to the mechanical sorting reduction, not to the causal chain the scenario actually depends on. As written, the mechanism overstates the certainty of the operative claim.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split the mechanism claim: keep [evidence: strong] on the arithmetic cap effect, and explicitly tag the retention-of-demand link as [evidence: weak], mirroring the evidence_status field so the strong tag cannot be read as covering the whole mechanism.

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The mechanism relies on "asymmetric peer effects" tagged [evidence: contested], yet expected_benefits asserts a "Weaker SES-outcome gradient within a decade" and equity_impact claims "the largest expected equity gain of all scenarios." The headline benefit is derived from the one mechanism link the scenario admits is contested. The benefit claim is internally inconsistent with the contested status of its own causal premise.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the equity_impact wording from an asserted "largest expected equity gain" to a conditional claim ("largest gain *if* asymmetric peer effects hold"), and reconcile expected_benefits [evidence: moderate] with the contested mechanism it rests on.

## S4.expected_benefits
Objection: expected_benefits tags "Politically inexpensive; implementable under any government [evidence: strong]" as strong, but political_risks states compensation lines are "the first cut in fiscal consolidation" and uncertainties rates budget-line durability at "confidence: low." Claiming strong evidence for implementability-under-any-government directly contradicts the scenario's own low-confidence durability finding.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reduce the "implementable under any government" tag to [evidence: moderate] and qualify it to enactment ease only, separating it from cross-cycle durability which the uncertainties field already flags as low-confidence.

## S1.equity_impact
Objection: equity_impact states gains "accrue to high-ability low-SES pupils; does nothing for the majority," while the goal claims to "Reduce the SES bias of entry" system-wide. The evidence_status field concedes "effects on system-level equity are indirect," yet the goal is framed as a system-level equity objective. The goal overclaims relative to what equity_impact and evidence_status concede the mechanism can deliver.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Narrow the goal to "reduce SES bias *of the selective intake gate*" rather than system-wide SES bias, aligning it with the equity_impact admission that the non-selected majority is unaffected.

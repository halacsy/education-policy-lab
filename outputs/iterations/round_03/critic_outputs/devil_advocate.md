# Critique: devil_advocate

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "middle-class parents will accept admissions quotas rather than seeking to bypass them through non-state alternatives" directly contradicts the scenario's own Political Risks section, which asserts "intense, well-organized resistance from urban middle-class parents" (evidence: moderate) and the Equity Impact section, which states "risk of displacement to unregulated non-state schools remains high." A load-bearing assumption cannot assert compliance behavior that the scenario's own risk analysis rates as likely to fail.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the assumption with an explicit acknowledgment that displacement to non-state schools is the expected middle-class response, and reframe the mechanism/expected_benefits to quantify residual sorting after displacement rather than assuming quota acceptance.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "non-state school maintainers can be restricted from absorbing the phased-out selective capacity" is flatly contradicted by the disagreement map, where the hungarian_education_system/school_network_planning experts explicitly warn (evidence: strong — maintainer statistics) that "banning selective tracks in state schools will likely push selective demand into the growing non-state sector." This is not a neutral assumption but a claim the record's own strong-evidence expert view rejects.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Drop the assumption or relabel it as contested; add a mechanism component addressing how subsidy conditionality or bilateral agreements would actually constrain church/foundation capacity expansion, since current text offers no lever for this beyond hope.

## S3.evidence_status
Objection: The scenario labels overall evidence_status "Strong — Supported by robust international difference-in-differences and longitudinal reform studies," yet its own Uncertainties section rates confidence as "low" on "the precise causal weight of the postponed tracking itself versus the accompanying teacher reforms in achieving Finland's equity outcomes." The strong rating is being applied to a bundled treatment (tracking + teacher reform) while the scenario's mechanism isolates tracking postponement alone — the cited studies support the bundle, not the isolated causal claim the mechanism asserts.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade evidence_status to "moderate" for the isolated-tracking claim, or reframe the mechanism as "postponing tracking combined with teacher-capacity investment" to match what Hanushek & Woessmann / Pekkarinen et al. actually estimate.

## S4.assumptions
Objection: The assumption "the teacher shortage cannot be mitigated in the short term through wage increases or recruitment alone" is asserted with no evidence tag, yet it is the linchpin justifying why a compensatory (non-structural) package is preferable to structural reform. The implementation_planning expert view (evidence: strong — OECD Education at a Glance; PSZ/PDSZ surveys) documents the shortage's severity but does not establish that wage/recruitment levers are foreclosed — that is a separate policy-lever claim never sourced.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Either tag this assumption as (speculative) and cite specific PSZ/PDSZ salary-elasticity findings, or remove the claim and instead state the weaker, supported point that shortage remediation via wages has not been attempted at the scale required.

# Critique: cost_checker

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The cost list omits the transition-year cost of running two systems in parallel. Under a felmenő-rendszer cap trajectory, already-enrolled 6/8-year cohorts continue for up to 8 years while general-school advanced programmes ramp up simultaneously — so the "medium, formula-based" recurring line understates peak-year expenditure, which spans overlapping obligations to both the shrinking selective track and the growing general-school programmes. This is a transition-vs-steady-state confusion: the steady-state formula cost is medium, but the transition peak is not costed at all.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The teacher-retraining line is tagged "One-off (high)" but national differentiation capacity is not a one-off cost. The mechanism itself states differentiation "substitutes for between-school tracks if teachers are trained for it," and the assumptions require capacity "built at national scale" — sustaining that across teacher turnover (retirements, new entrants) is an ongoing recurring cost, not a one-off. Mislabeling it as one-off understates the steady-state budget and hides the durability risk that S4 explicitly flags for its own recurring lines.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The two recurring lines (targeted funding "high," teacher premia "medium-high") carry no fiscal-cycle sensitivity range, yet the scenario's own political_risks and uncertainties fields identify budget durability as the central threat ("compensation lines are the first cut in fiscal consolidation"; "durability of the budget line" at low confidence). A cost field whose headline items are known to be the most fiscally volatile should express a range or a floor-under-consolidation figure, not point estimates; as written the cost_categories contradict the scenario's own risk assessment.

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: Both operational lines are tagged "low" but the SES-aware admission machinery — constructing and validating a per-applicant SES indicator (required by the assumptions) and running weighted-lottery/district-quota processes with compliance monitoring — is a recurring data-collection and audit cost, not a low administrative add-on. The pilot's own uncertainty field calls for "compliance audit design," which is an unbudgeted recurring line; the "low" recurring tag omits the verification cost that the scenario's own compliance-risk logic demands.

# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The steps rely on a "ministerial decree governing secondary admissions" enforced via Tankerületek audits, but Tankerületek only have jurisdiction over state schools. The equity_impact section itself states the reform "runs a high risk of middle-class bypass through non-state schools unless non-state admissions are strictly regulated," yet no implementation step gives the Ministry or Oktatási Hivatal legal authority or lead-time to bind church/foundation maintainer admissions — that would require separate legislation or contract renegotiation (as S2 recognizes is necessary and costly). As written, the mechanism can only be enforced on the subset of schools least likely to drive the sorting problem, making the stated goal infeasible with the listed steps alone. (speculative on exact bypass magnitude, but the legal gap itself is not speculative — decrees cannot override maintainer autonomy without a separate statutory basis.)

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: "Klebelsberg Centre — Oversee the transitional staffing plans as secondary teaching hours shift toward 4-year tracks" assumes teachers currently assigned to 6-/8-year academic tracks can be redeployed into 4-year gimnazium or general-school posts as capacity shrinks. This assumes subject/qualification fungibility that the scenario set elsewhere explicitly denies: S4's evidence_status names "severe regional teacher shortages" as the binding constraint on any non-structural reform, and S3's assumptions concede the general-school track "lacks the equivalent teacher quality and support systems." No feasibility check (teacher qualification mapping, retraining lead time, or attrition risk during the multi-year felmenő rendszer) is included, so the "smooth administrative transition" claimed under expected_benefits is asserted, not supported by the implementation_steps as specified.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: "Ministry of Education — Launch a nationwide teacher retraining program focused on differentiated instruction" is listed as a single, undated action item running in parallel with the National Assembly's "comprehensive ban on selective tracks before grade 9." No sequencing or lead-time estimate is given to ensure retraining completes before the legal tracking ban takes effect nationwide. Given the assumption block's own admission that comprehensive-track teacher quality is currently inadequate, a legally mandated structural change that outruns retraining capacity is a documented feasibility failure, not a hypothetical one — the scenario provides no administrative-bandwidth estimate (e.g., number of teachers requiring retraining, current in-service training throughput) to show the retraining step is achievable on the same timeline as the legal reform.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: "Teacher retention and relocation incentive schemes for marginalized regions" appears only as a cost line, with no corresponding action in implementation

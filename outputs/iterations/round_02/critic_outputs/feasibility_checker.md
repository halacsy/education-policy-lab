# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "teacher capacity for differentiated instruction can be built at national scale within the transition window" is the load-bearing element of the entire scenario, yet the implementation_steps allocate it a single line ("launch differentiation retraining at scale") with no capacity arithmetic. Differentiated instruction competence is precisely the skill Hungarian teachers are documented to lack at scale; retraining the entire lower-secondary teaching workforce is a multi-year throughput problem constrained by teacher-educator supply, release time, and an already-aging, shortage-hit profession. The scenario treats as an assumption what is in fact the binding feasibility constraint, and provides no evidence that the transition window (a decade) can absorb the required teacher-training throughput. This is unsupported, not merely uncertain.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "launch the general-school advanced-programme fund" and the assumption that general schools "can absorb and challenge high-achievers if funded for it" require additional qualified teaching and programme-design capacity in general schools precisely as they receive more high-achievers — but the plan specifies only a funding formula, no teacher-supply mechanism. Money does not create advanced-course teachers where none are posted. Given the synthesis's own "strong"-tagged finding that teacher supply binds any structural variant, S2's retention mechanism (its one unevidenced link, per its own evidence_status) depends on staffing capacity the implementation_steps never source.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "introduce premia and mentoring pipelines for disadvantaged schools" assumes teacher premia can redistribute experienced teachers toward disadvantaged schools, but the plan sequences premia introduction without accounting for the administrative lead time and the fixed national pool of experienced teachers. In a shortage labour market, premia move existing teachers between schools rather than adding supply — meaning gains in targeted schools come at the expense of others, a zero-sum reshuffle the implementation_steps neither acknowledge nor budget the bandwidth to manage. (The uncertainties section flags premium *size* but not the fixed-pool constraint.)

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The step "design and pilot the new assessment in volunteer districts" followed by "amend admission decree" understates legal lead time: replacing a central written admission examination with a new instrument plus SES-weighted rules requires decree drafting, consultation, and a legally mandated advance-notice period before an admission cycle so families can rely on stable rules. The steps present pilot→legislate→run-first-cycle as if sequential within reach, but do not reserve the statutory notice lead time between decree amendment and the first affected cohort's application — a real administrative-calendar constraint that could add a full cycle before "cycle 1" data exists.

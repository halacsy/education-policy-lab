# Expert analysis: portuguese_reform

## Findings (evidence)
- Portugal improved PISA results 2006-2015 without changing selection age, via curriculum standards, school clustering, targeted support (TEIP) and retention reduction. [evidence: moderate — OECD PISA trend reports; Crato 2020]

## Interpretation
Portugal demonstrates a non-structural improvement path: standards, targeted support and retention reduction moved outcomes without touching selection age.

## Assumptions
- Portuguese gains generalise beyond its starting point (low baseline). [assumption]

## Position
A non-structural package is a viable alternative or complement to structural reform.

## Uncertainties
- Whether non-structural packages can offset a selection system as early as Hungary's. (confidence: low; evidence that would reduce it: pilot evaluation in Hungarian districts.)

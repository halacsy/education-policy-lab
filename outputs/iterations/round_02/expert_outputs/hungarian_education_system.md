## Interpretation

The 6/8-year gimnazium system functions as a visible, bureaucratically explicit sorting mechanism that operates at a developmentally early stage (age 10–12) and produces measurable inequality in access. However, it is nested within a larger architecture of school choice, catchment variation, and fragmented maintainership that sustains between-school stratification independently. Removing the track system alone treats a symptom; the underlying sorting engines—catchment design, choice asymmetries, and maintainer diversity—remain active.

Any reform proposal must account for the fact that structural change across tankerulet governance, private/foundation sectors, and catchment rules is administratively complex and politically contested.

# rejected_framings.md

## S1 — Admission reform within the current structure

- CHOSEN: Reduce SES concentration at entry to 6/8-year gimnazium tracks by reforming admission criteria (SES-proportional quotas, validity-improved assessment) while preserving the existing institutional and legal structure; targets the bureaucratically explicit sorting mechanism but leaves choice and catchment effects active.

- REJECTED: "Admission reform can eliminate stratification without structural change" — reason: The mechanism explicitly acknowledges that "abolition or reform of selective intake without simultaneous reform of catchment rules and school choice regimes would likely preserve or displace rather than eliminate stratification [evidence: moderate]". This stronger framing would misrepresent the scenario's own caution and upgrade a contested claim to certainty.

- REJECTED: "Admission reform is cost-neutral relative to structural alternatives" — reason: The cost categories are acknowledged as likely underestimated ("scope likely underestimated given fragmented maintainer governance"), and the per-pupil funding claim carries only weak evidence. Framing this as cost-neutral would violate evidence discipline.

- REJECTED: "Church and foundation maintainers will cooperate with a common admission framework" — reason: This is already flagged in uncertainties (confidence: medium) and implementation feasibility is explicitly constrained. A chosen framing cannot presuppose the resolution of a flagged political-feasibility uncertainty.

## S2 — Gradual phase-down of 6/8-year entry places

- CHOSEN: Progressively reduce the number of 6/8-year gimnazium entry places over a multi-year trajectory (minimum 6–8 years), shifting selection later and deferring differentiation for a growing share of the cohort, while respecting statutory transitional guarantees; residual stratification persists on the retained share during phase-down.

- REJECTED: "Staged reform is lower-conflict than abrupt abolition" — reason: The assumption explicitly states this is "untested and may instead merely extend the period of conflict [assumption]". The political-risk section identifies "Sustained parent mobilisation over the multi-year window, with staged reform potentially extending rather than reducing the conflict period." A chosen framing cannot presuppose resolution of an unresolved assumption-uncertainty tension.

- REJECTED: "Phase-down guarantees reversal prevention" — reason: The political-risk section explicitly states "No design feature currently raises the cost to a successor government of freezing the cap trajectory; reversal risk is identified but not mitigated in this scenario's design". Framing phase-down as conferring reversal protection would misrepresent the scenario's own acknowledgement of this gap.

- REJECTED: "Teacher retraining can be front-loaded during the 2025–2035 retirement window at known cost" — reason: The assumption and uncertainties both flag that "no published cost/timeline estimate exists". The implementation steps note this requires "costing before commitment". A chosen framing cannot presuppose prior costing as complete.

## S3 — Comprehensive school to age 14 (structural reform)

- CHOSEN: Abolish early academic selection by establishing common comprehensive schooling to age 14, deferring between-school selection and coupled with teacher and support investment; directly removes the age-10–12 gatekeeper but gains are contingent on bundled complementary investment, and between-school sorting via choice/catchment persists unless reformed in parallel.

- REJECTED: "Comprehensive schooling will reduce the Hungarian SES-score gradient from 19–25% to 12–14% OECD average" — reason: While the elevated Hungarian gradient is strongly evidenced, "the reduction attributable to this reform is not directly evidenced for Hungary [evidence: moderate]". The chosen mechanism attributes equity gains to structure, but the evidence status explicitly warns that "the magnitude of gains for Hungary specifically and the isolable effect of structure vs. bundled supports remain unevidenced domestically." This framing upgrades moderate to strong.

- REJECTED: "Political ownership will survive two electoral cycles" — reason: The assumptions section flags: "this contradicts the political_feasibility position that no durable supermajority/consensus window is assured; the tension is unresolved." A chosen framing cannot presuppose resolution of a documented assumption-evidence contradiction.

- REJECTED: "Finnish equity gains isolate the causal effect of comprehensive structure" — reason: The evidence status explicitly states "Finnish equity gains under comprehensive schooling are entangled with teacher policy and support systems, so comprehensive structure alone is not a transparent cause of equity [evidence: moderate]". The chosen mechanism must preserve this causal ambiguity rather than attributing gains to structure alone.

- REJECTED: "Comprehensive reform guarantees cost recovery within 5–10 years" — reason: The cost categories note transition costs "exceed steady-state savings by a factor of 3–5 over 5–10 years", but "Teacher recruitment to offset the below-OECD staffing gap and ageing workforce (significant ongoing cost — magnitude not quantified in curated sources)". A chosen framing cannot presuppose cost boundaries when magnitudes are explicitly unquantified.

## S4 — Keep the structure, compensate general schools (Portuguese-style package)

- CHOSEN: Retain the 6/8-year gimnazium structure but raise outcomes and reduce equity costs by investing in non-selected schools via a Portuguese-style package (curriculum standardisation, targeted support TEIP-style, retention reduction); aims to raise the floor for non-selected pupils and leaves the early-selection sorting mechanism intact, so mitigates rather than reverses stratification.

- REJECTED: "Compensation can offset the equity costs of early selection" — reason: The assumptions section states: "this is doing the central work in S4 yet no country evidence exists at Hungary's selection age, so it is unverifiable ex ante [assumption]". A chosen framing cannot foreclose the central empirical uncertainty flagged in assumptions; the mechanism must remain conditional on this unverified premise.

- REJECTED: "Portugal's TEIP model transfers directly to Hungary" — reason: While Portugal's aggregate gains are strongly evidenced, the evidence status notes "transferability to Hungary at its earlier selection age... are weakly evidenced or untested". The assumptions also note Hungary's "higher intergenerational persistence may require stronger dosing than Portugal's higher-equity baseline." A chosen framing cannot presuppose transferability without flagging the context-difference caveat.

- REJECTED: "This option preserves low political conflict" — reason: The political-risk section identifies "the stakeholder coalition to fund a 5-year targeted programme when selection is nominally preserved is unidentified [evidence: weak]". Framing this as low-conflict presupposes coalition-building that the scenario itself flags as unresolved.

- REJECTED: "Early selection does not prevent absolute gains in non-selected cohorts if support intensity increases" — reason: While this appears in the assumptions, the evidence status notes "No randomised trial directly compares delayed selection versus early selection + targeted support in Central European contexts [evidence: weak]". A chosen framing cannot presuppose the direction of the absolute-gains comparison when the evidence is weak and context-specific data are absent.

## Cross-scenario framing discipline

**Preserved uncertainties and confidence calibrations:**

All four scenarios preserve every uncertainty from input; no scenario's chosen framing resolves or downgrades an uncertainty to certainty. Each scenario's confidence levels (low/medium/high) and reduction pathways reflect the input evidence discipline:

- S1: 4 uncertainties; all low or low/medium
- S2: 4 uncertainties; all low or medium/low  
- S3: 5 uncertainties; all low or low/medium
- S4: 4 uncertainties; all low or low/medium

**Evidence-status fidelity:**

No chosen framing upgrades an evidence status from its source:
- S1 mechanism: "contested" evidence remains contested in chosen framing
- S2 equity impact: "moderate" evidence remains stated conditionally ("residual stratification persists")
- S3 expected benefits: "moderate" evidence on Poland/Finnish cases is preserved; Hungarian effect size remains unevidenced
- S4 equity impact: "weak" evidence on transferability is preserved; compensation remains conditional

**Assumption-uncertainty tensions preserved:**

Where episodic memory flagged unresolved contradictions, the chosen framings preserve them as live tensions:
- S2: staged reform assumption vs. conflict-extension uncertainty — both survive
- S3: political-ownership assumption vs. political-feasibility evidence — both survive  
- S4: compensation assumption vs. lack of Hungary-age evidence — both survive

No chosen framing resolves these; they remain flagged for stakeholder attention.

# Critique: equity_checker

## S1.equity_impact
Objection: The scenario acknowledges that middle-SES or rural pupils without qualifying SES-proxy status could face reduced access if quotas displace them, but it does not offer any mechanism or safeguard to mitigate this potential negative equity impact. This omission makes the equity impact potentially negative for a significant group.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add a mechanism to mitigate displacement effects for middle-SES or rural pupils, such as a 'hold harmless' clause for a certain percentage of non-SES-qualified students or a phased implementation with monitoring to adjust quotas if displacement is significant.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The scenario states that residual selective places are likely to become more SES-skewed and lower-SES pupils lose access without compensating investment. This is a significant negative equity outcome that the scenario does not adequately address with a concrete compensatory mechanism within the scenario itself.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Integrate a concrete compensatory investment plan for general schools and lower-SES pupils who lose access to selective seats, such as dedicated funding for enrichment programs or additional support staff, directly into the scenario's mechanisms or implementation steps.

## S3.equity_impact
Objection: The scenario's largest identified risk is that sorting relocates into school choice, non-state maintainer expansion, catchment-area effects, and private tutoring markets, potentially neutralizing any equity gain. The scenario acknowledges this but does not propose concrete, proactive measures to prevent or significantly mitigate this migration, making the net equity gain highly uncertain.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add specific mechanisms to counter the migration of sorting, such as strict regulations on non-state maintainer expansion, policies to reduce school choice segregation (e.g., controlled choice models), or significant investment in public comprehensive schools to make them universally attractive.

## S4.equity_impact
Objection: The scenario explicitly states it could be 'equity-neutral or even equity-negative net of its entrenchment effect on the status quo' due to legitimizing early selection and the risk of widening low-SES high-performer gaps. This is a critical flaw for a scenario aiming for equity, and the proposed compensatory measures are not robust enough to counter this fundamental risk.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Strengthen the compensatory measures significantly, perhaps by linking them to a clear, measurable target for reducing the SES-achievement gap, and include a sunset clause for the early selection mechanism if these targets are not met within a defined timeframe, to prevent indefinite entrenchment of an inequitable system.

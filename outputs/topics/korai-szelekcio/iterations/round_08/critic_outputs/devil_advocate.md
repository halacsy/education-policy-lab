# Critique: devil_advocate

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes that uniform admission-equity rules can be applied across state, church, and foundation maintainers, despite expert findings that maintainer plurality is a structural complication. This assumption is directly contradicted by the 'political_risks' section which states 'Non-state maintainers may resist uniform equity mandates; maintainer fragmentation is treated in the expert record as a structural complication, not merely a difficult-but-solvable administrative matter, so resistance risk is at least moderate rather than weak.'
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Revise the assumption to acknowledge the high likelihood of non-state maintainer resistance and propose concrete mechanisms (e.g., strong incentives, legal frameworks) to address this structural challenge, or explicitly state that the scenario's success is contingent on overcoming this significant hurdle.

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes that church and foundation maintainers can be regulated or incentivized not to expand selective places to offset the state-sector reduction. However, the 'political_feasibility' minority position in the synthesis explicitly states this restriction assumption is 'not credible' given their growing share and independent veto-player status.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Acknowledge the high likelihood of non-state maintainer expansion and revise the scenario to either incorporate strategies to genuinely prevent this or explicitly state that the scenario's equity goals are likely to be blunted by this offsetting behavior.

## S3.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes that sorting does not simply migrate to school choice, non-state maintainers, catchment effects, or private tutoring markets to the point of neutralizing the equity gain. This is directly contradicted by the scenario's own 'equity_impact', 'political_risks', and 'uncertainties' fields, which identify exactly this migration as the largest risk and a low-confidence uncertainty.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Reframe this as a critical uncertainty or a major risk that requires explicit mitigation strategies within the mechanism, rather than an assumption that can be made. The scenario should detail how it plans to counteract or monitor this migration effectively.

## S4.assumptions
Objection: The scenario assumes that non-structural reform can operate independently of decisions about selection age and intensity, despite the 'mechanism' field noting that early selection may offset some benefits of compensatory support. This creates an internal tension, as the scenario aims to improve outcomes without claiming to neutralize inequities caused by early selection, yet the Portuguese evidence is from a system without early selection.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Clarify how the proposed non-structural reforms are expected to function effectively within a system that retains early selection, or explicitly acknowledge that the benefits will be significantly bounded by the continued existence of early selection, potentially leading to equity-neutral or negative outcomes as noted in 'equity_impact'.

# Critique: political_risk_checker

## S1.political_risks
Objection: The scenario acknowledges that a subsequent government could reverse the mechanism within a single electoral cycle due to implementation by ministerial decree, but it does not propose any specific entrenchment design to mitigate this high reversal risk.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a suggested revision to include statutory safeguards, independent monitoring triggers, or budget-lock mechanisms to entrench the reform beyond a single electoral cycle.

## S2.political_risks
Objection: The scenario states that reversal requires legislative repeal, offering more durability than S1, but it fails to acknowledge that a parliamentary majority change during the multi-year rollout still poses a significant reversal risk, especially given the acknowledged lobbying by urban middle-class families and school maintainers.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Acknowledge the vulnerability to parliamentary majority change and suggest exploring mechanisms to build broader political consensus or statutory safeguards that transcend a single legislative term.

## S3.political_risks
Objection: The scenario identifies a substantial reversal risk, citing the Polish precedent, and notes that the only entrenchment device (a cross-party political pact) is not specified with statutory safeguards, independent monitoring triggers, or budget-lock mechanisms. This makes the proposed entrenchment device weak and potentially ineffective.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Strengthen the entrenchment design by specifying concrete statutory safeguards, independent monitoring triggers, and budget-lock mechanisms for the cross-party political pact.

## S4.political_risks
Objection: The scenario states that targeted TEIP-style funding lines are likely first-line cuts during fiscal consolidation, making the central benefit-delivery mechanism budget-vulnerable. This significantly undermines the claim of the package being 'politically less disruptive' and presents a high risk of the policy failing to deliver its intended benefits.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Propose a mechanism to secure the funding for TEIP-style programs, such as a statutory funding floor with explicit budget protection, or explore alternative funding sources that are less vulnerable to fiscal consolidation.

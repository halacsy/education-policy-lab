# Critique: political_risk_checker

## S1.political_risks
Objection: The listed risks (middle-class parent resistance, church litigation) omit the more fundamental reversal vulnerability embedded in the mechanism/implementation_steps: the reform is implemented via "ministerial decree," not primary legislation. A decree can be rescinded unilaterally by a future minister with no legislative process required, unlike the Nkt. amendments proposed in S2/S3. This is a structural entrenchment failure, not just an opposition-intensity issue, and it is not mentioned at all.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a political-risk entry addressing decree-level fragility, and consider requiring codification of equity quotas via Nkt. amendment or a similarly durable legal instrument to raise the reversal cost for a future government.

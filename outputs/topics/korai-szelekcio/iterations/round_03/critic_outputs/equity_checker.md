# Critique: equity_checker

## S1.equity_impact
Objection: The equity_impact claim treats "equity" as purely socio-economic sorting within selective tracks, but ignores who is structurally excluded from the analysis entirely: students in settlements with no non-state alternative (i.e., poor rural/Roma communities), for whom "displacement to unregulated non-state schools" is not even an option. The field asserts a "moderate improvement" but the improvement is calculated only for the subset of families capable of exercising non-state exit — the students least likely to benefit from any regulation (rural, low-income, no access to church/foundation schools within travel distance) are not mentioned as beneficiaries or non-beneficiaries at all. The hungarian_education_system evidence tag supports sorting dynamics among urban school-choosing families, not the distributional effect on excluded rural cohorts.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add an explicit equity breakdown distinguishing (a) urban families with non-state exit options, (b) rural families without such options, and (c) any available data on ethnic/Roma segregation, rather than treating "students" as a homogeneous group with uniform exit capacity.

## S2.equity_impact
Objection: The claim "Significantly improves equity in the long run" is stated as the headline conclusion, but it is explicitly conditioned on "non-state sectors do not absorb the phased-out capacity" — an assumption the scenario's own uncertainties section rates at (confidence: low). A conclusion resting on a low-confidence assumption cannot be labeled "significant" improvement; at best it is a speculative upside bounded by an unresolved displacement risk that the scenario itself flags as high political risk ("middle-class flight to private or shadow education providers"). This is an internal contradiction: the equity_impact field asserts confidently what the uncertainties field says is unknown.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the equity_impact claim to "conditionally moderate, contingent on non-state absorption remaining limited (currently low-confidence)" and cross-reference the uncertainty entry directly.

## S3.equity_impact
Objection: "Maximizes educational equity" is asserted without acknowledging a distributional trade-off documented elsewhere in the same scenario: forced rural school consolidation (mentioned in cost_categories as "expanded student transportation budgets" and in mechanism as preventing "collapse of rural primary school networks"). Consolidation itself can be equity-negative for rural/low-income students who face longer commutes, school closures in their immediate community, and loss of local schooling — effects documented in the demography/school_network_planning literature but not weighed against the segregation-reduction benefit here. The scenario asserts equity is "maximized" for socio-economic gradient in test scores, while ignoring a distinct equity dimension (rural access) that this same reform's implementation could worsen.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Reframe equity_impact to note the reform improves SES-gradient equity in test outcomes but may introduce a separate access-equity risk for rural students via consolidation, and specify which sub-population bears each effect.

## S4.equity_impact
Objection: The claim "improves equity for students in targeted disadvantaged schools" assumes TEIP-style targeting can accurately identify and reach Hungary's disadvantaged population, but Hungary's disadvantaged/Roma population is heavily rural and dispersed (per demography/school_network_planning findings elsewhere in the record), unlike Portugal's more geographically concentrated TEIP target areas. The evidence tag (Crato 2020, OECD PISA trend reports) supports Portuguese national-level gains, not that the targeting mechanism transfers to Hungary's settlement pattern — this transferability gap is flagged only as an assumption, not reflected in the equity_impact conclusion, which states the benefit as though targeting effectiveness were already established.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Qualify equity_impact to state that gains depend on unverified transferability of TEIP-style targeting to Hungary's dispersed rural disadvantaged population, and flag this as the central equity risk rather than treating "targeted schools benefit" as a settled outcome.

# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The stated teacher-retraining and reallocation timeline (year 2-9) and sequencing assumes the teacher workforce can be expanded/retrained within that window, but this contradicts the scenario's own workforce evidence: ~50% of teachers are age 50+, only 7.2% are under 30, acute regional/STEM shortages are 2-3x worse, and CPD bandwidth is already committed to NAT and AI reforms. The plan provides no quantified recruitment/training pipeline, ignores multi-year certification/salary-negotiation lead times, and omits measurable staffing milestones — making the rollout timeline implausibly optimistic and risking degraded instruction during transition.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Before legislating phase-out timing, commission a teacher-capacity audit and supply model that (a) quantifies current regional/subject vacancies and retirements, (b) maps realistic recruitment/training throughput (pre-service + in-service retraining) over 1-,3-,5-,9-year horizons, and (c) produces staffing milestones tied to legal phase-in (e.g., minimum % of newly certified lower-secondary teachers per region by cohort year). Use that model to (i) stagger phase-in regionally where shortages exist, (ii) allocate dedicated, ring-fenced retraining budgets and recruitment incentives (salary top-ups, relocation grants) sequenced explicitly with the 2024–2031 salary reform, and (iii) include contingency triggers that pause cohort advance in regions failing to meet staffing milestones.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The implementation timeline and steps underestimate legal lead times and maintainer negotiation complexity for enforcing SES-aware quotas and uniform thresholds across state, church, and foundation maintainers. The scenario itself notes maintainer plurality and partial autonomy and cites felmenő-rendszer phase-in and at least ~1-year statutory lead time, yet the plan compresses design, legislation/decree, piloting, and national rollout into a 1–7 year window without (a) a legal mapping of church/foundation autonomy clauses, (b) negotiated memoranda of understanding with church/foundation federations, or (c) a funded enforcement capacity build for Oktatási Hivatal. Given Tisza coalition commitments protecting church-maintainer operating conditions and the documented litigation/consultation risks, this sequence is likely to be delayed or legally blocked.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Explicitly add a pre-legislation phase (6–12 months) to perform a maintainer-legal mapping and stakeholder negotiation round: (1) commission legal analysis of maintainers' autonomy and precedents for binding admission rules; (2) secure signed MOUs or negotiated concessions with major church/foundation federations before drafting national mandates; (3) cost and allocate dedicated enforcement staff and IT/data-reporting requirements for Oktatási Hivatal (including sanctions and fiscal-conditional levers), and (4) re-sequence pilot only after MOUs and enforcement capacity exist. Extend realistic national rollout to a 6–9 year horizon if maintainers do not sign pre-commitments, and document litigation contingencies and timeline buffers in the plan.

## S2.implementation_steps
Objection: The plan asks the Klebelsberg Center to formalize minimum-score thresholds in year 1-2 and the Ministry to design levers to discourage offsetting expansion by church/foundation maintainers in year 2-4, but it omits a prior legal and political feasibility analysis of whether funding-conditionality or licensing levers are legally available and politically executable given the coalition commitment to preserve church operating conditions. The scenario's own evidence records church/foundation expansion (10.4%→27%) and Tisza program constraints; without pre-validated legal instruments and a defined monitoring/enforcement unit, state contraction will very likely be offset by non-state expansion, nullifying intended effects.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Precede threshold formalization with a mandatory legal-policy impact assessment and maintainer-response simulation: (a) map all available levers (normatíva adjustments, licensing, building-transfer rules) and test them against concordat/contract clauses; (b) run a behavioural simulation using recent maintainer expansion data to project likely absorptive capacity of church/foundation actors under state contraction; (c) condition threshold adoption on at least one implementable lever (e.g., funding-conditional licensing) that has been legally cleared and politically negotiated with coalition partners; and (d) create a staffed, budgeted monitoring cell in Oktatási Hivatal with required data-sharing agreements and pre-defined sanctions so that threshold-driven contraction cannot be immediately arbitraged by non-state expansion. If legal/political clearance cannot be secured, delay threshold rollout and adopt a regional pilot tied to compensatory general-school investment funded in the same legislative package.

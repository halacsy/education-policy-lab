### FINDING 2 — The education minister has publicly committed to REVIEWING (not abolishing) the 6/8-year gimnázium
Minister Lannert Judit (education researcher turned minister), in her first ministerial interview (Balzac student media, June 2026), said competency-measurement data does not show 6/8-year gimnázium students develop faster; they "start from a higher level because they were selected," so the system's only function is that "the elite selects itself and secures its position in society." She framed it as a societal-policy rather than pedagogical question, called for broad social consultation including young people, and — critically — explicitly stated she is initiating **review, not abolition**, and wants current gimnáziums to keep their position. She also argued the selection system is increasingly pointless demographically ("they're lassoing children"), as declining child numbers undercut the admission exam's original rationing function.

### FINDING 5 — External EU pressure now aligns with the reform direction (durability tailwind)
The European Commission's 3 June 2026 country report (2026 European Semester spring package) states Hungary's education system still amplifies existing social/economic inequalities, that between-school segregation of students by social background is **the highest in the EU**, that measures to reduce Roma segregation are insufficiently targeted and poorly implemented, and that church/private-school growth reinforces segregation by attracting more-advantaged students in disadvantaged areas. This gives the reform an external anchor (EU funds conditionality) that raises the political cost of reversal.

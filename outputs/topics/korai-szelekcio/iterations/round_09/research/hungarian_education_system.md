## Research Notes — Early Selection & the 6/8-year Gimnázium (Round 9)

**1. Peer-effect / "value-added" evidence for 6/8-year gimnázium (2026).** Economist Dániel Horn (Corvinus University) reports that students in six- and eight-year gimnázium tracks show roughly 10% greater competency-test growth than similarly-abled, similarly-backgrounded peers in four-year gimnáziums, attributing this to peer composition, teacher quality, and pedagogical practice — while still criticizing the selective system's broader role. Only about 8% of a given cohort attends 6/8-year gimnázium tracks nationally.
Source: telex.hu/G7 podcast, July 2026 — https://telex.hu/g7/podcast/2026/07/04/dirik-podcast-horn-daniel-hatosztalyos-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumok
Source (summary): eduline.hu, July 2026 — https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260710_elitgimnazium-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-szelekcio-iskolarendszer-kompetenciameres-transzparencia

**2. Historical SES-selection baseline.** Horn's TARKI paper on "kisgimnáziumok" (early-selective gimnáziums) finds SES-index effects on selection into eight-grade gimnázium are substantial in relative terms even though only 2–3% of students attended eight-grade gimnázium at the time of that dataset (OKM 2008 panel/cross-section).
Source: tarki.hu — https://tarki.hu/tarsadalomkutatas/publikaciok/tpubl_a_927.pdf

**3. Parental-education sorting (Andor 2003, still cited).** Andor's 2003 study found a link between parental educational attainment and choice of school type: children of more educated parents are over-represented in six- and eight-year gimnáziums; in 2002, 15% of gimnázium students attended six-year and 8% eight-year programmes.
Source: Pedagógiai Folyóiratok / Új Pedagógiai Szemle — https://folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu/uj-pedagogiai-szemle/a-gimnaziumi-felveteli-vizsga-eredmenyenek-es-a-gimnaziumi-tanulas

**4. PISA 2022 equity gap.** In Hungary, socio-economically advantaged students (top 25% SES) outperformed disadvantaged students (bottom 25%) by 121 score points in mathematics, versus an OECD average gap of 93 points; this gap remained stable between 2012 and 2022.
Source: OECD PISA 2022 Country Note: Hungary — https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/pisa-2022-results-volume-i-and-ii-country-notes_ed6fbcc5-en/hungary_3df2ae68-en.html

**5. Segregation ranking (EU-wide).** The EU's Education and Training Monitor 2025 states Hungary has the highest level of socio-economic segregation of students into different schools in the EU, and this was also the highest in the EU in PISA 2022; the performance gap between children of graduate vs non-graduate parents is 53 points in Hungary vs an EU average of 33 points.
Source: European Commission, Education and Training Monitor 2025 — Hungary — https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/hungary.html

**6. SES explains more variance in Hungary than OECD average.** A 2026 MDPI study on resilient/vulnerable school profiles notes that PISA 2022 found socio-economic status accounted for 25% of the variation in mathematics performance in Hungary, compared with an OECD average of 15%.
Source: MDPI, "How Rare and How Stable? Resilient and Vulnerable School Profiles in Hungary" — https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7102/16/7/1131

**7. Family "admission strategy" burden.** A CEU-based qualitative comparative study (2019–2021, five CEE countries) on families navigating 6/8-year gimnázium admissions finds that talent and good grades alone are typically insufficient; most children need specialised preparation requiring real sacrifice from families, and admission conflicts are described as symptomatic of deeper social problems.
Source: ResearchGate / CEU Democracy Institute study — https://www.researchgate.net/publication/368720550_Csaladi_strategiak_a_hat-_es_nyolcosztalyos_gimnaziumokba_iranyulo_felveteli_folyamatban

**8. Segregation mechanism at the admissions stage.** Reporting on academic research into the admissions funnel found that students from disadvantaged-composition primary schools have almost no realistic chance of even attempting the 6/8-year gimnázium entrance exam, let alone passing it.
Source: index.hu, 2019 (cites underlying study) — https://index.hu/techtud/2019/12/05/hatosztalyos_nyolcosztalyos_gimnaziumi_felveteli_oktatas_szegregacio/

**9. Central written exam as the dominant gatekeeper (2026 admissions cycle).** For the 2026/27 admission cycle, roughly 8 out of 10 gimnáziums require the nationally standardized central written exam (Hungarian + math, 45 min each, max 50 pts each) as a precondition for admission; by ministerial decree the central exam must count for at least 50% of the total admission score, oral exams may count for at most 25%, and school grades ("hozott pontok") for up to 50%; ~40,000+ 4th/6th/8th graders sat the exam on 24 January 2026.
Source: Pénzcentrum, Nov 2025 — https://www.penzcentrum.hu/oktatas/20251121/igy-zajlik-a-kozponti-felveteli-jelentkezes-2026-ban-itt-vannak-a-gimnazium-felveteli-feladatok-a-korabbi-evekbol-1188471
Source: Eduktor felvételi hírek, 2025/26 — https://eduktor.hu/felveteli-hirek

**10. Confessional-maintainer admission filter beyond academic merit.** Church-maintained gimnáziums (e.g., a Székesfehérvár diocese school, the Piarista network) explicitly condition admission on acceptance of/commitment to a religious worldview, assessed via interview (pass/fail, not scored numerically) and pastoral recommendation letters, layered on top of the central exam and school-grade points.
Source: Czuczor Gergely Bencés Gimnázium admissions page — https://www.czuczoriskola.hu/gimnazium/jelentkezes-es-felvetel-gimnazium
Source: Váci Piarista Gimnázium admissions page — http://vac.piarista.hu/felveteli-beiskolaztatas

**Additional context (procedural/structural, 2024/25 Eurydice data):** Hungary has maintained a dense secondary-school network — 880 schools nationally as of 2024/25 — explicitly designed so that "every student can find a secondary general school close to their home," reachable by public transport in under an hour; six- and eight-year secondary schools have existed since 1991, with entry at grade 5 (eight-year) or grade 7 (six-year).
Source: Eurydice, "Organisation of general secondary education — Hungary" — https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/organisation-general-secondary-education

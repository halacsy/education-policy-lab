# Research Notes — school_network_planning (Round 9)

**Finding 1 — Network density & commuting design.** According to 2024/25 KSH-based Eurydice data, secondary education is provided at 880 schools across Hungary, designed so that "the aim is for the school to be accessible by public transport in under an hour," with larger cities almost always having a secondary school and many smaller towns too. Source: https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/organisation-general-secondary-education

**Finding 2 — Dormitory (kollégium) slack.** The same source notes dormitories "can accommodate most applicants, as there are far more places than applicants," and that demand is in decline — meaning the boarding-based safety valve for thin rural markets currently has spare capacity. Source: https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/organisation-general-secondary-education

**Finding 3 — Settlements with zero public school.** A 2025 spatial-clustering study using 2021–2024 settlement-level KIR data found that "by 2024, more than 1,200 of 3,155 settlements had no public educational institution," with school closures and teacher shortages disproportionately affecting peripheral regions. Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396483025_Shrinking_growing_or_overburdened_Territorial_patterns_in_child_demographics_and_institutional_burden_in_Hungary's_public_education_system_after_2021

**Finding 4 — Accelerating birth decline shrinks future cohorts.** KSH data reported by IntelliNews show Hungary recorded "only 5,979 children born" in October 2025 (down 10% y/y), pushing the population "below 9.5mn for the first time since 1952," with the fertility rate falling to 1.31 in the first ten months of 2025, down from 1.39 in 2024. Source: https://www.intellinews.com/hungary-s-demographic-decline-deepens-in-2025-413979/

**Finding 5 — Long-run population trajectory.** Long-run projections cited by OSW indicate Hungary's population "could fall to 8.5 million by 2050," a 13% decrease over three decades, meaning the school-age population base for network planning will keep shrinking for the entire policy-relevant horizon. Source: https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2025-04-14/hungarys-ongoing-demographic-decline-increase-birth-rates-only

**Finding 6 — Selection is spatially concentrated, not uniform.** Reporting on Oktatási Hivatal/national-competence-measurement data found that "a magyar általános iskoláknak több, mint az egyharmadából sokszor senki nem jelentkezik sem nyolc-, sem hatosztályos gimnáziumba," and a related Index piece put this at roughly 40% of general schools nationally (60% among rural/village schools), while in other schools — mostly in county seats and Budapest — 20-30% of a grade-6 class leaves for gimnázium. Sources: https://privatbankar.hu/cikkek/makro/kivaltsagosok-kerulnek-be-a-kozepiskolakba-.html ; https://index.hu/techtud/2019/12/05/hatosztalyos_nyolcosztalyos_gimnaziumi_felveteli_oktatas_szegregacio/

**Finding 7 — Effect on the "sending" general school.** Citing Horn et al. (2018), qubit.hu reports that when the best pupils leave for a 6-year gimnázium track, the effect on classmates who stay behind is on average mildly negative on grade-8 outcomes, though "ez a hatás inkább vegyes: a maradó jó tanulók valamennyit veszítenek emiatt, míg a rosszabb tanulók akár nyerhetnek is vele," and researcher Eszter Berényi confirms the outflow creates organizational problems because schools cannot plan class sizes or staffing needs in advance. Source: https://qubit.hu/2022/06/15/olyan-gyerek-akinek-kozmunkasok-a-szulei-hat-olyan-itt-nincs

**Finding 8 — Maintainer shift interacts with network shrinkage.** Citing "A közoktatás indikátorrendszere 2023," qubit.hu reports that church-school enrollment share grew fastest in small rural towns and villages between 2010 and 2022 (2.5x and "több mint 5-szörös" respectively), and that the share of pupils living in settlements with no remaining state-run primary school rose "1-ről 4 százalékra" over the same period — meaning in some villages the only school left is the one the church took over. Source: https://qubit.hu/2024/05/16/dramai-kepet-fest-a-magyar-iskolakrol-es-a-tanulok-teljesitmenyerol-a-kozoktatas-helyzetet-bemutato-tanulmanykotet

**Finding 9 — Class-size/tuition-year data point.** KSH preliminary 2024/25 data show gimnázium enrollment at 192,000 students (44.8% of secondary students) nationally, with general-school (általános iskolai) enrollment continuing to fall while secondary and tertiary enrollment rose — consistent with a shrinking base feeding into the 10-12 selection point. Source: https://www.ksh.hu/s/kiadvanyok/oktatasi-adatok-2024-2025-elozetes-adatok/index.html

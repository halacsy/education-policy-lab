# Expert Output — legal_and_governance (Round 9)

## Research notes (prose, with full URLs)

**1. Statutory basis for 6/8-year gimnázium (Nkt. §11).** The Nkt. (2011. évi CXC. törvény) explicitly allows gimnázium to operate with 4, 6 or 8 grades, but conditions the 6/8-year track on a "tehetséggondozás" (talent-nurturing) justification requiring elevated-level standards set by separate regulation — meaning selection age is not incidental but a distinct legal category with its own justification test. https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.tv

**2. Secondary phase generally starts at grade 9 — 6/8-year gimnázium is the codified exception.** Nkt. §4 defines the "középfokú nevelés-oktatás szakasza" as beginning at grade 9 "except for the 6- and 8-year gimnázium," confirming that early selection is a statutory carve-out from the general structural principle, not the default. https://njt.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00.0

**3. Current admission volumes (2025/26 cycle, published May 2026).** Nationally 89,541 students took part in the general secondary admission procedure; 76,198 applied to grade 9, 8,071 to 6-year gimnázium (+11% year-on-year, "mintegy ezerrel több tanuló"), and 5,272 to 8-year gimnázium (down more than 14% y-o-y). https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260516_kozepiskolai-felveteli-2026-hatosztalyos-gimnazium-bekerules

**4. Equity evidence, current EU monitoring.** The EU's 2025 Education and Training Monitor for Hungary states Hungary has the highest level of socio-economic segregation of students into different schools in the EU, based on 2022 PISA data. https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/hungary.html

**5. PISA-OECD equity baseline.** OECD's PISA 2022 Volume I finds the performance gap linked to socio-economic status is widest in Romania and Slovakia, "followed by Hungary, Israel and Chinese Taipei," and that disadvantaged students are far more likely not to reach basic proficiency. https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/pisa-2022-results-volume-i_53f23881-en/full-report/the-state-of-learning-and-equity-in-education-in-2022_e65c570e.html

**6. Legal-certainty doctrine on felmenő rendszer.** Hungary's ombudsman (Alapvető Jogok Biztosa) has stated in a formal position that in education, legislative changes are customarily phased in on a "felmenő rendszer" (grade-by-grade, grandfathered) basis because this "jogbiztonsági garanciaként szolgál" (serves as a rule-of-law/legal-certainty guarantee), citing Constitutional Court doctrine requiring adequate preparation time before a norm takes effect. https://atlatszo.hu/oktatas/2020/04/14/visszamenoleges-torvenykezes-es-egyre-szigorodo-feltetelek-nehezitik-az-egyetemistak-eletet/

**7. Judicial precedent applying felmenő logic to desegregation.** A Hungarian court ruling on Roma school segregation ordered the "jogsértő állapot felmenő rendszerű megszüntetését" (grade-by-grade elimination of the unlawful situation), obligating a desegregating class-organization practice — showing felmenő rendszer used as a remedial/implementation mechanism, not only a protective one. https://nemzetisegijogok.hu/documents/2657648/2659479/Jelent%C3%A9s+a+nemzeti+k%C3%B6znevel%C3%A9si+t%C3%B6rv%C3%A9ny+m%C3%B3dos%C3%ADt%C3%A1s%C3%A1r%C3%B3l+-+6010_2014.pdf/2ba750a7-4fed-65bb-d861-eee66fc09158

**8. Speed-of-change precedent from a different but structurally analogous domain (maintainer plurality can be legally reshuffled fast).** In June–July 2026, Hungary amended the Fundamental Law (Alaptörvény 16. módosítás) and passed implementing legislation (2026. évi XVIII. törvény) to dissolve the "kekva" foundation-maintainer model for public institutions; non-higher-education foundations must be wound up by 31 July 2026 and dissolved by court by 31 August 2026, with university-maintaining foundations given a longer, one-year transition to 1 August 2027 "to keep higher-education institutions' operation predictable and undisturbed." This shows that (a) maintainer structures — directly relevant to the maintainer-plurality dimension around gimnázium selection — can be legally altered within weeks once constitutional will exists, and (b) even the government's own justification for the longer runway for universities is continuity/predictability, an implicit endorsement of the felmenő-rendszer logic when the stakes for enrolled students/staff are high. https://kormany.hu/hirek/megkezdodott-a-kozerdeku-vagyonkezelo-alapitvanyok-atalakitasa and https://telex.hu/belfold/2026/05/21/kekva-alapitvanyi-egyetemek-fenntartas-fenntartovaltas-oktatasi-miniszterium

**9. Amendment lead-time practice for Nkt. structural provisions.** Historical amendments to Nkt. §4 (definition of the secondary phase) were enacted in 2019 (2019. évi CXII. törvény) but only entered into force from 1 January 2020, illustrating the normal ~6–12 month minimum lead time Hungarian legislative practice applies even to non-controversial structural changes in this law. https://net.jogtar.hu/getpdf?docid=a1100190.tv&targetdate=&printTitle=2011.+%C3%A9vi+CXC.+t%C3%B6rv%C3%A9ny

**10. Admission-procedure legal detail (annual ministerial regulation, not just Nkt.).** The 2025/26 admission calendar (published by the Oktatási Hivatal/rokusi.hu) shows the 6/8-year gimnázium admissions are governed by a detailed annually-issued procedural timetable (EMMI rendelet references, central written exam dates, deadline cascades) — meaning any reform of the selection age would require coordinated change across the Nkt., a government/ministerial decree, and the annual admission-order regulation, not a single legal instrument. https://www.rokusi.hu/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/kozepfokufelvetelieljaras2025-26.pdf

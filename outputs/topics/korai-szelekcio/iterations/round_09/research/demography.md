## Research Notes — Demography & Early Selection (Round 9)

**1. Fertility collapse is accelerating, not stabilizing.** Hungary's TFR fell to 1.31 in the first ten months of 2025, down from 1.39 in the equivalent period of 2024 and from 1.62 four years earlier; births in the first ten months totaled 60,304, 7.1% fewer than a year earlier, pushing the population below 9.5 million for the first time since 1952. Source: bne IntelliNews, "Hungary's demographic decline deepens in 2025" — https://www.intellinews.com/hungary-s-demographic-decline-deepens-in-2025-413979/

**2. Long-range population projections converge on continued decline through 2040+.** Experts cited in the same piece warn the population could fall to 8.5 million by 2040 and around 6 million by 2070 if current trends continue. A separate OSW analysis projects an 8.5 million population by 2050 under a trend-based scenario. Sources: https://www.intellinews.com/hungary-s-demographic-decline-deepens-in-2025-413979/ ; OSW Centre for Eastern Studies — https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2025-04-14/hungarys-ongoing-demographic-decline-increase-birth-rates-only

**3. Primary-school cohort shrinkage is already measurable and accelerating.** KSH data (via Népszava/Economx) show 710,000 students in daytime general (primary) schools in 2024/25 vs. 712,000 in 2023/24 (−0.3%); first-graders fell 1.9% to 95,000; and compared with 2005/06 (859,000 students) the 20-year decline reached 17.3%. Sources: https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh ; https://www.economx.hu/magyar-gazdasag/2025/07/15/altalanos-iskola-ksh-gyermek-diak-kozoktatas-psz-totyik-tamas-osztaly-812691/

**4. The school network is already contracting under demographic pressure.** The number of active primary-school sites ("feladatellátási hely") fell to 3,537 in 2024/25 — 13 fewer than the prior year, 38 fewer than 2022/23, and 88 fewer than 2021/22. The PSZ teachers' union president (Totyik Tamás) states some schools already receive zero first-grade enrollees and calls consolidation/closure of small schools "almost certainly unavoidable" within 5–10 years. Source: https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**5. Secondary/gimnázium network still largely untouched by the cohort shock (so far), but structurally dense.** Hungary has 880 secondary-general schools (2024/25 KSH data); the network is deliberately dense, designed so a school is reachable by public transport in under an hour. This buffer means demographic pressure on 10-12 year cohorts feeding into the 6/8-year gimnázium is currently absorbed more by primary-level consolidation than secondary restructuring — but that buffer is time-limited as smaller cohorts reach age 10-12. Source: Eurydice — https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/organisation-general-secondary-education

**6. Selection is unusually early and unusually strong by European standards — this is the structural precondition the demographic squeeze interacts with.** A CEU Center for Policy Studies working paper finds Hungary's between-school variation in PISA science performance exceeds that of most European countries that track as early as age 10-11, including Austria, Germany, Lithuania and the Netherlands, despite Hungary's income inequality being moderate — pointing to school-level/institutional selection mechanisms rather than pure income segregation as the driver. Source: https://cps.ceu.edu/sites/cps.ceu.edu/files/attachment/publication/3258/cps-working-paper-educ-social-selection-education-hungary-2020.pdf

**7. A live, current policy fight is directly reopening the selection/network question.** Hungary's education minister, Lannert Judit, has recently proposed (per Origo, ~2 weeks old) tying church-school public funding to accepting Roma and non-Roma pupils together, alongside floating school closures/consolidations due to falling child numbers, especially in rural areas, and shorter lesson periods. This is the first concrete ministerial linkage in recent reporting between demographic decline, network rationalization, and selection/segregation policy. Source: https://www.origo.hu/belpol/2026/07/lannert-judit-katolikus-gimnazium-egyhazi-iskola-jovo

**8. Equity trend is negative, not static.** A 2024 systematic review of 20 years of PISA equity data places Hungary among countries with negative trends in outcome equality (alongside France, Netherlands, Czechia, Slovakia), while segregation levels themselves have not measurably changed over the period. Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666374024000414

**9. Public investment intensity in education is falling even as system must absorb structural change.** OECD Education at a Glance 2025 shows Hungary's education spending as a share of the public budget fell from 6.7% to 5.8% between 2015 and 2022, even though per-student expenditure rose (USD 7,352 → USD 9,177) — meaning any network reform (mergers, 6/8-year gimnázium restructuring) would occur against a backdrop of relatively declining fiscal prioritization. Source: https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/education-at-a-glance-2025_1a3543e2-en/hungary_9b7c3dd1-en.html

**10. Intergenerational mobility gap remains extreme, framing the political stakes of any selection reform.** 71% of 25-34-year-olds with a tertiary-educated parent attain tertiary education themselves, vs. only 6% of those whose parents lacked upper-secondary completion — a 65-point gap, one of the largest in the OECD. Source: https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/education-at-a-glance-2025_1a3543e2-en/hungary_9b7c3dd1-en.html

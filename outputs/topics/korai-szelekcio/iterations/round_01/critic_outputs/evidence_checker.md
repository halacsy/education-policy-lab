# Critique: evidence_checker

## S1.mechanism
Objection: The claim that "quota systems for disadvantaged pupils reduces SES-based sorting" is tagged [evidence: moderate] but sourced only as "Model knowledge / Hungarian educational policy literature" — this source is not in the curated registry at all. No registry entry (school_choice, gimn_share, tracking_inequality) actually tests whether admissions quotas at the point of gimnazium entry reduce sorting; school_choice [moderate] instead shows sorting is already entrenched at the primary stage, which cuts against the mechanism working. The "moderate" grade overstates an unsupported causal claim that should be flagged as model speculation, not registry-backed evidence.

## S2.mechanism
Objection: "Gradually reducing enrollment caps... limits early academic selection" is tagged [evidence: strong — Model knowledge / Hungarian Public Education Act], but the only strong registry entry touching this (legal [strong]) supports merely that the 6/8-year tracks are legally anchored and phase-out requires legislation — it says nothing about the causal/behavioral claim that cap reduction "limits early selection" in practice (e.g., whether demand shifts to remaining slots, private schools, or oral-exam bypass per S2's own uncertainties). Tagging this as "strong" misattributes the legal registry's strength to an untested behavioral mechanism.

## S3.evidence_status
Objection: The scenario declares overall evidence "strong," blending Hanushek & Woessmann 2006 [strong, cross-country DiD on tracking age generally] with Jakubowski et al. 2016 [moderate, Poland-specific] into a single "strong" rating. The registry only grades the Polish case as moderate, and neither source is Hungary-specific; the "strong" overall label thus overclaims transferability to Hungary's institutional context, which the scenario's own assumptions section admits is uncertain ("Polish and Finnish... applicable to Hungarian context" is listed as an assumption, not established fact). Evidence_status should be split, not merged upward.

## S4.expected_benefits
Objection: "Improves student performance in disadvantaged areas without triggering middle-class resistance or political backlash [evidence: moderate]" conflates two distinct claims under one tag. The portugal_improvement registry entry [moderate] supports the performance-improvement part (curriculum standards, TEIP, clustering), but it says nothing about Hungarian middle-class political reaction — that half of the claim has no registry support at all and is pure domestic-political inference (parent_attachment [moderate] is about attachment to gimnazium slots, not about reaction to a compensation package that leaves those slots untouched). Tagging the political-quiescence claim at the same "moderate" level as the performance claim overstates its evidentiary basis.

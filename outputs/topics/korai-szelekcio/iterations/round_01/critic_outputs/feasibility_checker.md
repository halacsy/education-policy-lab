# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The step tasking the Ministry with "a nationwide teacher retraining program" to run the comprehensive lower-secondary model is infeasible given the shortage that the synthesis itself certifies as "sustained... particularly concentrated in mathematics, science, and disadvantaged regions" [evidence: strong — OECD Education at a Glance; PSZ/PDSZ surveys]. Retraining requires pulling currently-teaching staff out of classrooms or adding substitute capacity that does not exist in an already short-staffed system; the mechanism names no source of substitute/relief teachers, no phasing to avoid simultaneous classroom coverage gaps, and no minimum lead time. This is not a design detail but the central feasibility constraint the scenario claims to have accounted for (cost_categories mentions "major teacher retraining" only as a cost line, not a capacity plan).

## S2.assumptions
Objection: The assumption that "a gradual phase-out (felmenő rendszer) satisfies the constitutional principles of legal certainty and acquired rights" is asserted without specifying the duration required to actually meet that bar. The synthesis's own legal_and_governance minority position states, with strong evidence (Nkt. analysis; Constitutional Court practice), that a transition of "at least eight years" is needed to avoid violating acquired rights. S2's mechanism and implementation_steps only commit to "a multi-year period" — an unspecified duration that could easily fall short of the eight-year benchmark the legal evidence actually supports, making the assumption unsupported rather than merely uncertain.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: The step assigning the Klebelsberg Centre to "implement school clustering and coordinate targeted resource allocation" presumes administrative bandwidth that the scenario's own evidence_status explicitly disclaims: "the capacity of the centralized Kleibelsberg Centre to implement flexible, targeted programs is highly uncertain." No implementation step or cost_category addresses building this capacity (e.g., new regional staff, data infrastructure, monitoring units) before the Centre is handed a TEIP-style targeting mandate. The mechanism therefore assumes the administrative bandwidth as given rather than treating it as the scenario's own most-flagged risk.

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: "Monitoring and enforcement compliance costs for tankerületek" is listed as a line item, but the synthesis independently establishes that tankerületek will simultaneously be absorbing demographically-driven "school-network consolidation in the 2020s and 2030s" [evidence: strong — KSH demographic yearbooks] and operating amid the same teacher shortage. The cost category names no incremental staffing or budget to cover this added enforcement function on top of existing consolidation work, so the implementation_steps' reliance on tankerületek for compliance monitoring is not resourced against their real, concurrently-documented workload. (speculative: without a stated budget figure it is unclear whether this is underfunded or merely unspecified, but the omission itself is a concrete gap.)

# Expert analysis: polish_reform
## Findings (evidence)
- Poland's 1999 education reform postponed student tracking to age 15 by creating a comprehensive lower secondary tier (gimnazjum). [evidence: moderate — Jakubowski et al. 2016; OECD]
- Following the 1999 reform, Polish PISA scores rose substantially by 2012, with quantitative analyses attributing a significant portion of these gains to the delayed selection. [evidence: moderate — Jakubowski et al. 2016; OECD]
- The 1999 delayed-tracking reform was entirely reversed in 2016-2019 due to political reasons, restoring the earlier tracking structure. [evidence: moderate — OECD]
- Early tracking at ages 10 (8-year gimnazium) and 12 (6-year gimnazium) remains a core structural feature of the Hungarian secondary education system. [evidence: strong — model knowledge]

## Interpretation
Poland's experience demonstrates that delaying academic tracking can yield significant improvements in student outcomes across the system, particularly for lower-performing students who benefit from longer exposure to a comprehensive curriculum. However, the subsequent reversal in 2016-2019 provides a crucial political lesson: structural educational reforms that lack broad, long-term consensus among teachers, parents, and local authorities are highly vulnerable to political polarization and policy reversal. For Hungary, this means any attempt to dismantle the popular 6- and 8-year gimnazium system will face intense middle-class resistance, requiring a phased transition that builds broad stakeholder buy-in rather than a top-down mandate.

## Assumptions
- Educational outcomes measured by standardized tests like PISA are representative of overall school system quality. [assumption]
- The political dynamics and middle-class preferences for early tracking in Hungary are comparable to those that drove the reversal in Poland. [assumption]

## Position
Hungary should phase out the 6- and 8-year gimnazium system to delay academic selection until age 15, but must bundle this structural shift with teacher salary increases and local school autonomy to prevent a Polish-style political reversal.

## Uncertainties
- The precise extent to which the post-2016 Polish reversal has degraded student performance, as long-term post-reversal PISA data is still emerging.
- The exact proportion of Hungarian students currently enrolled in the 6- and 8-year gimnazium tracks compared to the standard 4-year track.

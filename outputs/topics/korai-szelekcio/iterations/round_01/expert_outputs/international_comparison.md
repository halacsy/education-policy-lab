# Expert analysis: international_comparison

## Findings (evidence)
- Cross-country difference-in-differences evidence indicates that earlier between-school tracking increases the inequality of educational outcomes without raising mean performance [evidence: strong — Hanushek & Woessmann 2006, Economic Journal].
- The share of reading/maths score variance explained by student socio-economic status (SES) in PISA is among the highest in the OECD for Hungary, ranging between ~19-25% across cycles compared to the OECD average of ~12-14% [evidence: strong — OECD PISA 2012-2022 country notes].
- Early academic selection at ages 10 (8-grade gimnazium) and 12 (6-grade gimnazium) is a structural driver that segregates Hungarian pupils by socio-economic background earlier than the standard age 14 transition [evidence: moderate — Model knowledge / Hungarian educational policy literature].

## Interpretation
The international empirical consensus suggests that early school tracking serves as a sorting mechanism rather than an academic accelerant. In Hungary, the 6- and 8-year gimnazium tracks allow advantaged families to hoard educational opportunities early, bypass the municipal primary school system, and concentrate resources. This structural sorting directly contributes to Hungary's exceptionally high PISA socio-economic gradients by separating students into distinct academic and vocational trajectories before basic skills are fully consolidated. 

## Assumptions
- PISA socio-economic status gradients accurately reflect systemic inequities in the acquisition of foundational cognitive skills [assumption].
- The cross-country findings on early tracking are structurally applicable to the Hungarian institutional context despite specific national labor market differences [assumption].

## Position
Hungary should phase out early selection into 6- and 8-year grammar schools (gimnazium) and delay formal school tracking until at least age 14 to mitigate socio-economic segregation and reduce the high variance in outcomes explained by student background.

## Uncertainties
- The precise extent to which the 6- and 8-year tracks act as a "safety valve" for middle-class flight, and whether their elimination would accelerate sorting into private or church-run schools [uncertainty].
- The exact quantitative reduction in the Hungarian PISA gradient that would result from delaying tracking, given confounding factors like geographical segregation and funding disparities [uncertainty].

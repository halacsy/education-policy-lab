# Research Notes — Implementation Planning Domain (Sequencing, Teacher Supply, Administrative Bandwidth, Timelines)

**1. Teacher workforce is aging sharply, constraining any capacity-based reform of the sorting system.** According to the latest national data (KSH, 2025), on full-time employed teachers, the number of teachers in the younger age group (30–44 years) has declined, and as a result, half of all teachers in the 2024/2025 academic year were 50 or older, while only 7.2% of teachers were under 30. This matters directly for sequencing: any structural alternative that requires redeploying or retraining subject teachers for a later-selection model faces a workforce that is retiring faster than it is being replenished.
Source: Education and Training Monitor 2025 — Hungary, European Commission. https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/hungary.html

**2. Teacher salary reform is mid-implementation on a multi-year EU-funded timeline, which structures the realistic window for any parallel administrative reform.** Backed by EUR 1.8 billion from EU funds between 2024 and 2031 (~HUF 735 billion), the plan represents an average increase of more than HUF 5 million (EUR 12,000) per teacher over the period; at the start of 2025 the second step took effect, raising average teacher pay to 80% of the average graduate wage. This is a load-bearing timeline: administrative bandwidth for restructuring selection is competing with an already-launched, EU-monitored salary/performance-evaluation rollout through 2031.
Source: "Hungary raises teachers' wages with EU support," European Social Fund Plus. https://european-social-fund-plus.ec.europa.eu/en/projects/hungary-raises-teachers-wages-eu-support

**3. A new performance-evaluation bureaucracy for teachers was just launched and is already being rolled back for administrative-burden reasons — a direct signal of limited administrative bandwidth.** In June 2026 the education minister announced they would suspend the performance-evaluation upload obligation for the current school year and undertake a full review of the evaluation system, stating the system "túl sok adminisztrációval jár, rengeteg energiát köt le az iskolákban, miközben nem ad valódi szakmai visszajelzést" (involves too much administration, absorbs a lot of energy in schools, while not giving real professional feedback to teachers). This is directly relevant: any new selection/sorting reform would land on top of an administration already straining under a barely-year-old evaluation regime.
Source: Telex, "Lannert Judit: Az adófizetők forintjaiból nem fogunk két iskolát fenntartani egy iskolányi gyerek számára," June 9, 2026. https://telex.hu/belfold/2026/06/09/lannert-judit-oktatas-atakitas-reform-elkepzeles

**4. Capacity in the 6/8-year track has been essentially frozen for years despite demand, meaning any expansion or contraction scenario starts from a rigid base.** 25,962 students studied in six- or eight-grade gimnázium in 2024/2025, roughly unchanged from previous years, according to KSH data, despite enormous demand for "kisgimnázium" places — in one top school 369 applicants competed for 38 spots. This structural rigidity (flat supply for a decade-plus) is a key sequencing constraint: neither expanding access nor phasing it out can be done quickly without a multi-year capital/staffing plan for either building out or repurposing these tracks.
Source: Eduline, "Hiába sokszoros a túljelentkezés, évek óta gyakorlatilag ugyanannyi a férőhely," Feb 18, 2026. https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260216_hatosztalyos-nyolcosztalyos-gimnazium-tuljelentkezes-ferohely-elitkepzes-szegregacio

**5. Demand-side signals are already shifting, altering the political urgency/timeline calculus.** In the 2026 admissions round, 89,541 students took part overall; 8,071 applied to six-grade gimnázium (an 11% rise) while applications to eight-grade gimnázium fell more than 14%, and the ministry itself now flags that "demográfiai szempontból nézve egyre céltalanabbá válik a jelenlegi felvételi rendszer" (from a demographic perspective the current admissions system is becoming increasingly pointless). Falling cohort sizes mean the argument that any structural fix must be delayed for capacity reasons is weakening on its own, independent of policy choice — this changes the realistic timeline for phase-out/redesign options.
Source: Eduline, "Középiskolai felvételi 2026: idén jóval több diák szeretett volna hatosztályos gimnáziumba bekerülni," May 15, 2026. https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260516_kozepiskolai-felveteli-2026-hatosztalyos-gimnazium-bekerules; Portfolio.hu, June 11, 2026. https://www.portfolio.hu/gazdasag/20260611/lannert-judit-ujra-megszolalt-a-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumokkal-kapcsolatban-842766

**6. A live, unresolved political controversy over reforming selection began in June 2026, giving a concrete, current test case for political-risk sequencing.** The new education minister stated on record that the six/eight-grade gimnázium system's main function is that "az elit kiszelektálja magát és biztosítsa a pozícióját a társadalomban" (the elite selects itself and secures its position in society), prompting the Fidesz parliamentary leader to publicly accuse her of pushing a "communist-era" unified four-grade model; the minister then explicitly walked this back, meeting church-school maintainers and stating "nincs szó sem az egyházi fenntartású iskolák, sem a hat- és nyolcosztályos intézmények megszüntetéséről" (there is no question of abolishing either church-maintained schools or six/eight-grade institutions). This shows the realistic political ceiling on any near-term structural reform: even a sitting minister sympathetic to change immediately retreated to "review and social consultation," not abolition, within days of political pushback.
Source: Telex, June 6, 2026, https://telex.hu/belfold/2026/06/06/lannert-judit-oktatasi-miniszter-interju-vorosmarty-mihaly-gimnazium-balzac; WMN, June 8, 2026, https://wmn.hu/wmn-szuro/67460-Lannert-Judit-hat-nyolc-osztalyos-gimi-onszelektalas-Gulyas-Gergely-tarsadalmi-egyeztetes; Portfolio.hu, June 11, 2026, https://www.portfolio.hu/gazdasag/20260611/lannert-judit-ujra-megszolalt-a-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumokkal-kapcsolatban-842766

**7. The minister's own stated process is explicitly consultative and long-horizon, not a fast administrative fix.** She said the topic "megéri azt, hogy társadalmi egyeztetést tartsanak róla, és hogy a fiatalokat bevonják ebbe" (is worth holding a social consultation about, and involving young people in it), framing the six/eight-grade question as "inkább társadalompolitikai, mint oktatási kérdés, amit meg fognak vizsgálni" (more a social-policy than an education question, which they will examine) — i.e., no legislative timetable has been set, only a review process.
Source: Kontroll.hu, June 6, 2026. https://kontroll.hu/cikk/belfold/2026/06/06/lannert-judit-a-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumoknak-csak-egy-ertelme-van

**8. Instruction-time and salary-cost linkages constrain any scenario requiring more teaching hours (e.g., extending primary school to grade 6 instead of grade 4/8).** In Hungary, students receive 666 hours of compulsory instruction per year in primary education and 796 hours in lower secondary education, below the OECD average of 804 and 909 hours respectively; the amount of compulsory instruction time affects teacher salary costs as it influences the number of teachers needed. Any structural reform that lengthens the common lower-secondary phase (a proposal actively floated in Hungarian commentary) has direct, quantifiable staffing-cost implications given already-low instructional hours and an aging, shrinking teacher pool.
Source: OECD, "Education at a Glance 2025: Hungary." https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/education-at-a-glance-2025_1a3543e2-en/hungary_9b7c3dd1-en.html

**9. Existing admissions administration is already decentralized to individual schools for the 6/8-year track, a relevant baseline for any centralization/decentralization reform option.** If a student applies to a grade lower than grade 9 of secondary school (i.e., a 6- or 8-grade gimnázium), the application forms may be initiated independently by the applicant or parent without the involvement of the primary school, and secondary schools themselves determine the criteria by which they admit applicants, publishing this information on their own websites. This decentralized, school-level admissions authority is a structural feature any reform (e.g., moving to a Dutch-style teacher-recommendation model) would need to reallocate administratively — a nontrivial bandwidth question given thousands of individual school offices currently running their own procedures.
Source: Oktatási Hivatal / Eduline, "Rendkívüli felvételi eljárás," May 15, 2026, https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260515_rendkivuli-felveteli-eljaras-szabad-ferohelyek-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumok; Pénzcentrum, Nov 21, 2025, https://www.penzcentrum.hu/oktatas/20251121/igy-zajlik-a-kozponti-felveteli-jelentkezes-2026-ban-itt-vannak-a-gimnazium-felveteli-feladatok-a-korabbi-evekbol-1188471

**10. A 2019 study (cited in current commentary) quantifies the private preparation burden that any admissions-redesign must reckon with administratively.** Research by Berényi Eszter (2019) found that parents preparing children for eight- and six-grade gimnázium admissions invested on average 3.5 hours per week and 34,000 forints per month in preparation — evidence that the current централ exam functions partly as a proxy for family investment capacity, a factor any redesigned, more "school-readiness-based" or teacher-recommendation-based system would need to explicitly counteract to avoid simply relocating the same sorting mechanism.
Source: Telex, "Nem a hatosztályossal van a baj, hanem az ötödik osztállyal," June 8, 2026. https://telex.hu/velemeny/2026/06/08/hat-nyolcosztalyos-gimnazium-altalanos-iskola-felso-tagozat-atalakitas-halacsy-peter

# RESEARCH NOTES — legal_and_governance domain (Round 10)

## Finding 1 — Statutory basis of the 6/8-year gimnázium (Nkt.)
The 6- and 8-year gimnázium tracks are anchored in the Nemzeti köznevelési törvény (2011. évi CXC. törvény, Nkt.). Compulsory schooling can be fulfilled "az általános iskolában és az ötödik évfolyamtól kezdődően a gimnáziumban" — i.e. from grade 5 (8-year) upward. The Act guarantees free school choice ("free school choice for all students") and, critically, system permeability: §... states the school system is permeable and a student may transfer to another school or school-type even mid-year ("Az iskolarendszer átjárható... akár tanév közben is át lehet lépni"). Evidence: strong. Sources: https://njt.jog.gov.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00 ; https://mkogy.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.TV ; https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/organisation-general-secondary-education

## Finding 2 — Legal definition of "felmenő rendszer" (ascending system)
The Nkt. §4 interpretive provisions define "felmenő rendszer" as the gradual phasing-in of curricular and examination requirements, whereby a change may be introduced starting only at the lowest affected school year ("a tanulmányi és vizsgakövetelmények bevezetésének fokozatos ütemezése, amelynek értelmében a változást az érintett legalacsonyabb iskolai évfolyamon kezdve lehet bevezetni"). This is the binding legal constraint on the *speed* of any structural reform: abolishing/restricting 6/8-year tracks lawfully means no cohort already enrolled can be forced out — a full phase-out takes up to 8 years. The National Core Curriculum is itself reviewed every 5 years and amended in an ascending system. Evidence: strong. Sources: https://mkogy.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.TV (§4 pt 7) ; https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/teaching-and-learning-general-secondary-education

## Finding 3 — Admissions procedure is centrally regulated (20/2012 EMMI + Onytv.)
The central written exam (magyar + matematika) and the mid-level admissions procedure are regulated by 20/2012. (VIII. 31.) EMMI rendelet and the oktatási nyilvántartásról szóló 2018. évi LXXXIX. törvény (Onytv.). Any gimnázium requiring the central written exam must accept results regardless of where taken; only schools requiring the written exam may also hold an oral exam. Schools may set their own admission requirements and organise entrance exams (Nkt. §50). For 6/8-year applications, the parent/pupil may initiate the application independently, without the primary school's involvement. This means much of the selection machinery can be altered by *ministerial decree*, not primary legislation — a faster legal route than amending the Nkt. Evidence: strong. Sources: https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1200020.emm ; https://www.oktatas.hu/kozneveles/kozepfoku_felveteli_eljaras/tajekoztato_felvetelizoknek/tajekoztato_6_8_evf

## Finding 4 — Historical precedent: "quality requirements + downgrade to 4-year" mechanism already existed
An older MKM rendelet (11/1994. VI. 8.) required the maintainer of a 6- or 8-year gimnázium to also organise 4-year instruction under specified conditions (e.g. insufficient gimnázium places in the 30-minute catchment). Nahalka's 2010 blog records that minister Hoffmann Rózsa planned quality requirements for szerkezetváltó gimnáziumok, with schools failing them after a grace period being "downgraded" to 4-year. This shows a legally-tested lever exists: conditioning the track on quality standards rather than outright abolition. Evidence: moderate (one measure is old regulation; the "downgrade plan" is a contemporaneous blog account of a ministerial statement). Sources: https://njt.hu/jogszabaly/1994-11-20-67 ; http://nahalkaistvan.blogspot.com/2010/07/gimnaziumi-felvetelik-indulat-nelkul.html

## Finding 5 — Maintainer plurality: state (tankerület), church, foundation, nationality self-government
KSH data: in 2019/20, tankerületi központok operated 31% of gimnázium feladatellátási helyek (266) with 52% of students (112.8k); churches ran 30% (255 sites) with ~25% of gimnázium students (55k); 8 gimnáziums under national nationality self-governments held 0.9% (1.9k). Sector-neutral per-capita ("normatíva finances students, not institutions, regardless of maintainer") funding is a constitutional principle historically. Evidence: strong (KSH). Sources: https://www.ksh.hu/docs/hun/xftp/idoszaki/oktat/oktatas1920/index.html ; https://www.epa.hu/01500/01551/00033/pdf/879.pdf

## Finding 6 — Rapid growth of the church-maintained secondary sector
Church-maintained share of secondary institutions rose from 10.4% (2010) to 25.2% (2020); by 2022 nearly 30% of secondary students attended a church institution. Foundation-maintained gimnázium enrolment grew ~20% in one period (to 16k); church gimnázium enrolment +14% over five years. Private (non-state/non-church) share fell after 2011. Relevance: any reform touching 6/8-year tracks must legally engage church maintainers, who enjoy funding agreements and autonomy — this raises the political-legal cost and slows change. Evidence: strong (KSH + Index reporting citing KSH). Sources: https://index.hu/belfold/2024/03/02/iskola-egyhazi-allami-magan-felveteli-diakok-oktatas/ ; https://www.ksh.hu/docs/hun/xftp/idoszaki/oktat/kozepiskola2021/index.html

## Finding 7 — Current live policy debate (June 2026): minister raised review, not abolition
Minister Lannert Judit (Oktatási és Gyermekügyi Minisztérium) stated in a Balzac student-media interview that the mid-level admissions system tied to 6/8-year gimnáziums produces significant social selection and deserves "társadalmi egyeztetés" (societal consultation); she explicitly denied proposing abolition. Gulyás Gergely characterised it as an abolition proposal / invoked "communism"; Lannert rebutted. Teacher-profession is split: Totyik Tamás (PSZ president) would support phasing the system out; Nagy Erzsébet (PDSZ) prefers reducing segregation + correcting admissions; Horváth Péter (Nemzeti Pedagógus Kar) defends them as real options for motivated pupils, citing sustained oversubscription. Evidence: strong (multiple June 2026 outlets). Sources: https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260608_lannert-judit-gulyas-gergely-vita-gimnaziumok-felveteli-rendszer ; https://magyarnemzet.hu/belfold/2026/06/hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumok-reakcio-lannert-judit-vita ; https://pedagogusok.hu/hirek/van-aki-megszuntetne-mas-megorizne-a-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumokat/

## Finding 8 — Comparative rarity of the simultaneous 4/6/8-year structure
Sociologist Kende Ágnes states the coexistence of 4-, 6- and 8-year admission is a very rare configuration; to her knowledge only the Czech Republic has a close analogue, partly Slovakia (where the 8-year gimnázium is permitted mainly in church maintenance). She locates the Hungarian origin in the post-1989 decentralisation, when schools passed to municipalities and no school-form was prohibited; the 2000 PISA first exposed the resulting selectivity. Evidence: moderate (expert testimony in interview, not a comparative dataset). Source: https://www.valaszonline.hu/2026/06/26/elit-gimnaziumi-igazgatok-szociologus-vita-szelekcio-oktatas/

## Finding 9 — Equity/segregation evidence load-bearing for the legal debate
Berényi Eszter's 2019 research: families preparing children for 6/8-year admission invested on average ~3.5 hours/week and ~HUF 34,000/month (an earlier Index figure cited ~HUF 20,000/month) in preparation; predominantly graduate-parent children get in — described as structural segregation. Telex cites OECD 2019: 85% of Hungarian 15-year-olds attend performance-selective schools vs. OECD average 43%. Roughly every tenth eighth-grader attends gimnázium instead of general school (2020 figure). Evidence: moderate–strong (one academic study, OECD figures via secondary reporting). Sources: https://telex.hu/velemeny/2026/06/08/hat-nyolcosztalyos-gimnazium-altalanos-iskola-felso-tagozat-atalakitas-halacsy-peter ; https://index.hu/techtud/2019/12/05/hatosztalyos_nyolcosztalyos_gimnaziumi_felveteli_oktatas_szegregacio/

## Finding 10 — Maintainer registry / removal powers (governance lever)
Nkt. specifies the kormányhivatal issues operating licences and can order removal from the register for serious professional legal breaches (for non-state/non-municipal maintainers, together with licence withdrawal). This is the formal governance mechanism through which quality-conditioning of tracks (Finding 4) could be enforced. Evidence: strong. Source: http://njt.hu//cgi_bin/njt_doc.cgi?docid=139880.366984&mobile=1 ; https://njt.jog.gov.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00

I'll research current demographic and policy data relevant to early selection and the 6/8-year gimnázium system in Hungary, focusing on cohort decline trends through 2040.
## Research Notes — Demography & Early Selection (Round 10)

**1. Cohort collapse is now the dominant driver of school-network stress, not selection design itself.** Hungary's fertility rate fell below 1.3 in 2025, down from 1.55 in 2023, and births have declined in nearly every month of 2025 versus the already record-low prior year. Source: "Demográfiai apokalipszis – Muszáj hozzányúlni az oktatási rendszerhez," Economx, Oct 7 2025, https://www.economx.hu/magyar-gazdasag/kozoktatas-demografiai-valsag-pedagogusok-szakszervezete-iskola.817275.html

**2. Concrete forward-looking cohort-size numbers exist.** PSZ president Totyik Tamás stated the system is still "calibrated" to annual cohorts of ~110,000 children, but within a few years annual cohorts will fall to about 72,000 — a ~35% drop in cohort size that mechanically shrinks demand for both general and selective secondary tracks, including 6/8-year gimnázium intake. Source: Economx, May 19 2026, https://www.economx.hu/belfold/2026/05/19/robbanas-elotti-allapot-totyik-tamas-psz-demografiai-kozoktatas-gyermekszam-pedagogusok/

**3. Regional divergence is extreme, meaning uniform national selection rules will hit districts very unevenly.** In districts like Bélapátfalva only 55 children were born last year, in Tabi 67, and several Budapest districts (XII, XVI) and Dunakeszi already show over 40% divergence between current birth numbers and first-grade enrollment forecasts. Source: same Economx May 2026 article, https://www.economx.hu/belfold/2026/05/19/robbanas-elotti-allapot-totyik-tamas-psz-demografiai-kozoktatas-gyermekszam-pedagogusok/

**4. Primary-school enrollment has already fallen substantially, with closures accelerating.** Compared with 20 years ago, general-school enrollment is down 17.3%: the 2005/2006 school year had about 149,000 more pupils (859,000) than today; the number of primary-school sites fell to 3,537 in 2024/2025, 13 fewer than the previous year and 88 fewer than in 2021/2022. Source: Népszava/KSH, July 14 2025, https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**5. Kindergarten decline is a leading indicator for the next cohort entering the 10-12 selection point.** Kindergarten enrollment fell from 341,000 in 2011/2012 to about 324,000 in 2024/2025, and preschool closures are already underway, foreshadowing further primary and secondary contraction. Source: Válasz Online, Oct 17 2025, https://www.valaszonline.hu/2025/10/17/ovodak-iskolak-bezarasa-demografia-oktatas/; Népszava, July 14 2025, https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**5b.** The under-1 cohort is already about 17,000 smaller than the 4-year-old cohort — equivalent to 600-800 fewer kindergarten/class groups, i.e., at least "száz kisiskolányi gyerekkel" fewer children nationally. Source: Válasz Online, Oct 17 2025, https://www.valaszonline.hu/2025/10/17/ovodak-iskolak-bezarasa-demografia-oktatas/

**6. Union leadership frames consolidation as unavoidable but politically untouched.** PSZ's Totyik stated that within five to ten years population decline could cause severe strain and that school-network mergers/closures are "szinte biztos" (nearly certain), while noting no political side is currently addressing the issue; some schools already receive zero first-grade enrollments, and this tanév had 80 schools with no first grade and 200 schools with only ~10 first-graders. Source: Economx, July 15 2025, https://www.economx.hu/magyar-gazdasag/kozoktatas-demografiai-valsag-pedagogusok-szakszervezete-iskola.817275.html and https://www.economx.hu/magyar-gazdasag/2025/07/15/altalanos-iskola-ksh-gyermek-diak-kozoktatas-psz-totyik-tamas-osztaly-812691/

**7. Government position resists framing this as closures.** A government commissioner stated the goal is not closure but protecting small settlements' schools "amíg erre van igény" (as long as there is demand), while acknowledging mergers ("összevonásokkal") will be used. Source: Válasz Online, Oct 17 2025, https://www.valaszonline.hu/2025/10/17/ovodak-iskolak-bezarasa-demografia-oktatas/

**8. Selection's equity cost has strong, longstanding empirical support in the Hungarian case, independent of demographic pressure.** Kertesi and Kézdi's research (as synthesized in later literature) established that Hungary's free-choice, early-tracking system allows schools "complete discretion... to hand-pick their students," producing substantial socioeconomic segregation in primary and secondary schooling. Source: "Early tracking and competition – A recipe for major inequalities in Hungary," https://www.academia.edu/126047095/Early_tracking_and_competition_A_recipe_for_major_inequalities_in_Hungary

**9. PISA 2022 confirms Hungary remains an outlier on socioeconomic determinism of outcomes.** In Hungary, socioeconomic status accounted for 25% of variation in mathematics performance versus an OECD average of 15%, per OECD's PISA 2022 report. Source: MDPI, "How Rare and How Stable? Resilient and Vulnerable School Profiles in Hungary," https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7102/16/7/1131

**10. Union counter-narrative: small-school size is not the main driver of underperformance — social status is.** Totyik argued it is a "tévhit" (myth) that closing small schools would reduce underperformance rates, since the correlation between competency-test results and social background is far stronger than between results and "small-school character," even though small rural schools disproportionately enroll disadvantaged pupils. Source: Economx, Oct 7 2025, https://www.economx.hu/magyar-gazdasag/kozoktatas-demografiai-valsag-pedagogusok-szakszervezete-iskola.817275.html

**11. Cost-efficiency threshold data exists for merger economics.** Hungarian school cost-function estimates (Hermann 2005b, cited by OFI) show per-pupil costs fall sharply as enrollment rises toward roughly 200 students, after which economies of scale flatten — meaning consolidation below ~200 pupils yields the largest unit-cost savings, though the aggregate national savings remain uncertain. Source: Oktatáskutató és Fejlesztő Intézet, "A kistelepülések kisiskolái – társulás?", https://ofi.oh.gov.hu/tudastar/onkormanyzat-kozoktatas/kistelepulesek

**12. Current sectoral scale for the 6/8-year track transition point.** In 2024/2025, of students continuing beyond general school, 44.8% (192,000) went into gimnázium (which includes the 6/8-year tracks alongside 4-year), 41.9% (~180,000) into technikum/szakgimnázium, and 13.3% into non-matriculation vocational programs. Source: KSH, "Oktatási adatok, 2024/2025," https://www.ksh.hu/s/kiadvanyok/oktatasi-adatok-2024-2025-elozetes-adatok/index.html

**13. Population-level demographic ceiling reinforces long-run planning need through 2040-2050.** Hungary's population, 9.6 million per the 2022 census (a 3.4% decline, the largest ever recorded between census cycles), is projected under a trend-based scenario to fall to 8.5 million by 2050, a 13% decrease over three decades — directly shrinking the school-age population base that the 6/8-year gimnázium network draws on. Source: OSW Centre for Eastern Studies, April 2025 (updated), https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2025-04-14/hungarys-ongoing-demographic-decline-increase-birth-rates-only

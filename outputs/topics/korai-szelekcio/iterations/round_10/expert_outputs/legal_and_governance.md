# Expert analysis: legal_and_governance

## Findings (evidence)
- The 6- and 8-year gimnázium tracks are anchored in the Nemzeti köznevelési törvény (2011. évi CXC. törvény, Nkt.) and can be discontinued only through primary legislative amendment, with any phase-out constrained by the felmenő rendszer (ascending system) principle: no cohort already enrolled may be forced out, meaning a full discontinuation takes up to 8 years from legislative change. [evidence: strong — Nkt. (Nemzeti köznevelési törvény) statutory analysis; https://njt.jog.gov.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00; https://mkogy.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.TV]
- The admissions procedure—including the central written examination (magyar + matematika)—is regulated by ministerial decree (20/2012. [VIII. 31.] EMMI rendelet) and the Onytv. (2018. évi LXXXIX. törvény). Alterations to admissions rules can therefore be achieved by ministerial decree without amending the Nkt., providing a faster legal route than primary legislation. [evidence: strong — https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1200020.emm; https://www.oktatas.hu/kozneveles/kozepfoku_felveteli_eljaras/]
- Maintainer plurality is constitutionally entrenched: state school district centres (tankerületi központok) operate 52% of gimnázium students (2019/20 data); churches maintain 25%; foundations and nationality self-governments hold a small remainder. Any structural reform affecting 6/8-year tracks must legally engage non-state maintainers, raising political-legal cost and slowing change. [evidence: strong — KSH (Központi Statisztikai Hivatal) oktatási statisztika 2019/20; https://www.ksh.hu/docs/hun/xftp/idoszaki/oktat/oktatas1920/index.html; https://www.epa.hu/01500/01551/00033/pdf/879.pdf]
- Church-maintained secondary share grew from 10.4% (2010) to ~30% (2022), and foundation-maintained gimnázium enrolment increased ~20% in one period. This expansion complicates uniform mandate: church schools enjoy constitutionally-backed funding agreements and autonomy guarantees, making phase-out negotiation politically-costlier. [evidence: strong — https://index.hu/belfold/2024/03/02/iskola-egyhazi-allami-magan-felveteli-diakok-oktatas/; KSH közoktatási adatai]
- The Nkt. guarantees system permeability (átjárhatóság): students may transfer to another school or school-type even mid-year. Free school choice is a distinctive Hungarian feature. These principles are statutory constraints on selection reforms, as any restriction would require explicit re-legislation. [evidence: strong — Nkt. §... analysis; https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/hungary/organisation-general-secondary-education]
- A historical precursor exists: an earlier MKM rendelet (11/1994. VI. 8.) required maintainers of 6- or 8-year gimnáziums to also organise 4-year instruction under specified conditions. Minister Hoffmann Rózsa (2010) planned quality-standard conditionality for 6/8-year tracks, with schools failing quality reviews after a grace period to be downgraded to 4-year operation. This shows a legally-tested mechanism exists short of abolition. [evidence: moderate — https://njt.hu/jogszabaly/1994-11-20-67; http://nahalkaistvan.blogspot.com/2010/07/gimnaziumi-felvetelik-indulat-nelkul.html]
- As of June 2026, minister Lannert Judit stated that the mid-level admissions system tied to 6/8-year tracks produces significant social selection and merits societal consultation ('társadalmi egyeztetés'), while explicitly denying a proposal for abolition. She attributed segregation concerns to the selection system itself, not to the track existence. [evidence: strong — https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260608_lannert-judit-gulyas-gergely-vita-gimnaziumok-felveteli-rendszer; https://magyarnemzet.hu/belfold/2026/06/hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumok-reakcio-lannert-judit-vita]
- Teacher-profession opinion is divided: Totyik Tamás (PSZ, Pedagógusok Szakszervezete president) would support phasing out the system; Nagy Erzsébet (PDSZ) prefers segregation reduction and admissions correction without abolition; Horváth Péter (Nemzeti Pedagógus Kar) defends 6/8-year tracks as genuine options for motivated pupils, citing sustained oversubscription. [evidence: strong — https://pedagogusok.hu/hirek/van-aki-megszuntetne-mas-megorizne-a-hat-es-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumokat/]
- Equity evidence: Berényi Eszter (2019) found families preparing children for 6/8-year admission invested ~3.5 hours/week and ~HUF 34,000/month in private preparation; predominantly graduate-parent children gained entry. OECD 2019 reports 85% of Hungarian 15-year-olds attend performance-selective schools vs. 43% OECD average. Roughly 10% of eighth-graders attend gimnázium instead of general school (2020). [evidence: moderate — https://index.hu/techtud/2019/12/05/hatosztalyos_nyolcosztalyos_gimnaziumi_felveteli_oktatas_szegregacio/; https://telex.hu/velemeny/2026/06/08/]
- The Nkt. empowers kormányhivatalok (county government offices) to issue operating licences and to order removal from the maintainer register for serious professional-legal breaches. This is the formal governance mechanism by which quality-conditioning of 6/8-year tracks (as precedent suggests) could be enforced without abolition. [evidence: strong — http://njt.hu//cgi_bin/njt_doc.cgi?docid=139880.366984&mobile=1; https://njt.jog.gov.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00]

## Interpretation
The 6/8-year gimnázium is a constitutionally embedded feature with strong statutory anchoring, a two-tier maintainer structure (state + church-dominated), and a permeability guarantee that constrains abolition by 8 years (felmenő rendszer). However, admissions rules—the proximate mechanism of selection—are ministerial-decree territory, allowing faster technical reform. Current (June 2026) debate frames the problem as selection-mechanism segregation, not track-existence. A quality-conditioning pathway exists (historical precedent: downgrade-to-4-year); enforcement machinery is in place (körmeghívatal register powers). Equity evidence is moderate-strong (OECD, Berényi); teacher opinion is divided (abolish vs. restrict vs. defend). Legal feasibility of any structural change remains contested because maintainer autonomy and funding-agreement guarantees (especially church) create a political veto point requiring multi-party consent.

## Assumptions
- That the 'felmenő rendszer' principle applies identically to track-discontinuation as to curriculum-phase-in (i.e. that no cohort already admitted can be expelled), even though the statutory text does not explicitly rule-out back-end forced-transfer clauses. [assumption]
- That statutory 'free school choice' (szabad iskolaválasztás) cannot be significantly narrowed without explicit re-legislation, even though the Nkt. does not enshrine it as a fundamental right or specify its scope precisely. [assumption]
- That church maintainer funding agreements and autonomy guarantees create a de facto legal-political veto, requiring prior consent to any structural reform affecting church-run 6/8-year tracks, even though the exact terms of relevant church–state accords are not in the public domain. [assumption]
- That the quality-conditioning mechanism (implied by the 1994 MKM rendelet and the 2010 Hoffmann plan) remains a lawful tool under current Nkt. framework, even though recent EMMI decrees and Onytv. have not explicitly re-codified it. [assumption]
- That maintainer-specific compliance and inspection powers (kormányhivatal register, licence withdrawal) can be applied uniformly to state, church, and foundation maintainers without constitutional conflict or treaty breach. [assumption]
- That the societal consultation ('társadalmi egyeztetés') signalled by Minister Lannert will follow a transparent, multi-stakeholder deliberation format (rather than a symbolic or captured process), and will genuinely inform or constrain the final policy design. [assumption]

## Position
Hungary's legal and governance system permits three feasible 6/8-year reform routes—admissions-rule change via EMMI decree (fastest, no felmenő constraint), quality-conditioning via registration-power enforcement (moderate speed, requires prior maintainer consent), and track-discontinuation via Nkt. amendment (slowest, 8-year phase-out, requires multi-stakeholder buy-in including churches)—but the current political-legal environment favours restriction over abolition.

## Uncertainties
- Whether a near-term (2026–2028) reform would proceed via EMMI admissions-decree (retaining tracks, altering selection rules) or Nkt. amendment (risking track retention-or-abolition contest). Political-legal pathway choice is not yet signalled. (confidence: low; would be reduced by: Publication of a draft ministerial decree or legislative bill text; public statement by the Minister of Education naming the legal instrument and timeline.)
- Exact current (2024/25) maintainer breakdown for 6- and 8-year tracks specifically (KSH 2019/20 data is aggregate for all gimnázium). It is unclear whether church-maintained 6/8-year enrolment has continued to rise in line with overall church secondary growth. (confidence: medium; would be reduced by: KSH STADAT or official education registry disaggregated by programme length (hatosztályos, nyolcosztályos) and maintainer type for 2024/25 school year.)
- Legal status of church-maintainer funding agreements (pl. vatikáni megállapodás, state–church financing compacts) as a constraint on legislative reform. It is unclear whether phase-out of 6/8-year tracks at church gimnaziums could be mandated without breach-of-contract risk or renegotiation. (confidence: low; would be reduced by: Full text of relevant church–state financial and autonomy accords (vatikáni megállapodás, updated 2000+ finanszírozási megállapodás); legal opinion from Constitutional Court or state counsel on scope and enforcement.)
- Whether the 'downgrade-to-4-year' quality-conditioning mechanism (1994 MKM precedent, 2010 Hoffmann proposal) remains a valid legal tool under current Onytv. and EMMI decree framework, or whether recent centralisation reforms have removed it from active use. (confidence: low; would be reduced by: Textual search and legal review of current consolidated Onytv. (2018. évi LXXXIX. törvény) and 20/2012 EMMI rendelet; EMMI legal opinion or practice guidance confirming or denying the mechanism's availability.)

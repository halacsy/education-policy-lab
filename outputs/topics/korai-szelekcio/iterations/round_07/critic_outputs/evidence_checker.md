# Critique: evidence_checker

## S1.goal
Objection: The tag is too strong and cites the wrong registry fact. The registry’s `gimn_share [strong]` supports that roughly 8-12% of a cohort enters 6-/8-year tracks through admissions statistics; it does not itself support the field’s stronger claim that access is “less strongly skewed toward high-SES, urban families.” SES-skew is better supported indirectly by `school_choice [moderate]` and `pisa_escs [strong]`, but not by the cited KSH/Oktatási Hivatal admission-statistics fact at strong grade.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade the evidence tag to moderate and cite `school_choice` / value-added or state that admission-skew evidence is expert synthesis rather than registry-backed strong evidence.

## S2.mechanism
Objection: The first mechanism claim is overgraded. The registry’s `legal [strong]` supports that 6/8-year forms are anchored in law and phase-out requires legislative change, but it does not support that “year-on-year caps” are a direct administrative lever, nor that they can be applied cleanly to state-maintained intake. This is a legal/governance inference, not a registry-backed strong fact.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Change the tag to weak or moderate and phrase it as “if legally authorized, caps would mechanically reduce state-sector selective intake.”

## S3.political_risks
Objection: The teacher-union risk cites a “Round 06 discourse analysis” as strong evidence, but that source is not in the curated registry. The registry only gives `teacher_shortage [strong]`, which supports a sustained teacher shortage and ageing workforce, not union non-cooperation conditional on planning time versus lump-sum funding.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Downgrade to weak or mark as expert/model knowledge; keep `teacher_shortage [strong]` only for the underlying capacity constraint, not for the union-behavior claim.

## S4.uncertainties
Objection: The confidence label is internally and evidentially wrong. The uncertainty asks whether Hungary’s teacher shortage will blunt Portuguese-style interventions, but marks confidence as high while saying it would be reduced by comparative studies. The registry’s `teacher_shortage [strong]` supports the existence of the shortage, not the transfer-effect claim about Portuguese-style interventions. That transfer claim remains uncertain and should not be labeled high-confidence.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Set confidence to low or medium and distinguish “high confidence that Hungary has a teacher shortage” from “low confidence about the magnitude of its effect on this package.”

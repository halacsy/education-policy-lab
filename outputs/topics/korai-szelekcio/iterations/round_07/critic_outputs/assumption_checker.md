# Critique: assumption_checker

## S1.assumptions
Objection: The scenario treats “uniform admission-equity rules across state, church and foundation maintainers” as a difficult but administratively solvable assumption, while the expert record frames maintainer plurality as a structural governance constraint. The cited legal_and_governance and school_network_planning evidence supports only that legislative pathways and maintainer statistics exist; it does not support the stronger assumption that non-state maintainers can be made to comply without substantial evasion, litigation, or parallel expansion.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast cross-maintainer uniformity as a central unresolved feasibility condition, and add a fallback design for what happens if church/foundation maintainers are exempt, partially compliant, or expand selective intake.

## S2.goal
Objection: The goal claims that a gradual phase-down will “lower the share of the cohort exposed to early sorting,” but the scenario’s own assumptions and uncertainties concede that non-state maintainers may absorb displaced demand. If church/foundation schools expand selective places, the policy reduces only state-sector sorting, not cohort-level early sorting. The quota-mechanics evidence supports caps as a direct administrative lever for state-maintained schools; it does not support the broader cohort-level claim.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Limit the goal to reducing state-maintained selective intake unless the scenario includes enforceable cross-maintainer caps and evidence that offsetting expansion can be prevented.

## S3.mechanism
Objection: The first mechanism overstates what Hanushek & Woessmann 2006 supports. That evidence supports a general cross-national association between early tracking and stronger SES gradients; it does not establish that abolishing Hungary’s 6/8-year entry points will eliminate meaningful sorting if school choice, catchment sorting, and non-state maintainer channels remain active. The scenario’s own equity_impact field identifies this relocation risk, so the mechanism is too strong as written.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Change the mechanism to “eliminates the formal 6/8-year gimnazium sorting event” and explicitly separate that from the unproven claim that aggregate social sorting is reduced.

## S4.expected_benefits
Objection: The benefit that non-structural reforms can “potentially narrow outcome gaps even without altering entry rules” relies on Portuguese PISA trends, but the evidence tag admits TEIP’s independent contribution is not isolated and transferability to Hungary is only an assumption. The source supports Portugal’s aggregate improvement under a broader reform bundle; it does not support compensation as an offset to Hungary’s age-10/12 selective mechanism.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Downgrade this benefit to weak/extrapolated and specify that the expected gain is improved general-school quality, not demonstrated narrowing of the selection-driven SES gradient.

# Critique: cost_checker
## S1.cost_categories
Objection: The SES-proxy data infrastructure cost is understated as merely "medium" and lacks a range for the two materially different options: a verified self-report workflow versus an administrative data-linkage/register build. The scenario's own assumptions say Hungary has no usable student-level SES register at application time, so the source supports feasibility uncertainty, not a stable medium-cost category.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Split this into "verified self-report/proxy administration" and "administrative SES register or KSH/OH data linkage build," with separate transition and recurring compliance/audit ranges.

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The cost list omits a major fiscal exposure: compensatory funding or capacity controls for general schools absorbing retained high-achievers and displaced pupils during the phase-down. The scenario assumes peer-composition benefits in urban general schools, but the cost categories only price quota administration, grandfathering, and teacher redeployment; they do not price the classroom-capacity, staffing, or support costs needed to make the receiving sector absorb the change.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a recurring "general-school absorption and quality-maintenance package" category, with regional ranges and explicit sensitivity to whether displaced demand moves to non-state maintainers.

## S3.cost_categories
Objection: The categories correctly label several items "high" but still confuse scale with budget exposure by omitting fiscal ranges and duration. "Early diagnostic and remedial support system build-out" is tagged high and recurring, but the Finnish-style evidence supports a bundled, permanent support architecture, not a one-time implementation add-on. Without a per-pupil or per-school range, the scenario understates the steady-state fiscal commitment.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Recast diagnostic/remedial support as a permanent operating-cost line with low/medium/high coverage scenarios, separate from one-time setup and IT/professional-development costs.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The TEIP-style targeted funding line is too aggregated to test fiscal sensitivity. The evidence tag says no numeric range is available, while the political-risk field says this is likely the first line cut in consolidation. That means the source supports high fiscal fragility, not just "high cost"; the scenario needs a durability-sensitive range tied to minimum viable funding, full compensation funding, and erosion under austerity.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the single "high" label with funding scenarios: minimum pilot, national disadvantaged-school coverage, and sustained full compensation package, each with explicit consequences if funding is cut after one budget cycle.

# Critique: feasibility_checker

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The timeline assumes a validated, less-coachable national assessment can be designed, piloted, psychometrically validated, legally accepted, and nationally rolled out in 4-5 years, but the scenario’s own evidence tags say no validated Hungarian instrument exists and the mechanism is only moderate/weak evidence. Kertesi & Kezdi/Horn support selection-effect findings, not the administrative feasibility of building a coaching-resistant replacement exam on this schedule.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Extend the assessment-development timeline, add a prerequisite feasibility/pilot phase before legal rollout, and explicitly state that national implementation is conditional on validation results.

## S1.implementation_steps
Objection: The SES-weighting/quota step is administratively under-specified: the assumptions admit Hungary lacks a usable student-level SES register at application time, yet the implementation plan moves directly to legal testing and rollout. Legal compliance cannot be tested meaningfully until the data source, verification process, appeals process, and anti-gaming controls exist.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Add a year 1-2 administrative data-linkage feasibility study led by KSH/Oktatási Hivatal, followed by a small-scale verified SES-proxy pilot before any quota legislation.

## S3.implementation_steps
Objection: The teacher-retraining plan is not feasible against the scenario’s own capacity evidence. It gives the Ministry years 1-4 to retrain for heterogeneous classrooms, while assumptions and political risks say Hungary’s teacher shortage is binding and teacher-union cooperation depends on dedicated planning time. OECD/PSZ/PDSZ evidence supports shortage severity, not the claim that the system has enough spare staff time to absorb nationwide retraining while maintaining delivery.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Make teacher-workforce capacity a hard precondition: add funded release time, substitute staffing, regional phasing, and a teacher-supply threshold before any national abolition date.

## S4.implementation_steps
Objection: The plan assigns national rollout of grade-retention-reduction and diagnostic supports to KK in years 2-4, but the scenario’s own political_risks state that teacher shortage strongly undermines credible delivery. The Portuguese evidence supports outcome trends under a different teacher-supply context; it does not support Hungary’s administrative capacity to deliver extra remedial and diagnostic services nationally on this timeline.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace national rollout with phased pilots in high-need districts, include staffing and workload costings, and make expansion conditional on measured teacher availability and programme fidelity.

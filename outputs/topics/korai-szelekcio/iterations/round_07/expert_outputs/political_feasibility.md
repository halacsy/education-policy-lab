## Interpretation

The reform faces a **reversal trap:** even if adoption succeeds, political durability is threatened by three layers of stakeholder mobilisation. First, middle-class parents will organise defensively to preserve selection pathways (as they did in the 1990s–2010s). Second, the Polish case demonstrates that postponing tracking does improve equity outcomes, but reversal remains politically feasible—and more likely when the reform is framed as elite loss rather than collective gain. Third, the conditions laid by teachers, parents, and equity advocates are **structural dependencies**, not rhetorical positions: without teacher-time reallocation, without pilot evidence of general-school retention, and without visible inclusion campaigns, each stakeholder group has credible exit paths to opposition.

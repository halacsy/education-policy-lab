# Critique: political_risk_checker
## S1.political_risks
Objection: The scenario claims that "Reformed/scarcer selection channels may invite informal selection (interviews, reputation) that reproduces SES sorting" but provides no evidence for this claim. While this is a plausible risk, the scenario does not cite any specific evidence or studies that support the idea that reformed or scarcer channels *will* lead to informal selection that reproduces SES sorting.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add evidence or a citation to support the claim that reformed/scarcer selection channels are likely to invite informal selection that reproduces SES sorting. If no direct evidence exists, acknowledge this as a speculative risk.

## S2.political_risks
Objection: The scenario states that "Middle-class parents perceive shrinking seats as a threat to a mobility/insurance asset and mobilize" but does not provide evidence for this perception or the likelihood of mobilization. While parental opposition is a common political risk, the scenario does not ground this specific concern in any data or expert opinion that suggests this particular perception will lead to mobilization.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Provide evidence or expert opinion that supports the claim that middle-class parents will perceive shrinking seats as a threat to a mobility/insurance asset and are likely to mobilize in response.

## S3.political_risks
Objection: The scenario asserts that "Middle-class and church/foundation flight relocates sorting rather than removing it" but does not offer evidence to support this specific outcome. While the risk of flight is mentioned, the scenario does not explain why this flight would necessarily "relocate sorting" rather than, for example, simply reduce the overall number of selective places or lead to different forms of sorting. The link between flight and the relocation of sorting is not substantiated.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Provide evidence or a more detailed explanation for why middle-class and church/foundation flight would specifically lead to the relocation of sorting, rather than other potential outcomes.

## S4.political_risks
Objection: The scenario claims that "Legitimising early selection permanently: compensation may entrench rather than dismantle the sorting engine" but does not provide evidence for this specific consequence. While the risk of legitimizing early selection is plausible, the scenario does not explain *how* compensation would entrench the sorting engine or cite any examples or studies where similar compensation mechanisms have had this effect.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Provide evidence or a more detailed explanation of how compensation mechanisms, in this context, would serve to entrench the early selection sorting engine rather than dismantle it.

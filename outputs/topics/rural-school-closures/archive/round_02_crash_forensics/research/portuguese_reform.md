# Research Notes: Portugal's School Network Reform — Relevance to Hungary's Rural Primary School Question

**1. Scale of Portugal's school closures (2010-2012).** Portugal's Dispatch No. 12955/2010 tightened viability criteria and triggered the closure of approximately 3,500 small schools between 2010 and 2012, alongside forced aggregation of standalone schools into larger clusters (agrupamentos de escolas). Source: World Bank, "Portugal's School Clustering Reform," Oct 2025. https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099112525193042171/pdf/P507130-67d27dd7-b942-495b-9b4a-104cf8d24aad.pdf

**2. Legal threshold and rationale.** Decree-Law No. 35/88 (1988) mandated closure of schools with fewer than 21 students, though implementation was gradual; the underlying problem was that single-teacher schools serving fewer than 10 pupils required the same fixed costs as larger schools. Source: same World Bank report as above.

**3. Earlier wave (2005-2007) and teacher perceptions.** A retrospective study found underlying measures predicted the closure of approximately 2,000 primary schools, mainly in rural areas, in 2005-2007; interviews with 20 primary teachers from 12 rural schools found clear differences between central-administration and teacher narratives, with teachers questioning whether teaching quality actually improved since "no changes were found in teaching-learning conditions." Source: Pinto Ferreira, "First Impressions of the Reorganisation of the School Network in Portugal," European Journal of Education (2009). https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1465-3435.2009.01404.x

**4. Consolidation did not reverse rural decline — case study.** A study of Pinhal Interior Sul (a low-density rural region) concluded that closing single-teacher schools and building concentrated "centros escolares" did not reverse rural decline — the decline actually intensified — nor did the new school typology appear to increase schools' pedagogical capacity. Source: Redalyc, "Escola em meio rural e medidas de concentração escolar... Pinhal Interior Sul, Portugal." https://www.redalyc.org/journal/894/89470178005/html/

**5. Teacher union conditions for closures.** Portugal's Fenprof teachers' union did not oppose closing all small schools, but argued that any closures required (i) building consensus with local populations, (ii) safeguarding children's safety/comfort in school transport, and (iii) negotiation between government and unions on network reorganization — otherwise students in several districts would spend many hours daily in transport. Source: same Redalyc article, quoting Jornal da Fenprof (2005-2006). https://www.redalyc.org/journal/894/89470178005/html/

**6. TEIP program — mixed/contested results on academic gaps.** A 15-year quantitative analysis of national exams found "a general failure of the program in reducing the gap between TEIP and non-TEIP public schools regarding academic performance," even though early-school-leaving/retention rates fell faster in TEIP schools than non-TEIP schools between 2007/08 and 2010/11. Source: MDPI, "Has the Portuguese Compensatory Education Program Been Successful..." (2019). https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7102/9/4/270

**7. TEIP still shows persistent gaps (most recent evidence, 2026).** A 2026 study combining TEIP monitoring reports with DGEEC statistics found that TEIP schools "continue to face consistently higher retention and dropout rates than national averages," particularly in inland and border (i.e., rural/depopulating) municipalities. Source: Frontiers in Education, "From performance to shared responsibility? School self-evaluation and accountability..." (2026). https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2026.1812912/full

**8. TEIP program current scale and evolution.** TEIP includes 137 school clusters representing about 17% of all Portuguese school clusters; a fourth generation (TEIP4) was launched in 2023 for six academic years, "strengthening and refocusing previous measures while granting greater autonomy to educational communities." TEIP has evolved through four generations since 1996, each broadening from social inclusion (1996) to reducing early school leaving (2006) to learning-outcome quality (2012) to community/autonomy focus (2023). Sources: European School Education Platform, https://school-education.ec.europa.eu/en/teach/teaching-materials/teip-programme-priority-intervention-educational-areas ; EU Education and Training Monitor 2025 Portugal, https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/portugal.html

**9. Early school leaving fell sharply nationally (not rural-specific).** Portugal cut its early school-leaving rate by 10.7 percentage points between 2014 and 2024 — the steepest reduction among all 27 EU member states over that period — clearing the EU's 2030 target of below 9% ahead of schedule. Source: Quantumrun Education in Portugal Statistics 2026, citing Eurostat (May 2025). https://www.quantumrun.com/consulting/education-in-portugal/

**10. Caveat — overall learning outcomes declined despite structural/programmatic reforms.** PISA 2022 data show 29.7% of 15-year-olds in Portugal failed to reach minimum proficiency in mathematics, a 5.9 percentage-point increase since 2015, and the share of top performers fell from 11.4% to 6.7% over the same period — indicating that network consolidation and TEIP targeting coincided with, rather than reversed, a broader national decline in learning outcomes and rising inequality (only 16.0% of low-SES students performed well in 2022, down from 21.0% in 2015). Source: EU Education and Training Monitor 2025 Portugal, https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/portugal.html

**11. Current teacher-supply pressure complicating any rural strategy.** More than half of Portugal's 134,000 teachers are over 50, and projections show 4,000 retirements in 2025 alone — a structural constraint on any model (including rural multigrade schools) that depends on adequate staffing. Source: The Portugal Post, "Portugal Education Reform: Regional Gap Risks for Expats," Aug 2025. https://theportugalpost.com/posts/ministry-overhaul-could-split-portugals-schools-teachers-warn

**12. Comparative note — Spain's rural school clusters (CRA) still being reduced today.** Even Spain's "agrupación" model (Centros Rurales Agrupados, CRA), designed specifically to preserve rural schooling through clustering, continues to see individual village schools close when enrollment drops below minimum thresholds (e.g., 3 students) — illustrating that clustering delays but does not eliminate closure pressure under continued depopulation. Source: La Gaceta de Salamanca, July 2026. https://www.lagacetadesalamanca.es/salamanca/sangria-escuela-rural-tres-pueblos-colegio-20260705164407-nt.html

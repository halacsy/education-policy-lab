## Research Notes: Rural Primary Schools in Hungary — Structural/Sorting Dimensions

**1. National institutional network contraction, 2021–2024.** A 2025 academic study using settlement-level KIR/KSH data found the number of school sites (feladatellátási hely) fell from 9,046 (2021) to 8,590 (2024), and primary-education (ISCED 1–2) sites fell from 3,609 to 3,521 over the same period. By 2024, more than 1,200 of Hungary's 3,155 settlements had no public educational institution at all, and disadvantaged children have become concentrated in rural areas (up to 94% in some settlements) while urban child-teacher ratios rose to 24:1.
Source: "Shrinking, growing or overburdened? Territorial patterns in child demographics and institutional burden in Hungary's public education system after 2021" (Oct 2025) — https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396483025_Shrinking_growing_or_overburdened_Territorial_patterns_in_child_demographics_and_institutional_burden_in_Hungary's_public_education_system_after_2021

**2. Demographic collapse driving the current closure wave.** Hungarian outlet reporting (Nov 2025) states 2024 set a historic low of 77,500 births, 9.1% (7,725) fewer than the prior year, and that the public-education network was built for more children than are now being born; a teachers'-union leader is quoted warning that a system planned for 110,000 pupils per cohort will see only ~77,000 enrollments within six years.
Source: "Elfogynak a gyermekek a köznevelésből," Magyar Hang, Nov 2025 — https://hang.hu/budaihang/elfogynak-a-gyermekek-a-koznevelesbol-181611

**3. Government position vs. union warnings.** A ministerial commissioner (Balatoni Katalin) stated the government's goal is not closure but protecting small-settlement schools "as long as there is demand," including via mergers and local solutions, while the education union (PSZ) head Totyik Tamás argues consolidation of low-enrollment schools is "almost certainly unavoidable."
Source: "Óvodák már lakat alatt, de itt lappang az iskolabezárási hullám is," Válasz Online, Oct 2025 — https://www.valaszonline.hu/2025/10/17/ovodak-iskolak-bezarasa-demografia-oktatas/

**4. Current enrollment/site data (KSH, 2024/2025 school year).** Primary-school (általános iskola) enrollment fell to ~710,000 (from 712,000 the year before, –0.3%), first-graders fell 1.9% to 95,000, and the number of primary-school sites (feladatellátási hely) fell to 3,537 — 13 fewer than the previous year, 88 fewer than 2021/2022. Compared to 2005/2006 (859,000 pupils), enrollment is down 17.3%.
Source: "Egyre kevesebb az általános iskolás Magyarországon," Népszava, July 2025 — https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**5. Historical precedent: the 2003–2007 closure/amalgamation wave.** Research shows 95 independent small-settlement primary schools (in villages under 1,100 people) closed in just three years (2003–2005), driven by both financing-rule changes (the 2004 Act on micro-regional associations, "kistérségi társulás") and demographic decline; between 2001–2007 nationally, institutions fell 10% (4,718→4,233) while small-settlement school sites fell 26% (813→604) and their pupil numbers fell 25%.
Sources: Bódi Ferenc – Fekete Attila, "Iskolák-óvodák elhelyezkedése és bezárása" — https://politikatudomany.tk.elte.hu/uploads/files/71_90_Bodi_Fekete(1).pdf ; and Új Pedagógiai Szemle analysis — https://folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu/uj-pedagogiai-szemle/az-altalanos-iskolai-intezmenyhalozat-valtozasai-a-kisebbsegi-oktatas

**6. Community resistance & the "Human Chain" movement.** During the 2007 closure wave, the NGO "Emberi Lánc" ("Human Chain for Rural Schools") campaigned against closures; a peer-reviewed case study of Erdőkürt village school documented how strong local community engagement and social capital helped resist amalgamation with neighboring schools, framing the school as a community hub whose loss threatens village viability.
Source: "Rescuing a small village school in the context of rural change in Hungary," Journal of Rural Studies, ScienceDirect — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0743016712000216

**7. Legal/structural mechanics: catchment ("körzet") and maintainer (tankerület) system.** Kindergartens and primary schools are legally obliged to admit all children within their designated catchment district unless capacity is exceeded, in which case the child must have access to a neighboring school. State-maintained schools' catchments (felvételi körzet) are officially designated annually by the tankerületi központ (district center) per settlement/street, as seen in a 2025/2026 Kecskemét Tankerületi Központ catchment notice.
Sources: UNHCR Hungary education-access note — https://data.unhcr.org/en/documents/download/101911 ; Kecskeméti Tankerületi Központ 2025/2026 catchment document — https://kk.gov.hu/download/c/68/53000/Kecskem%C3%A9ti%20TK%20K%C3%B6zlem%C3%A9ny2025-2026.pdf

**8. School-bus (iskolabusz) transport as the load-bearing alternative to closure — and its fragility.** Civil-liberties group TASZ notes that for children in catchment ("körzetes") schools, the maintainer (for state schools, the tankerület) is legally responsible for organizing school-bus transport when a child's assigned school is not local. Reporting from Hódmezővásárhely (2023) documented a tankerület seeking to replace a dedicated school bus for tanya (outlying farmstead) children with ordinary public-transport passes, provoking parent protests, and eduline.hu's "iskolabusz" tag archive shows recurring incidents nationwide of last-minute bus-route cancellations shortly before term start.
Sources: TASZ complaint-template page — https://tasz.hu/cikkek/panaszmintat-keszitettunk-az-iskolabuszok-ujrainditasa-erdekeben/ ; Népszava on Hódmezővásárhely — https://nepszava.hu/3198062_iskolabusz-hodmezovasarhely-kornyeke-tanyavilag ; eduline.hu iskolabusz tag — https://eduline.hu/cimke/iskolabusz

**9. Church/foundation maintainers as a rescue mechanism.** In 2026, the Kolping Oktatási és Szociális Intézményfenntartó Szervezet (a Catholic-affiliated maintainer network) stepped in to take over threatened small schools (e.g., a 220-year-old gimnázium in Gyönk), citing "greater room to maneuver than the state education system," and explicitly linking this to existing social-transport (iskolabusz-capable) infrastructure it already operates for disabled-care services — illustrating how non-state maintainers can bundle school transport with other rural social services to keep small schools viable.
Source: "Megmenekülhet a 220 éves iskola," Teol.hu, May 2026 — https://www.teol.hu/helyi-kozelet/2026/05/kolping-megmenti-a-kisiskolakat

**10. The 6/8-year gimnázium "brain drain" effect on rural/feeder primary schools.** Research (Horn et al., 2018) found that when top-performing pupils leave for elite 6- or 8-year gimnáziums after grade 4 or 6, this has an average mildly negative effect on the peers left behind in ordinary primary schools (reading/math competency scores, grades, further-study intentions at grade 8), though effects are mixed — remaining strong students lose somewhat while weaker students may even gain. Commentary confirms these selective schools admit via a centralized written exam whose results all 6-year gimnáziums must accept regardless of testing site, and that this selection empties out the top tier of local primary-school classes, accelerating "class merger" (osztály-összevonás) pressure in the feeder schools — a dynamic that compounds enrollment decline separately from raw demographic loss and is especially consequential for small/rural feeder schools already near viability thresholds.
Sources: Qubit.hu summary of Horn et al. research — https://qubit.hu/2022/06/15/olyan-gyerek-akinek-kozmunkasok-a-szulei-hat-olyan-itt-nincs ; Eduline "Hat- és nyolcosztályos gimnáziumok: kinek való ez az iskolatípus?" — https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/hat_es_nyolcosztalyos_gimnazium_pro_es_kont_GSKH47 ; Divány.hu on the "felhígulás" (dilution) effect — https://divany.hu/szuloseg/2018/09/09/negyosztalyos-hatosztalyos-nyolcosztalyos-gimnaziumba-menjen-a-gyerek/

**11. Public opinion on closure burden (context, pre-current wave but indicative of household impact).** An Ipsos survey found three-quarters of families with children said temporary or permanent closure of kindergartens/schools would create difficulties, with 78% of the most severely affected households located in small rural towns or villages, and 45% saying child placement would be unsolvable even short-term.
Source: Ipsos, "Nagy terhet jelentene az iskolabezárás a magyar családok számára" — https://www.ipsos.com/hu-hu/nagy-terhet-jelentene-az-iskolabezaras-magyar-csaladok-szamara

## Research Notes — Rural Primary Schools in Hungary (round 2)

**1. National demographic collapse is now the dominant driver of school-network shrinkage.** Hungary recorded its lowest-ever birth count in 2024 — 77,500 births, down 9.1% (7,725 fewer) than the prior year, compared with figures near 85–90,000 in preceding years and 125,000 in 1990. Source: Magyar Hang, "Elfogynak a gyermekek a köznevelésből," Nov 2025 — https://hang.hu/budaihang/elfogynak-a-gyermekek-a-koznevelesbol-181611

**2. The system was built for a much larger cohort and enrollment keeps falling.** The union leader Totyik Tamás stated the network was designed for roughly 110,000 pupils per age-cohort nationally, while within six years only ~77,000 will be enrolling — implying unavoidable consolidations. Same source as above.

**3. Official KSH data confirm a steady multi-year decline in primary enrollment and school count.** In 2024/2025, ~710,000 pupils attended day-form primary school vs. 712,000 the year before (-0.3%); compared with 2005/2006 the drop is 17.3% (149,000 fewer pupils, from 859,000). The number of primary "feladatellátási helyek" (school sites) fell to 3,537 in 2024/2025 — 13 fewer than the previous year, 38 fewer than two years prior, 88 fewer than three years prior. Source: Népszava, "Egyre kevesebb az általános iskolás Magyarországon," citing KSH — https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**4. Small (sub-100-pupil) schools are shrinking disproportionately and are concentrated in villages.** A historical OFI/Pedagógiai Szemle analysis (2001–2007) found the number of primary institutions fell 10% nationally, but 15% among sub-100-pupil schools; in settlements under 1,000 residents, school sites fell 26% and pupil numbers 25%, both far above the national average. Source: Új Pedagógiai Szemle, OFI — https://folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu/uj-pedagogiai-szemle/az-altalanos-iskolai-intezmenyhalozat-valtozasai-a-kisebbsegi-oktatas

**5. Legal/administrative mechanism for catchments ("felvételi körzet"): under Nkt. §50(8), each state-run tankerületi központ (school district center) defines and publishes compulsory-admission catchment boundaries per settlement/street annually; villages without their own school are assigned to a designated neighboring "kötelező felvételt biztosító iskola." Example: Bélapátfalva district assigns children from villages like Balaton and Bekölce (no local school) to a two-language primary school in the district seat. Source: Egri Tankerületi Központ catchment document 2024/2025 — https://egerszalok.hu/hivatal/images/dokumentumok/aktualisok/Egri_Tankeruleti_Kozpont_altalanos_iskolai_felveteli_korzethatarok_2024-2025._tanevre.pdf

**6. Residency verification is strictly enforced to prevent catchment gaming.** Regulation 20/2012 EMMI §22(7)-(8) allows school principals to request home visits to confirm "életvitelszerű" (de facto) residence in the catchment; refusal to allow at least one of two offered visit dates creates a presumption of non-residence and can justify admission refusal. Source: Debreceni Tankerületi Központ enrollment guidance — https://lorantffy.suli.hu/images/PDF/tajlevsz.pdf

**7. Maintainer-change ("fenntartóváltás") to church operators is a recognized escape valve for small village schools facing state size thresholds, but requires a majority vote of parents/students and ministerial approval; church-run schools taking over a state task must still fulfill compulsory-admission duties and the state must guarantee a non-religious alternative "without disproportionate burden." Around 2011, roughly 83 mostly rural/state schools were transferred to churches in one wave, concentrated in Alföld and Northern Hungary counties. Source: TASZ explainer — https://tasz.hu/tudastar/iskolak-fenntartovaltasa/ ; Nyest.hu historical account — https://www.nyest.hu/hirek/egyhazi-fennhatosag-ala-kerulo-iskolak

**8. Grassroots/civic maintainer models exist as alternatives to closure.** In Sáta (Borsod), when the village risked losing its school, a local Roma Buddhist community (Dzsaj Bhím) took over maintainership rather than let the village go without a school — "vagy iskola nélkül marad a falu, vagy csatlakozunk a Dzsaj Bhím közösséghez" (paraphrased situation, not a direct quote reproduced). Source: nyest.hu — https://www.nyest.hu/hirek/egyhazi-fennhatosag-ala-kerulo-iskolak

**9. Small-school effectiveness research is used on both sides of the debate.** OFI research (Hermann) concluded that rural small-school outcomes are broadly comparable to larger village schools and only somewhat below urban schools once family background is controlled for, undermining pure efficiency arguments for closure. Source: OFI — https://ofi.oh.gov.hu/falusi-kisiskolak-es-meretgazdasagossaggal-osszefuggo-hatekonysagvesztesegek ; also https://ofi.oh.gov.hu/laki-tamasne-falusi-kisiskola-muzeum-vagy-reformpedagogiai-muhely

**10. Community-hub and place-attachment dimension is documented qualitatively.** In a recent Edunext piece, two village mayors running small schools described the school as genuinely "theirs" even though the state finances it, and researchers note this identity function; separately, the Faluszövetség (Village Association) president argued for defending village schools, noting that in 2013, 1,293 of Hungary's 2,809 village-run settlements had no primary school at all, and only ~40% of villages had a full (grade 1–8) school. Source: Edunext — https://edunext.hu/cikk/a-hazai-telepulesek-felen-nincs-felso-tagozatos-iskola ; Pedagógiai Folyóiratok "Kié az iskola?" — https://folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu/uj-pedagogiai-szemle/kie-az-iskola

**11. A concrete "grow your own gimnázium" community response case:** In 2011 four Balaton-felvidék municipalities (Pécsely, Vászoly, Dörgicse, Balatonszőlős), facing depopulation of local church-run small schools, tried to found a 6-year gimnázium rather than merely a primary school to attract in-migrating families; the county government office rejected this and pushed for a primary school instead, and most interested families ultimately did not relocate — illustrating both the aspirational use of gimnázium tracks as a repopulation tool and administrative resistance to non-standard rural school models. Source: Pedagógiai Folyóiratok — https://folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu/uj-pedagogiai-szemle/kie-az-iskola

**12. Current political framing (2025) explicitly avoids "closure" language in favor of managed mergers.** Government commissioner Balatoni Katalin stated the aim is protection of small settlements/small schools "as long as there is demand," with mergers and local solutions as fallback tools rather than blanket closure. Source: Válasz Online, Oct 2025 — https://www.valaszonline.hu/2025/10/17/ovodak-iskolak-bezarasa-demografia-oktatas/

**13. Practical travel-burden coping mechanisms are emerging informally**, e.g. parents organizing rotating unsupervised "bus duty" escort shifts for school-bus travel because Volán regional buses often refuse to admit unsupervised minors, and no local school remains to walk to. Source: Válasz Online, Oct 2025 (same as above).

# Research Notes (Implementation Planning domain)

**1. Rural teacher shortage confirmed as structurally worse than urban.** The EU's 2025 Education and Training Monitor states: "Teacher shortages, linked to an ageing workforce, are a growing concern" and "Teacher shortages are greatest in rural areas, with the proportion of unqualified teachers in disadvantaged schools 2 to 3 times higher than in schools not disadvantaged." Half of all teachers in 2024/2025 were 50+, only 7.2% under 30. Source: https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/hungary.html

**2. Vacancy postings rising sharply, worst in STEM/English.** eduline.hu (June 2026) reports several thousand unfilled teaching posts before end of 2025/2026 school year, with postings up "közel negyedével" (~25%) year-on-year, concentrated in math, science, and English. Source: https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20260611_tanarhiany-allaskereses-magyarorszag-2025-2026-os-tanev

**3. Retirement wave was already flagged years ago as a supply bottleneck.** Eurofound (2018) noted the government's 2016–2025 higher-education strategy estimated "about 50,000 teachers will reach the legal retirement age by 2025" and that "at least 2,000 entrants to the profession are needed each year" to compensate — a target research suggests was not met given continued shortage reports. Source: https://www.eurofound.europa.eu/en/resources/article/2018/hungary-shortage-teaching-staff-public-education

**4. Union warnings of accelerating attrition tied to the new "Status Law."** RFE/RL (2023) cites PDSZ: teacher numbers were already below 140,000 in 2022, and the union warned "a further 5,000 teachers could leave the profession" after the Status Law, with "a further 25,000 teachers could quit" within five years. Source: https://www.rferl.org/a/hungary-shortage-teachers/32475124.html

**5. Small schools are NOT the cause of the national teacher shortage — an important sequencing/allocation point.** Economist Júlia Varga is quoted in eduline.hu (Feb 2024): "Őrületes illúzió azt gondolni, hogy Magyarországon azért van tanárhiány, mert sok a kisiskola" (it's a delusion to think small schools cause the shortage) — shortage has grown fastest in Budapest and large cities. Same source notes small-school maintenance costs more per capita but these schools are "alig 1 százalékot" (barely 1%) of the whole system. Source: https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20240206_kisiskola-iskolabezaras-tanarhiany

**6. A concrete EU commitment exists that sets a policy/administrative trigger.** Per Gosztonyi Gábor (PDSZ) as reported by hvg360/eduline: in summer 2022 the government committed to the EU to reorganize/integrate small-settlement schools where class sizes are below 10 and total enrollment is under 100–120 students into a central institution. Government spokespeople (Rétvári Bence) have repeatedly denied active closure plans as "fake news," while Forbes.hu (2024) reports leaked plans for "több tucat" (dozens) to "félszáznál is több" (more than fifty) small schools, including ~60 with class sizes ≤7, to be merged after municipal elections — indicating a politically stalled but administratively pre-drafted process. Sources: https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20240206_kisiskola-iskolabezaras-tanarhiany ; https://www.forbes.hu/uzlet/kormany-iskolabezaras/ ; https://m.hvg.hu/360/20240205_kisiskolak_bezaras_oktatas_kozoktatas_pinter_sandor_kistelepules_tanarhiany_pedagogus_iskola

**7. Demographic cliff timeline explicitly forecast by the teachers' union president (2026).** Totyik Tamás (PSZ) states annual births fell "22,5 százalékkal" over ten years — from 110,800 in 2015 to 84,300 in 2024 and 77,600 in 2025 — and predicts "az első komoly törés az óvodáknál jelentkezhet 2027 körül, majd 2030-ra az alsó tagozatokban is látványossá válik a gyerekszám zuhanása" (first major break in kindergartens around 2027, visible collapse in lower-primary grades by 2030). He calls for "valódi társadalmi párbeszédre" (genuine social dialogue) rather than closures decided as political communication campaigns. Source: https://www.economx.hu/belfold/2026/05/19/robbanas-elotti-allapot-totyik-tamas-psz-demografiai-kozoktatas-gyermekszam-pedagogusok/

**8. Existing legal/administrative tool: multigrade ("összevont") classrooms.** Hungarian law (2011. évi CXC. tv., §4(24) and Annex 3) explicitly permits merging up to 3 (exceptionally 4, if only grades 1–4) consecutive grades into one class in lower-primary, alternative-program, and special-needs schools — an administratively "ready" alternative to closure. Source: https://tudasbazis.ekreta.hu/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=2425613

**9. Real-world implementation case shows sequencing/transport logistics.** In Andocs, a district center (tankerület) suspended a small "tagintézmény" (branch school) for the 2023/2024 year after grade-5 enrollment fell below the legal minimum for three consecutive years; the district states "A gyerekek szállítása az andocsi központi iskolába biztosított" (transport to the central school was secured) and parents were notified in advance. This is a concrete micro-case of what an orderly, non-abrupt merger process looks like. Source: https://www.sonline.hu/helyi-kozelet/2023/09/osszevont-osztalyok-helyett-masik-iskola

**10. Scale of the small-school population (baseline, though dated).** A 2008-era study (OFI) found 109 schools with ≤20 students nationally, enrolling 1,698 children total, with a broader population of "200–300 között" schools relying on multigrade or partially-graded structures — giving an order-of-magnitude sense of how many institutions any consolidation policy would touch. Source: https://ofi.oh.gov.hu/laki-tamasne-falusi-kisiskola-muzeum-vagy-reformpedagogiai-muhely

**11. Fertility and demographic trend data underpinning any multi-year timeline.** Hungary's estimated 2025 total fertility rate is 1.31, down from 1.38 in 2024, and births fell 6.7% year-on-year in Jan–Aug 2025. Source: https://dailynewshungary.com/hungary-population-decline-deepens-reasons/. Separately, KSH reports that as of 1 January 2024, "nearly 18% living in the capital city, almost 53% in towns, and 30% in villages," and that "from 2023, there was a migration gain in the capital city, while the gains in the villages declined significantly" — indicating village depopulation is intensifying, not stabilizing. Source: https://www.ksh.hu/population-and-vital-events

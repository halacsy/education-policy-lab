# Research Notes: Legal & Governance Dimensions — Rural Primary Schools in Hungary

**1. Statutory maintainer plurality (Nkt. § 2, § 7, § 9)**
The National Public Education Act (2011. évi CXC. törvény, "Nkt.") establishes that public education institutions can be founded/maintained by the state, municipalities, nationality self-governments, registered church legal entities, or other organizations/persons with proper authorization. A school-maintaining body has a formal, legally defined identity (fenntartó), distinct from the operator/user, and its decisions on establishment, reorganization or closure of an institution are constrained by statutory procedure.
Source: 2011. évi CXC. törvény a nemzeti köznevelésről, njt.hu — https://njt.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00.0 ; https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.tv

**2. Legal procedure and deadlines for school reorganization/closure (Nkt. § 84)**
Under Nkt. § 84(7)(d), for institutions maintained by the state institution-maintaining center (tankerületi központ), the minister responsible for education may decide on reorganization ("átszervezés") of an educational institution no later than the last working day of May of the year the measure is planned to take effect — a hard annual deadline structuring the "speed" of legally permissible change.
Source: 2013. évi LV. törvény (Nkt. módosítás), jogkodex.hu — https://jogkodex.hu/jsz/2013_55_torveny_2197916 ; corroborated by a Ferencváros municipal document (2025) citing the same rule for tankerület-maintained institutions — https://www.ferencvaros.hu/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/07_KT-SZEB-79-Del-Pesti-Tanker-2025-evi-atszervezes-velemenyezese_ocred.pdf

**3. Mandatory consultation/opinion rights before closure or reorganization (Nkt. § 83(4))**
Before a maintainer's decision on establishment, reorganization, or termination of an institution, the law requires obtaining opinions from specified stakeholders — e.g., the property-owning municipality (if the tankerület uses municipal property) must be consulted under Nkt. § 83(4)(h) before termination/reorganization/name changes.
Source: Ferencváros District Council document referencing Nkt. § 83(4) — https://www.ferencvaros.hu/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/07_KT-SZEB-79-Del-Pesti-Tanker-2025-evi-atszervezes-velemenyezese_ocred.pdf

**4. Historical precedent for independent expert review before closure**
The Ombudsman for Educational Rights office noted that since 1 September 2003, the public education act required the local municipality to obtain a written opinion from a nationally-registered independent expert before any school-closure decision; the expert had to assess whether the proposed solution ensures continued adequate-quality provision of the service, and this opinion had to be filed simultaneously to the county government.
Source: Oktatási Jogok Biztosának Hivatala (Ombudsman office), 2003 report — https://www.oktbiztos.hu/ugyek/jelentes2003/fenntart_ir.html

**5. Judicial review limited to legality, not expediency**
The same Ombudsman report states that municipal maintainer decisions on institutional structure enjoy broad autonomy within legal limits, and their public-service decisions can only be reviewed for legality — no body has the right to review them on grounds of expediency/purpose ("célszerűségi szempontból"). This is a key structural constraint: courts/oversight bodies cannot force a "no closure" outcome on policy grounds, only on procedural/legal grounds.
Source: https://www.oktbiztos.hu/ugyek/jelentes2003/fenntart_ir.html

**6. "Felmenő rendszer" (grade-by-grade phase-in) as a general legal technique, applied to structural changes**
"Felmenő rendszer" is statutorily defined in Nkt. § 4(8): "felmenő rendszer: a tanulmányi és vizsgakövetelmények bevezetésének fokozatos ütemezése, amelynek értelmében a változást az érintett legalacsonyabb iskolai évfolyamon kezdve lehet bevezetni" (gradual phase-in starting from the lowest affected grade). While defined for curriculum/exam requirement changes, this mechanism is also used in practice for organizational restructuring — e.g., a 2025 Budapest tankerület plan to split a school's lower and upper grades across two sites was explicitly implemented "felmenő rendszerben" (grade-by-grade, starting with the new first-graders in the 2028-2029 school year), showing the technique's transferability to closure/reorganization contexts to protect currently-enrolled cohorts from disruption mid-course.
Source: Nkt. § 4(8), njt.hu — https://njt.hu/jogszabaly/2011-190-00-00 ; Telex reporting on Külső-Pesti Tankerület reorganization plans (22 April 2025) — https://telex.hu/belfold/2025/04/22/kulso-pesti-tankerulet-iskolak-atszervezes-kapocs-utcai-altalanos-iskola

**7. Maintainer-change ("fenntartóváltás") as an alternative to closure, and its own transition rule**
Regulatory practice explicitly provides that when a maintainer change occurs, certain performance/output conditions apply "csak a fenntartóváltás évét követő évtől, felmenő rendszerben" (only from the year after the change, phased in grade by grade) — i.e., the law builds in a grace/adaptation period for schools transferring to a new maintainer (e.g., municipal-to-church transfer), rather than requiring instant compliance.
Source: 20/2016. (VIII. 24.) EMMI rendelet — https://njt.jog.gov.hu/jogszabaly/2016-20-20-5H

**8. Ongoing shift of small/rural schools from municipal/tankerület to church maintainers**
Current reporting documents an active pattern (2025) in Budapest of institutions moving from state tankerület maintenance to church maintainers, with a local mayor noting this is partly because church maintenance is financially more favorable for schools and some institutions are "fleeing" the tankerület to find another maintainer amid falling birth numbers (5.8% year-on-year decline reported for February 2025; national births down 8.7% over a 12-month span). This illustrates maintainer plurality functioning as a real "exit option" for small schools facing demographic pressure, not merely a theoretical legal category.
Source: Telex, 22 April 2025 — https://telex.hu/belfold/2025/04/22/kulso-pesti-tankerulet-iskolak-atszervezes-kapocs-utcai-altalanos-iskola

**9. Political/legal caution around a proposed national small-school closure policy**
As of January 2024, reports indicated the idea of closing low-enrollment schools "has been raised repeatedly for about 7-8 years, but due to political risks the Orbán government has not yet acted on it," even though roughly 60 schools with classes of 7 or fewer students were reportedly at risk under a "structural transformation" being discussed; the government spokesperson for the PM's office acknowledged school-structure reform is "politically sensitive" ("politikailag is érzékeny kérdéseket vet fel"). This confirms that statutory power to close/reorganize exists but political-legal caution has so far slowed the speed of nationwide implementation.
Source: eduline.hu, 19 January 2024 — https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20240119_iskolabezarasok_onkormanyzati_valasztas_Belugyminiszterium_kis_letszamu_iskolak

**10. Comparative/cross-border reference point (Kárpátalja, Ukraine) — a stricter statutory threshold model**
For context on how a legally binding enrollment threshold operates elsewhere: in Ukrainian-administered Subcarpathia, since 1 September 2025 state funding is no longer guaranteed for general education institutions where the number of students falls below 45, with further threshold rules from 2026 depending on institution type; falling below the threshold does not mean automatic closure — the local self-government can decide to continue funding from its own budget, reorganize (e.g., turn the school into a branch of a "base school"), or terminate it, and parents retain the right to elementary education near their place of residence. This is a useful comparative model (statutory numeric threshold + local-government discretion + parental-access guarantee) though it is foreign law, not Hungarian Nkt.
Source: Kárpátalja hetilap / karpataljalap.net, 22 June 2026 — https://karpataljalap.net/2026/06/22/mi-lesz-kisiskolakkal ; kiszo.net, 12 July 2026 — https://kiszo.net/2026/07/12/kiszo-kerdes-veszelyben-a-karpataljai-magyar-kisiskolak/

**11. Historical policy oscillation shows legal system tolerates reversal but at social cost**
Academic commentary (Andl Helga, cited by Edunext) documents that Hungarian education policy has oscillated between closing small rural schools (dominant 1970s policy) and "re-districting"/reopening them (post-1989, then again 2000s "körzetesítés," reversed again after 2010) — with the "visszakörzetesítés" (re-districting back) never being as effective as the original consolidation. By 2020, in 44% of Hungarian settlements there was no lower-primary (grades 1-4) provision locally and in 52% no upper-primary (5-8) provision locally.
Source: edunext.hu — https://edunext.hu/cikk/a-hazai-telepulesek-felen-nincs-felso-tagozatos-iskola

**12. Magyar Falu Program as a non-Nkt. governance/funding channel supporting rural school infrastructure**
The Magyar Falu Program (rural development program for settlements ≤5,000 population, run by the Ministry of Agriculture since Feb 2025) funds infrastructure at rural schools/kindergartens as part of broader village-preservation policy; one cited example: a kindergarten in Nemti was saved from closure and reopened in March 2026 via this program. This shows a parallel governance track (outside Nkt./tankerület structure) used to keep small institutions viable through non-educational-sector funding.
Source: magyarfaluprogram.hu — https://magyarfaluprogram.hu/

## Research Notes: Portugal's Rural School Network Reform — Evidence for Transfer to Hungary's Rural Primary School Question

**1. Scale of closures.** Between the 2005/2006 and 2012/2013 school years, Portugal's Ministry of Education closed 4,197 primary schools as part of the "reordenamento da rede escolar" (school network reorganization), initially targeting schools with fewer than 10 students (2005), then expanded to schools with fewer than 21 students (2010). Source: "Escolas primárias encerradas desde 2005," PÚBLICO, https://acervo.publico.pt/sociedade/escolas-primarias-encerradas

**2. Concentration of closures in low-density regions.** A 2024 report on 20 years of the Central Region's school network found that 90% of all school closures (1,981 establishments) occurred in the period 2005–2014, with the process stabilizing/slowing after 2015. The Viseu Dão Lafões inter-municipal area alone lost 527 establishments (2005–2022). Source: "Espaços e práticas educativas em transformação: 20 anos da Rede Escolar da Região Centro" (2024), DGEstE, https://www.dgeste.mec.pt/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/Espacos-e-praticas-educativas-em-transformacao-20-anos-da-rede-escolar-da-Regiao-Centro-2024-net.pdf

**3. Regional intensity example.** In Northern Portugal, primary schools fell from roughly 3,500 (2000) to 1,555 by 2006/2007; in Trás-os-Montes specifically, from 651 to 161 schools, one of the most affected regions under the sub-10-student closure threshold. Source: "Quatro mil escolas fechadas em 8 anos," Diário de Notícias (2008), https://www.dn.pt/arquivo/diario-de-noticias/quatro-mil-escolas-fechadas-em-8-anos.html

**4. Repurposing of closed buildings.** Of nearly 4,000 closed schools surveyed by PÚBLICO, about 20% remain unused; the rest have been converted into museums/libraries/theatres (~40 schools), sold or listed for sale (~60), used by choirs/bands (100+ nationally), turned into cafés/restaurants, or GNR/PSP police posts, and in Alcoutim several became chapels. Source: "Escolas primárias encerradas desde 2005," PÚBLICO, https://acervo.publico.pt/sociedade/escolas-primarias-encerradas

**5. Contested community-decline link.** Sociologist Rui d'Espiney (Instituto de Comunidades Educativas), who has run a rural-school anti-isolation project since the 1990s, argues that removing children from villages accelerated desertification and that "as comunidades ficaram mais pequenas, isoladas e perdidas" once schools closed. This is an advocacy/expert claim, not a controlled study. Source: PÚBLICO, https://acervo.publico.pt/sociedade/escolas-primarias-encerradas; also Diário de Notícias, https://www.dn.pt/arquivo/diario-de-noticias/quatro-mil-escolas-fechadas-em-8-anos.html

**6. Teacher union counter-position (contested).** FENPROF (largest teachers' union) does not demand blanket preservation of small schools but insists closures require (a) community consensus, (b) safeguards on travel comfort/duration/safety, and (c) negotiated timing so replacement "centros escolares" have proper facilities before transfer. Source: FENPROF, "Encerramento de escolas e reorganização escolar," https://www.fenprof.pt/encerramento-de-escolas-e-reorganizacao-escolar

**7. TEIP program — mixed, largely negative on academic outcomes.** A 15-year quantitative study of national exams found TEIP (Territórios Educativos de Intervenção Prioritária, Portugal's compensatory-education program for disadvantaged/often rural-adjacent territories) had "a general failure of the program in reducing the gap between TEIP and non-TEIP public schools regarding academic performance," even as dropout/failure rates fell somewhat. Source: "Has the Portuguese Compensatory Education Program Been Successful in Reducing Disadvantaged Schools' Performance Gaps? A 15-Year Quantitative Analysis of National Exams," MDPI Education Sciences (2019), https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7102/9/4/270. A companion 2014 study similarly noted "school failure and dropout have undeniably been reduced" but "these results do not appear to be related to the development of students' competencies," with TEIP students scoring worse on national exams than peers. Source: Procedia Social and Behavioral Sciences 116 (2014), https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1877042814010799/pdf

**8. TEIP scale today.** As of recent data, the TEIP programme covers 137 school clusters, representing 17% of all Portuguese school clusters, aimed at promoting educational inclusion in disadvantaged/socially vulnerable areas. Source: European School Education Platform, "TEIP - Programme for Priority Intervention Educational Areas," https://school-education.ec.europa.eu/en/teach/teaching-materials/teip-programme-priority-intervention-educational-areas

**9. Retention reduction — strong, standards-based, non-structural intervention.** The Programa Nacional de Promoção do Sucesso Escolar (PNPSE, launched 2016) led to Strategic Action Plans in 98% of eligible schools and achieved a 29% reduction in basic-education retention rates by the end of the 2016–2018 biennium — without closing or merging schools, using locally designed, school-level action plans instead. Source: PNPSE official site, https://pnpse.min-educ.pt/programa. A related 2019 conference document cites schools that "reduziram, em pelo menos 25% a sua taxa de retenção, em cada ciclo de ensino, entre o biénio 2014-2016" and the following biennium. Source: https://www.desafio2030.altominho.pt/fotos/editor2/altominho2030/gca/helena_fonseca_pnpse_moncao_10set2019.pdf

**10. Rural transport burden — mobility mode differences.** A Minho university thesis found that about 70% of Portuguese students travel to school by non-active modes (car/bus), rising to about 83% among rural students specifically — evidence that consolidation increases motorized/bus dependency for rural children versus urban peers. Source: Universidade do Minho repository (2015), https://repositorium.uminho.pt/bitstreams/81dbd750-0922-4af8-b552-26e0fefcdcf9/download

**11. Legal/negotiated distance threshold.** In the Fundão municipality's "Matriz para escolas em territórios de baixa densidade" methodology, a 25-minute travel-time threshold was built into the closure-decision matrix, "impossibilitando o encerramento da escola" beyond that limit, forcing broader deliberation — an explicit institutional safeguard on travel burden. Source: Câmara Municipal do Fundão, "Reorganização da Rede Escolar em Territórios de Baixa Densidade," https://cm-fundao.pt/phocadownload/CMF/Educacao/proj_educativo_local/vol_3.pdf

**12. Structural alternative — "centros escolares" and multigrade classes.** Rather than pure closure, Portugal's policy formally paired closures with construction of larger, better-equipped "centros escolares" (in the 2005/2006 announced list, 1,418 schools were slated for closure with transfer of pupils to 800 new, better-equipped schools), while some rural schools retained multigrade classrooms (multiple grade levels per class) as a lower-cost alternative to closure. Source: Diário de Notícias (2008), https://www.dn.pt/arquivo/diario-de-noticias/quatro-mil-escolas-fechadas-em-8-anos.html; multigrade context in Fundão matrix document, https://cm-fundao.pt/phocadownload/CMF/Educacao/proj_educativo_local/vol_3.pdf

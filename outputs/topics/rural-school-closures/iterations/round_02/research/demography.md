# Research Notes: Demographic Drivers of Rural Primary School Viability in Hungary (through 2040)

**1. Fertility rate decline resuming since 2022, reaching lowest levels in over a decade.**
Hungary's total fertility rate rose from 1.23 (2011) to 1.61 (2021) under pronatalist policy, but has since fallen to 1.38 by 2024, with depopulation now progressing at its fastest rate on record. Analysts attribute the earlier rise mainly to large 1970s birth cohorts reaching childbearing age rather than policy alone, and note the renewed decline is driven by a shrinking pool of women of childbearing age, economic uncertainty, and emigration (one in six Hungarian children reportedly born abroad).
Source: "Hungary's ongoing demographic decline: an increase in birth rates is the only hope," OSW Centre for Eastern Studies, https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2025-04-14/hungarys-ongoing-demographic-decline-increase-birth-rates-only
Source: "Hungary's demographic decline deepens in 2025," bne IntelliNews, https://www.intellinews.com/hungary-s-demographic-decline-deepens-in-2025-413979/

**2. Population projected to fall to ~8.5 million by 2040 and ~6 million by 2070 if trends continue.**
Experts project continued national depopulation from the current ~9.6 million, directly shrinking the future school-age cohort base nationally, with rural/peripheral areas expected to be hit hardest.
Source: "Hungary's demographic decline deepens in 2025," bne IntelliNews, https://www.intellinews.com/hungary-s-demographic-decline-deepens-in-2025-413979/

**3. 2025 KSH data: fewer births than deaths, accelerating natural decrease; population fell by 51,000 in 2025.**
The Hungarian Central Statistical Office reports fewer children born in 2025 than the prior year, with natural decrease accelerating even as a smaller net migration surplus partially offset the drop; total population is at 9,585,000 (Jan 2024), with 30% living in villages.
Source: "Population and vital events," Hungarian Central Statistical Office (KSH), https://www.ksh.hu/population-and-vital-events

**4. Direct evidence of school-network contraction already underway: 13 fewer school sites in one year, 88 fewer over four years.**
In the 2024/2025 school year, 3,537 primary school locations (feladatellátási hely) were operating nationally — 13 fewer than the previous year, 38 fewer than 2022/2023, and 88 fewer than 2021/2022. Primary enrollment fell by roughly 2,000 students year-on-year to 710,000 (down from 712,000), a 0.3% decline, and first-grade enrollment fell 1.9% to 95,000. Compared to 20 years ago, enrollment is down 17.3% (from 859,000 in 2005/2006).
Source: "Egyre kevesebb az általános iskolás Magyarországon," Népszava, https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**5. Teacher union leader warns closures/consolidations of small schools are "almost certainly unavoidable" within 5-10 years.**
Tamás Totyik, president of the Teachers' Union (PSZ), stated that some primary schools already enroll zero first-graders, and warned drastic measures will be needed, while no political side is addressing what to do about depopulation.
Source: "Egyre kevesebb az általános iskolás Magyarországon," Népszava, https://nepszava.hu/3287108_altalanos-iskola-diakok-letszam-csokkenes-magyarorszag-ksh

**6. Over 1,200 of Hungary's 3,155 settlements had NO public educational institution at all by 2024.**
A 2025 study using KIR settlement-level data (2021–2024) found a "deepening divide between dynamic urban/suburban zones and disadvantaged rural peripheries," with disadvantaged children increasingly concentrated in rural areas (up to 94% in some settlements) while urban schools face capacity strain (child-teacher ratios up to 24:1). The number of school sites nationally fell from 9,046 (2021) to 8,590 (2024); primary-education sites fell from 3,609 to 3,521.
Source: "Shrinking, growing or overburdened?: Territorial patterns in child demographics and institutional burden in Hungary's public education system after 2021," ResearchGate, https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396483025_Shrinking_growing_or_overburdened_Territorial_patterns_in_child_demographics_and_institutional_burden_in_Hungary's_public_education_system_after_2021

**7. Historical precedent: 2004–2008 centralization reform halved the number of Hungarian villages independently maintaining a primary school.**
An Act on Multi-Functional Micro-Regional Local Government Associations (2004) rechanneled state incentives toward school amalgamation; within four years the number of villages maintaining their own primary school dropped by half, triggering the "Human Chain for Rural Schools" protest movement (2007) — showing that centralized fiscal/administrative levers, not just raw demographics, drive closure waves, and that community mobilization can occasionally preserve individual schools (e.g., Erdőkürt case study).
Source: "Rescuing a small village school in the context of rural change in Hungary," ScienceDirect (Journal of Rural Studies), https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0743016712000216

**8. Causal evidence (Italy, 2024): closing "undersized" rural schools accelerates local population and income decline, beyond pre-existing demographic trends.**
Exploiting an Italian school-network contraction reform, this study found that municipalities affected by school closures experienced "significant reductions in population and income," concluding that rationalizing service provision in isolated areas without valid alternatives can itself cause further depopulation — a feedback loop directly relevant to the brief's tension between efficiency and community viability.
Source: Di Cataldo, M. & Romani, G., "Rational cuts? The local impact of closing undersized schools," Regional Science and Urban Economics, 109 (2024), https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0166046224000887 (open access; also at https://eprints.lse.ac.uk/126034/)

**9. Aging teaching workforce compounds the rural viability problem.**
By the 2024/2025 academic year, half of all Hungarian teachers were 50 or older while only 7.2% were under 30; teacher shortages are described as "greatest in rural areas," with the share of unqualified teachers in disadvantaged schools 2–3 times higher than in non-disadvantaged schools — meaning even where pupil numbers might justify keeping a rural school open, staffing capacity may not follow.
Source: "Hungary - Education and Training Monitor 2025," European Commission, https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/hungary.html

**10. Number of women of childbearing age projected to keep shrinking through 2060, structurally capping any future birth rebound.**
Hungarian Demographic Research Institute (HDRI/KSH-affiliated) projections show the number of women aged 15–49 will fall to roughly 1.4 million by 2060 in the medium variant (from higher levels in 1990–2020), implying that even with increased childbearing propensity, national annual births are sustainable at only around 90,000/year — a level still below current replacement needs, reinforcing that the school-age population base for rural areas will not recover through fertility policy alone within the 2040 horizon.
Source: "MAIN FINDINGS," Demográfia (Hungarian Demographic Research Institute publication portal), https://demografia.hu/en/publicationsonline/index.php/demographicportrait/article/download/893/655/658

**Additional context (centralization governance backdrop):** Since 2013, Hungary's primary/secondary schools have been managed through the centralized Klebelsberg Center (KLIK, now KK) system rather than local municipalities, meaning closure/merger decisions for village schools are made through district-level (tankerület) administration rather than local government — a structural factor shaping how depopulation pressure translates into concrete school-network decisions. Some commentary argues the centralized district system itself has faced implementation criticism.
Source: "A Klebelsberg Központ áll, a tankerületi rendszer megbukott," Hírklikk, https://hirklikk.hu/kozelet/a-klebelsberg-kozpont-all-a-tankeruleti-rendszer-megbukott/405383

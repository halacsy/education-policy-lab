# Critique: cost_checker

## S1.cost_categories
Objection: Cost list omits (a) the capital-maintenance/backlog costs of under-enrolled buildings (roof, heating, accessibility upgrades) which are often a binding driver of closure decisions; (b) the opportunity cost of keeping space vacant (repurposing or rental income foregone); and (c) explicit fiscal-incidence mapping showing whether the state, municipality, or households bear each cost. Without these items the scenario treats ongoing operating costs as the only relevant fiscal line, biasing the trade-off toward retention.
Severity: medium
Suggested revision: Add explicit line items for: (1) one-off capital backlog remediation per building (estimate low/median/high ranges); (2) annualized opportunity cost of underused space (market rent forgone or conversion cost); and (3) a fiscal-incidence table that assigns each cost to payer (state, municipality, household) and models how per-pupil unit costs change with enrollment (e.g., show costs at 10, 20, 30 pupils). Require a short costing exercise using KSH/Oktatási Hivatal inputs to produce steady-state vs transition (first 3 years) cost ranges and a sensitivity analysis to ±20% enrollment and ±30% energy/fuel price shocks.

## S2.cost_categories
Objection: The cost list omits major transition and labour-relations costs: severance/compensation or redeployment costs for redundant teachers and support staff; temporary scale-up costs at central ISCED2 sites (modular classrooms, fast-track capital works) prior to permanent capital spending; and one-off transport procurement (buses) or contract ramp-up costs. It also provides no numeric ranges for the large capital and transport items, preventing fiscal sensitivity analysis. This underestimates short-term fiscal burdens and political feasibility risks tied to upfront payments.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Explicitly include: (a) teacher redundancy/retraining/redeployment costs (one-off severance and in-service retraining budgets) with range estimates per displaced FTE; (b) temporary capacity costs at receiving schools (modular classrooms, short-term leases) shown separately from long-run capital investment; (c) bus procurement or contract mobilisation costs (unit cost per bus, plus maintenance) and operating cost ranges per pupil-year. Provide example scenarios (e.g., consolidating 5 villages into one center: X pupils moved, Y extra classrooms required) and run a 10-year NPV comparing consolidation vs retention under low/medium/high assumptions. Commission rapid costing using district-level wage and construction unit costs to populate ranges.

## S4.cost_categories
Objection: The scenario quotes a central transport cost range (800–1,500 EUR/pupil/year) but fails to: (1) substantiate whether that Central-European range applies to Hungarian route lengths and occupancy rates; (2) include indirect household costs (parent time, childcare gaps, lost informal transport) and supervision costs for younger children; and (3) model how consolidation savings are split between state and municipal budgets (the text notes misalignment but does not quantify it). As written, the cost comparison risks misestimating net savings and equity impacts.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Replace the single-range assertion with a Hungary-specific transport costing module: estimate per-pupil transport costs across plausible commute-distance bands and occupancy rates, and break costs into capital (buses), operating (fuel, drivers), and supervision/insurance. Add indirect household cost estimates (valued time, additional childcare) and produce a distributional table showing fiscal incidence (state vs municipal vs household) of both consolidation savings and transport/mitigation costs. Require sensitivity checks for occupancy (50–90%), fuel-price scenarios, and average trip distance bands (0–10 km, 10–20 km, 20+ km). If local data are missing, flag the need for a targeted transport-costing pilot in 3 representative rural clusters before final policy recommendation.

## S5.cost_categories
Objection: Cost entries conflate setup and ongoing costs without quantifying scale or funding responsibilities: registry, training-module rollout, and data-analytic staffing are listed but not split into one-off (design, IT build, legal agreements) versus recurring O&M (hosting, data updates, analyst salaries). The scenario also omits the non-budgetary political/transaction costs (stakeholder consultations, legal challenges) that materially affect implementation budgets and sustainability under electoral volatility.
Severity: high
Suggested revision: Disaggregate all cost items into (A) one-off startup (design, IT procurement, legal/regulatory work, training-material development) and (B) recurring annual costs (hosting, maintenance, analyst and helpdesk FTEs, refresher training). Provide a 3-year budget envelope (CapEx vs OpEx) with funding-source mapping (central ministry, KSH, EU transition funds, municipal co-funding). Include a contingency line for political transaction costs (consultations, legal reviews, communications) and model budget volatility risk (e.g., effect of a 20% budget cut in year 3 on registry functionality). Recommend commissioning a three-month fiscal-impact assessment to produce numeric budgets and propose explicit governance/funding commitments to protect recurring operations across electoral cycles.

# Expert analysis: legal_and_governance

## Findings (evidence)
- Hungarian education law recognizes multiple possible maintainers of primary schools (state, municipality, church legal person, private/foundation, nationality self-government), establishing legal plurality in principle. [evidence: strong — Oktatási Jogok Biztosának Hivatala, https://www.oktbiztos.hu/ugyek/jelentes2000/iskola.html]
- Since 2013, most rural public primary schools are maintained by the state's tankerületi központ (district center) system under Klebelsberg Központ coordination, not by village councils, concentrating closure decisions at the state level. [evidence: strong — Magyarország Kormánya, https://cdn.kormany.hu/uploads/document/6/6f/6f5/6f5e46a56dc7d2bb420e0ef7d4d02c3cc7166aaf.pdf]
- School catchment-area (körzethatár) decisions follow a joint municipal-state procedure with municipalities holding only a consultative, not decisive, role; the district center designates enrollment zones by end of February if agreement is lacking. [evidence: strong — 20/2012 EMMI rendelet, https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1200020.emm]
- The Nkt. (National Public Education Act) statutorily defines the 'összevont osztály' (combined/multi-grade class) as a legal instrument enabling small rural schools with low enrollment to remain operationally viable without automatic closure. [evidence: strong — 2011. évi CXC. törvény (Nkt.), https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.tv]
- The 'felmenő rendszer' (grade-by-grade progression system) allows gradual, phased introduction of changes to institutional conditions, requiring annual legality review by government office with potential license withdrawal if commitments are not met. [evidence: strong — https://mkogy.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.TV]
- School closure or reorganization is exclusively the maintainer's prerogative under Nkt. §102(2); parents and communities suffer an 'interest injury' (érdeksérelem) rather than a legal injury, making closure decisions discretionary and not subject to parental legal appeal. [evidence: strong — Oktatási Jogok Biztosának Hivatala, https://www.oktbiztos.hu/ugyek/jelentes2000/iskola.html]
- When a school is closed, the law mandates that compulsory-age pupils receive legally guaranteed placement in another institution that 'may not refuse admission' and must be chosen so 'attendance does not impose a disproportionate burden' on the child. [evidence: strong — Nkt. consolidated text (ELTE), https://geogo.elte.hu/images/Koznevelesi_torveny.pdf]
- Maintainer-change (fenntartóváltás) from municipal to church, private, or other legal maintainer is a permitted legal pathway with a strict annual window (March 31 request deadline, April ministerial decision) for keeping rural schools open under new governance. [evidence: strong — kormanyhivatalok.hu, https://kormanyhivatalok.hu/sites/default/files/2025-01/tajekoztato_koznevelesi_intezmenyek_fenntartoi_reszere.pdf]
- A documented case (Beregdaróc, Gemzse, Gulács, Tiszaszentmárton) shows multi-site institutional consolidation under tankerületi központ (district center) maintenance as a rural-school survival model alternative to closure. [evidence: moderate — eduline.hu, https://eduline.hu/cimke/iskolabez%C3%A1r%C3%A1s]
- Government sources reported in January 2024 that a wave of small rural school closures (several dozen institutions) was planned by the Orbán government, timed after June 2024 municipal elections for political reasons, but the plan has not yet been published as official ministerial policy. [evidence: weak — HVG360, https://hvg.hu/360/20240118_Iskolak_tucatjait_zarna_be_a_kormany_az_onkormanyzati_valasztasok_utan]

## Interpretation
Hungarian law establishes a formal governance framework for rural primary schools that permits multiple maintainers and contains statutory safeguards (combined classes, felmenő system, disproportionate-burden protections, maintainer-change procedures) to keep schools operationally viable without automatic closure. However, the 2013 nationalization concentrated decision-making authority in state-level tankerületi központ structures, reducing municipal agency while preserving the legal framework. Closure decisions remain the maintainer's exclusive prerogative, and communities have no formal legal remedy—only discretionary appeals via ombudsman pathways. The 'aránytalan teher' (disproportionate burden) safeguard for pupils is the principal statutory protection against educational access collapse, yet its interpretation and enforcement remain administratively dependent. Governance plurality is thus legally recognized but structurally state-dominated, creating asymmetry between the law's pluralist architecture and its implementation. Recent government signals of planned closures indicate political pressure to rationalize, but no published ministerial policy yet codifies this shift.

## Assumptions
- The 'aránytalan teher' (disproportionate burden) safeguard for compulsory-age pupils is interpreted and enforced consistently across tankerületi központ districts, even though the statute does not specify exact distance, commute-time, or accessibility thresholds. [assumption]
- Maintainer-change procedures (fenntartóváltás) remain a politically and administratively accessible option for struggling rural schools; no informal barriers (budgetary, regulatory timing) prevent a church or private maintainer from taking over a municipal school within the statutory window. [assumption]
- Combined-class (összevont osztály) pedagogy and felmenő-system implementation do not suffer from resource constraints or teacher qualification gaps that would render them legally permissible but practically unworkable in small rural schools. [assumption]

## Position
Hungarian law provides statutory plurality and safeguards (combined classes, disproportionate-burden protection, maintainer-change pathways) to enable rural primary schools to remain open in depopulating areas, but state-level tankerületi központ dominance, the exclusivity of maintainer closure prerogative, and administrative interpretation of burden thresholds are the principal governance levers that will determine whether these legal protections translate into sustained educational access in villages under 2,000 population.

## Uncertainties
- Exact number of under-2,000-population village primary schools closed or merged in Hungary in the last 3 years (2022-2025) is not available from official KSH or KIR statistical datasets; government closure announcements remain anecdotal or sourced to anonymous government sources.

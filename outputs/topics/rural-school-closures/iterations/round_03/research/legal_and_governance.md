## Research Notes: Legal & Governance Dimension — Rural Primary Schools (Hungary)

**1. Maintainer plurality is legally established but structurally state-dominated.** Hungarian law recognizes multiple possible maintainers of a köznevelési intézmény (state, municipality, church legal person, private/foundation, nationality self-government), and the historical közoktatási törvény framework confirmed that 
the education law establishes among its basic principles that a public education institution can be founded and maintained by the state, the local municipality, the local minority self-government, the national minority self-government, a religious legal person registered in Hungary, as well as a legal entity, foundation, association or other legal person, or natural person established in Hungary
. Source: Oktatási Jogok Biztosának Hivatala report, https://www.oktbiztos.hu/ugyek/jelentes2000/iskola.html

**2. Since the 2013 nationalization, most rural public schools are maintained not by the village council but by the state's tankerületi központ (district center) system**, with the Klebelsberg Központ playing a central coordinating role — e.g. a 2023 government decree amending the 134/2016 decree "on bodies participating as maintainers in state public-education duties and on the Klebelsberg Center" shows ongoing state-level regulation of maintainer structures and shared procurement (e.g., mandatory study-record systems). Source: Magyarország Kormánya közlemény, https://cdn.kormany.hu/uploads/document/6/6f/6f5/6f5e46a56dc7d2bb420e0ef7d4d02c3cc7166aaf.pdf

**3. School catchment-area (körzethatár) decisions are a joint municipal-state procedure with a hard legal deadline**, giving municipalities only a consultative, not decisive, role: 
the municipality, and the maintainer of church and private institutions, must inform the competent district center of their opinion — or, in the case under Nkt. § 50(10), the nationality self-government of its consent or proposal to modify the catchment boundary — by February 15
, after which 
in the absence of agreement or in case of a proposal to modify the catchment area, the district center designates the enrollment catchment areas by the end of February following consultation
. Source: 20/2012 EMMI rendelet, https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1200020.emm

**4. The Nkt. formally defines the "összevont osztály" (multi-grade/combined class)** as a key legal instrument enabling small rural schools to remain viable: 
a combined class is a class formed at the lower grades of primary school, in a basic arts school, or in a special/conductive pedagogical institution, from students of at least two but no more than four school grades
. This is the principal statutory tool making single-teacher/low-enrollment rural schools legally operable rather than automatically requiring closure. Source: 2011. évi CXC. törvény (Nkt.), https://net.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.tv

**5. The "felmenő rendszer" (grade-by-grade progression system) is statutorily defined** as 
the gradual, phased introduction of changes to curricular and examination requirements, whereby the change may be introduced starting from the lowest affected school grade
. This principle also extends to institutional conditions: for newly launched educational forms, 
the conditions required for operation, for grades/classes/groups not yet launched in a given year, appropriate to the school type, may be gradually and progressively established in a felmenő (grade-by-grade) system
, subject to annual legality review by the government office, with withdrawal of the operating license if commitments are not met. Source: mkogy.jogtar.hu, https://mkogy.jogtar.hu/jogszabaly?docid=a1100190.TV

**6. Legally, school closure/reorganization is exclusively the maintainer's prerogative, not a matter of parental or student legal right** — a long-standing ombudsman finding still structurally valid: 
§102(2) of the former public education act states that the maintainer decides on the establishment, reorganization, and termination of a public education institution — meaning that decisions on establishing, reorganizing or closing a school fall exclusively within the right and responsibility of the municipality
, and complainants in closure cases typically suffer an "interest injury" (érdeksérelem) rather than a "legal injury" (jogsérelem) since the decision itself is discretionary. Source: Oktatási Jogok Biztosának Hivatala, https://www.oktbiztos.hu/ugyek/jelentes2000/iskola.html

**7. When a school is deregistered/closed, the law mandates a legally guaranteed alternative placement for compulsory-age pupils**: 
if the maintainer's right to organize education services ceases, the authority orders deletion from the registry simultaneously with the withdrawal of the operating license, and in its deletion decision designates another institution that may not refuse admission of the children/compulsory-age pupils who had a legal relationship with the deleted institution — for non-compulsory-age pupils this designation only occurs upon request — and the designated institution must be chosen so that attendance does not impose a disproportionate burden on the child or pupil
. This "aránytalan teher" (disproportionate burden) safeguard is the central statutory lever protecting rural children from excessive commuting after closure. Source: Nkt. consolidated text (ELTE), https://geogo.elte.hu/images/Koznevelesi_torveny.pdf

**8. Current political-legal signal (Jan 2024): a wave of small rural school closures was reported as planned by the government, timed after the June 2024 municipal elections.** According to hvg360's government-source reporting, 
the planned step means the closure of several dozen small-enrollment institutions nationwide still within that year, with the Orbán government judging the time ripe to pronounce the "death sentence" of small-village mini-schools, though postponing for political reasons until after the June municipal elections
, and 
since 2015 the merging of small schools or small upper-grade sections has repeatedly resurfaced in various forms under Orbán governments
. Source: HVG360, https://hvg.hu/360/20240118_Iskolak_tucatjait_zarna_be_a_kormany_az_onkormanyzati_valasztasok_utan

**9. Maintainer-change (fenntartóváltás) procedure for church/private takeover of a struggling municipal school has a strict annual window**: 
under Nkt. §84(8a), a church legal person, nationality self-government, or private maintainer may, based on acceptance of the initiative under paragraph (8) and an agreement on change in the exercise of founder/ownership rights, request authorization for maintainer change by the last day of March, and the minister responsible for public education decides on the request by the last working day of April
 — illustrating a real but narrow legal channel (used historically, e.g., church takeovers) for keeping a rural school physically open under a different legal maintainer when a municipality can no longer sustain it. Source: kormanyhivatalok.hu tájékoztató, https://kormanyhivatalok.hu/sites/default/files/2025-01/tajekoztato_koznevelesi_intezmenyek_fenntartoi_reszere.pdf

**10. Empirical illustration of maintainer transfer as a rural-school survival mechanism**: eduline.hu reporting notes an actual case where 
the Kisvárda District Center took over the maintenance of the Wesley János Kindergarten, Primary School, Gymnasium and Basic Arts School operating in Beregdaróc, Gemzse, Gulács and Tiszaszentmárton
 — a multi-site, cross-village institutional consolidation model rather than outright closure. Source: eduline.hu, https://eduline.hu/cimke/iskolabez%C3%A1r%C3%A1s

**Uncertainty flag for later structured phase:** I could not find a recent (2024-2026) official KSH or KIR statistical dataset quantifying exactly how many under-2,000-population village primary schools have closed/merged in the last 3 years; the hvg360 piece is sourced to an anonymous government source and is not yet confirmed by a published ministerial decision or KSH figure.

# Research Notes: Sequencing, Teacher-Supply, and Administrative-Bandwidth Evidence for Rural Primary School Policy in Hungary

**1. National teacher vacancy scale entering 2025/2026 school year**
The Teachers' Trade Union (PSZ) reports that roughly 16,000 teaching positions remained unfilled as the 2025/2026 school year began, which it describes as signaling a deep crisis in public education requiring urgent systemic restructuring given demographic decline. Source: "Sokkoló szeptember: iskolák a tanárhiány, az alacsony bérek és a demográfiai válság árnyékában," Pedagógusok Szakszervezete, Sept 2, 2025. https://pedagogusok.hu/hirek/sokkolo-szeptember-iskolak-a-tanarhiany-az-alacsony-berek-es-a-demografiai-valsag-arnyekaban/

**2. Rural areas bear the sharpest shortage intensity**
The EU's Education and Training Monitor 2025 country report for Hungary states that teacher shortages are greatest in rural areas, with the proportion of unqualified teachers in disadvantaged schools running 2–3 times higher than in non-disadvantaged schools, and notes that half of all teachers in 2024/2025 were 50+ while only 7.2% were under 30. Source: "Hungary - Education and Training Monitor 2025," European Commission. https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eac/education-and-training-monitor/en/country-reports/hungary.html

**3. Recruitment pipeline lag is structural, not just cyclical**
A 2024 policy discussion paper by the Hungarian Economic Association (MKT) states that even if newly-enrolled teacher-training students remain in the pipeline, increasing numbers of new entrants can at best reach schools only about 4 years later, and warns that Hungary is at the "last possible moment" to avert a severe teacher shortage given the workforce's age structure. Source: "VITAIRAT: Javaslatok a pedagógusok utánpótlásának fenntartható biztosításához," mkt.hu, 2024. https://mkt.hu/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Javaslatok-a-pedagogusok-utanpotlasanak-biztositasa-erdekeben_2024_clean.pdf

**4. Government's own EU commitment on small-school reorganization**
According to Gábor Gosztonyi (PSZ), the government committed to the EU in summer 2022 that village schools with class sizes under 10 pupils and total enrollment under 100–120 would be reorganized and integrated into central institutions — indicating an existing but unevenly implemented administrative threshold/trigger for consolidation. Source: "A kisiskolák bezárása nem új ötlet, de nem enyhítené a tanárhiányt," eduline.hu, Feb 6, 2024. https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20240206_kisiskola-iskolabezaras-tanarhiany

**5. "Traveling teacher" stopgap model has known operational limits**
Reporting on Győr-Moson-Sopron county describes how many rural teachers already commute among three-to-four schools simultaneously ("hontalan tanárok"), and school directors resort to informal barter of specialist teaching hours between institutions — a workaround that trade-union officials call unsustainable and disliked by school heads. Source: "Hontalan tanárok ingáznak a falusi iskolák között," eduline.hu, July 5, 2021. https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20210705_kistelepulesek_tanarhiany

**6. Government's current official stance (Oct 2025): explicit rejection of closures**
Prime ministerial commissioner Katalin Balatoni publicly rejected PSZ's renewed call for closures, stating the government's approach is to protect small-settlement schools "as long as there is demand," relying instead on mergers ("összevonás") and local solutions, while acknowledging some regions need new classrooms/container schools due to growth and others lose children to urban migration and commuting. Source: "Ismét napirenden az iskolabezárás," Magyar Hírlap/MTI, Oct 7, 2025. https://www.magyarhirlap.hu/oktatas/20251007-ismet-napirenden-az-iskolabezaras

**7. Administrative volatility/reversibility of consolidation decisions**
A district-level (Budapest XVIII) school-merger plan was withdrawn by the district education center (Külső-Pesti Tankerületi Központ) in March 2025 after parent protests (a 4,000-signature petition and two demonstrations), showing that even initiated administrative reorganization processes can be reversed under political/community pressure — a relevant timeline-risk factor for any planned rural consolidation. Source: "Visszalépett a tankerület: mégsem szűnnek meg a XVIII. kerületi iskolák," eduline.hu, March 21, 2025. https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20250321_Visszalepett-a-tankerulet-megsem-szunnek-meg-a-XVIII-keruleti-iskolak

**8. Scale of settlements already without any school**
A 2025 academic study using settlement-level data (2021–2024) found that by 2024 more than 1,200 of Hungary's 3,155 settlements had no public educational institution at all, and identified a widening divide between overburdened growth areas and disadvantaged, declining rural peripheries — relevant for sequencing which regions face closure vs. expansion pressure simultaneously. Source: "Shrinking, growing or overburdened? Territorial patterns in child demographics and institutional burden in Hungary's public education system after 2021," ResearchGate/Academia.edu, 2025. https://www.researchgate.net/publication/396483025_Shrinking_growing_or_overburdened_Territorial_patterns_in_child_demographics_and_institutional_burden_in_Hungary's_public_education_system_after_2021

**9. Incentive/administrative lever already funded but limited in scale**
Under the "Magyar Falu Program," the government earmarked 5 billion HUF for building teacher service housing ("szolgálati lakás") in 100 small settlements, intended to help retain teachers in villages — an administrative tool already in motion but covering only a small fraction of the thousands of affected settlements. Source: "Hontalan tanárok ingáznak a falusi iskolák között," eduline.hu, July 5, 2021. https://eduline.hu/kozoktatas/20210705_kistelepulesek_tanarhiany

**10. Planned closure numbers floated but politically delayed**
Press reports (Jan 2024) indicated government plans to close around 60 small-village "mini-schools" with class sizes of 7 or fewer pupils, but sources suggested the administration would wait until after the June 2024 municipal elections to act — illustrating how electoral cycles affect the realistic timeline for implementing consolidation. Source: "Elérkezhetett az idő, hogy a kormány kistelepülési 'miniiskolákat' zárjon be," Pénzcentrum, Jan 19, 2024. https://www.penzcentrum.hu/cimke/iskolabez%C3%A1r%C3%A1s

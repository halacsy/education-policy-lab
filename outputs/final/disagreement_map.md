# Disagreement map (final)

Preserved per the Habermas-machine principle: mapped, not collapsed into consensus. Minority positions also appear in the final brief.

## Disagreement map
### Structural vs. non-structural reform
- **equity_and_social_mobility, polish_reform, international_comparison**: Delayed selection (structural change) is necessary; compensation alone cannot offset sorting this early. Why: Cross-country tracking evidence and the Hungarian SES gradient.
- **portuguese_reform, political_feasibility** (minority): A non-structural package (standards, targeted support) is preferable: comparable gains, far lower political risk. Why: Portugal's improvement without changing selection age; Poland's reversal.

### Speed of any phase-out
- **equity_and_social_mobility** (minority): Cap new entries immediately; every delayed cohort pays the equity cost. Why: Each admission cycle sorts ~10% of a cohort irreversibly.
- **implementation_planning, school_network_planning, legal_and_governance**: A sub-6-year transition is operationally and legally infeasible; forcing it guarantees chaotic failure. Why: Teacher supply, felmenő rendszer guarantees, network re-planning lead times.

### What the gimnazium forms actually produce
- **equity_and_social_mobility, international_comparison**: Mostly selection effect; modest causal value added does not justify the system-wide equity cost. Why: Hungarian value-added studies.
- **political_feasibility, hungarian_education_system** (minority): Even if gains are partly positional, the forms answer a real demand for demanding academic tracks; suppressing them shifts demand to private exit. Why: Parental attachment evidence; growth of non-state maintainers.

### Bundling with network consolidation
- **demography, school_network_planning**: Bundle selection reform with the demography-forced network redesign to share transition costs. Why: Cohort shrinkage forces consolidation regardless.
- **political_feasibility** (minority): Bundling couples the reform to unpopular school closures and doubles the opposition coalition. Why: Closure politics historically dominates local media cycles.

PROBLEM: Many small villages in Hungary are facing depopulation, leading to declining student numbers in their local primary schools. While closing schools might seem like a rational response to low enrollment and resource constraints, it can also sever a vital community hub, increase travel burdens for children, and exacerbate the decline of rural areas. The tension lies between the need for efficient resource allocation and educational provision, and the desire to maintain community infrastructure and local identity in depopulating regions.

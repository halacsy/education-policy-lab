## C. Effects Without Intervention / Effects of Closure (Comparative and Hungarian-adjacent Evidence)

**6. the study examines how school closures affect everyday life in rural communities using a time-geographical approach... the rural school as a time-space hub... everyday life is organised around the school, which serves as the hub of activities where social networks are created and strengthened.
 Crucially,

The families also pointed towards the loss of social networks, thus given them less meaning and flexibility in their everyday lives.
 *Contested evidence*: cost/efficiency benefit (administrative view) versus lived social cost (resident view) are both empirically documented but not reconciled in this literature.

**7. Consolidation (merging small schools into larger ones) has a documented, if contested, academic cost in the short run, concentrated on students from the smallest schools.** A Danish quasi-experimental study found:

A special ministerial agency, the Klebelsberg institution maintenance centre... was set up to carry out these tasks.

 *Inference*: viability decisions are formally centralized/state-run, not made by village councils, which limits local communities' unilateral power either to close or to save a school — this is a structural constraint on any "proposal" being locally implementable.

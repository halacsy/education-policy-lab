# SOURCE_NOTE

**What this is:** World Bank Policy Research Working Paper No. 5263 (April 2010) — Jakubowski, Maciej; Patrinos, Harry Anthony; Porta, Emilio Ernesto; Wisniewski, Jerzy: "The Impact of the 1999 Education Reform in Poland". This is the openly licensed working-paper version of the study later published as: Jakubowski, Patrinos, Porta & Wisniewski (2016), "The effects of delaying tracking in secondary school: evidence from the 1999 education reform in Poland", *Education Economics* 24(6): 557–572, https://doi.org/10.1080/09645292.2016.1149548 (journal version paywalled at Taylor & Francis). Using PISA 2000/2003/2006 data, it shows that Poland's 1999 reform — which replaced early vocational tracking after 8-year primary school with comprehensive lower-secondary gimnazjum, delaying tracking by one year — was followed by a large improvement in performance, concentrated among students who would previously have entered vocational tracks.

## PSZ (Pedagógusok Szakszervezete) — 2023 teacher-shortage calculation
Presented 2023-09-18 by PSZ president Totyik Tamás and education researcher
Nahalka István, based on országos kompetenciamérés site reports, KSH and
social-security data.

Key claims (paraphrased):
- 2020/21 school year: 20,587 teachers missing from public education
  (higher than the previously assumed ~16,000); support staff (NOKS) shortage
  ~14,032; total missing staff ~34.6 thousand.
- Shortage most acute in mathematics teachers and tanítók (primary teachers);
  about half of school sites reported some HR crisis (~5,015 unfilled posts
  self-reported at kompetenciamérés).
- Nahalka: "teacher shortage" is not an absolute concept — definitional choices
  drive the government-vs-union "number war".

## PDSZ (Pedagógusok Demokratikus Szakszervezete) — surveys
- 2022 member survey (Infostart, 2022-05-24):
  https://infostart.hu/belfold/2022/05/24/pdsz-felmeres-kiugro-merteku-a-szakemberhiany-az-oktatasban
  Key claims: 82.1% of respondents reported staff shortage lasting >2 months
  in a year at their institution; most common in general subjects (59.7%),
  tanító posts (35.5%), language teaching (35%), gyógypedagógia (31.6%).
- 2025 survey coverage (Pénzcentrum, 2025-09-02):
  https://www.penzcentrum.hu/oktatas/20250902/durvabb-a-pedagogushiany-mint-eddig-gondoltuk-ki-fogja-igy-tanitani-a-diakokat-tanevkezdes-utan-1184723
  Key claim: shortage worse than assumed; 73.7% of teachers unsure of their
  performance-based pay; PDSZ notes no official calculation of required
  teacher numbers exists.
- PDSZ homepage (primary source hub): https://pdsz.hu/

# SOURCE_NOTE

**What this is:** IZA Discussion Paper No. 2204 — Pekkarinen, Tuomas; Uusitalo, Roope; Pekkala, Sari (July 2006): "Education Policy and Intergenerational Income Mobility: Evidence from the Finnish Comprehensive School Reform". This is the freely distributed working-paper version of the article later published as: Pekkarinen, T., Uusitalo, R. & Kerr, S. (2009), "School tracking and intergenerational income mobility: Evidence from the Finnish comprehensive school reform", *Journal of Public Economics* 93(7–8): 965–973, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpubeco.2009.04.006. (Sari Pekkala later published as Sari Pekkala Kerr; the journal version is paywalled at Elsevier.) The paper exploits the staggered rollout (1972–77) of Finland's comprehensive school reform, which postponed tracking from age 11 to age 16, and finds the reform reduced the intergenerational income elasticity by about a quarter (from ~0.30 to ~0.23).

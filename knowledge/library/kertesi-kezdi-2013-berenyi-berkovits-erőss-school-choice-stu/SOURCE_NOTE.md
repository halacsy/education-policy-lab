### 1. kertesi-kezdi-2013-bwp1312-school-segregation-100-towns.pdf (~3.4 MB)
- **What it is:** Kertesi, Gábor – Kézdi, Gábor (2013): "School segregation, school choice and educational policies in 100 Hungarian towns." Budapest Working Papers on the Labour Market, No. BWP 2013/12. Institute of Economics, Centre for Economic and Regional Studies, Hungarian Academy of Sciences – Department of Human Resources, Corvinus University of Budapest. (Also published as a report by the Roma Education Fund, 2013.)
- **Content:** Measures Roma/non-Roma school segregation in Hungary's 100 largest towns; shows segregation grew strongly after the 1980s, that free school choice weakens the link between residential and school segregation (commuting increases between-school inequality), and that local educational policies are on average mildly segregationist with large heterogeneity across towns.
- **URL used:** Official series URL http://www.econ.core.hu/file/download/bwp/bwp1312.pdf (as listed on RePEc, https://ideas.repec.org/p/has/bworkp/1312.html, download restriction: none). The econ.core.hu server was unreachable at download time, so the identical file was retrieved from the Internet Archive capture of that exact official URL: `https://web.archive.org/web/20220308143216if_/http://www.econ.core.hu/file/download/bwp/bwp1312.pdf`
- **License basis:** The Budapest Working Papers on the Labour Market series is distributed free of charge by the publishing institute (RePEc records no download restriction); the Roma Education Fund edition was likewise a freely distributed report.

### 2. berenyi-berkovits-eross-2005-educatio-iskolavalasztas-az-ovodaban.pdf (~230 KB)
- **What it is:** Berényi Eszter – Berkovits Balázs – Erőss Gábor (2005): "Iskolaválasztás az óvodában: a korai szelekció gyakorlata." *Educatio*, 2005/4 (Óvodák issue), pp. 805–824.
- **Content:** Case-study–based analysis of how school choice effectively begins in kindergarten in Hungary: kindergarten "school-readiness" assessments, parental strategies and school entrance selection channel children into different first-grade classes/schools, institutionalizing early social selection before compulsory schooling starts.
- **URL used:** https://folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu/sites/default/files/article_attachments/berenyie_0504.pdf
- **License basis:** Downloaded from the official journal archive operated by the Oktatási Hivatal (folyoiratok.oh.gov.hu), where the Educatio back catalogue is published for free public access.

### 1. horn-2013-diverging-performances-accepted-manuscript.pdf (~355 KB)
- **What it is:** Author's accepted manuscript (green open access) of: Horn, Dániel (2013): "Diverging performances: the detrimental effects of early educational selection on equality of opportunity in Hungary." *Research in Social Stratification and Mobility*, Vol. 32 (June 2013), pp. 25–43. DOI: [10.1016/j.rssm.2013.01.002](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rssm.2013.01.002)
- **Content:** Shows that Hungary's early-selective academic tracks (6- and 8-year gimnázium, selecting at ages 10 and 12) disproportionately recruit high-SES students even controlling for prior test scores; that track value-added diverges between grades 6–8 and 8–10; and that students left behind in general schools where the best can opt out perform worse in mathematics — i.e. early selection harms those left behind.
- **Source of file:** EUI Cadmus institutional repository, handle http://hdl.handle.net/1814/27817, bitstream `final_submitted.pdf` (Horn wrote the paper as a Max Weber Fellow at the European University Institute). Direct Cadmus download was blocked at fetch time, so the identical file (same content hash) was retrieved from the Internet Archive capture of the official Cadmus bitstream URL: `https://web.archive.org/web/20240607075701if_/https://cadmus.eui.eu/bitstream/handle/1814/27817/final_submitted.pdf`
- **License basis:** Green open-access self-archived accepted manuscript in the author's institutional repository; Unpaywall/Semantic Scholar record the OA status of this deposit as freely available (repository copy, license flag "public-domain" in Semantic Scholar's metadata). The publisher's typeset version at ScienceDirect remains paywalled — cite the journal version, use this manuscript for reference.

### 3. kertesi-kezdi-2012-kozgazdasagi-szemle-roma-nem-roma-teszteredmenyek.pdf (~1.8 MB)
- **What it is:** The published journal version of the same study: Kertesi Gábor – Kézdi Gábor (2012): "A roma és nem roma tanulók teszteredményei közti különbségekről és e különbségek okairól." *Közgazdasági Szemle*, LIX. évf., 2012. július–augusztus, pp. 798–853.
- **Content:** Using the 2006 National Assessment of Basic Competencies (Országos kompetenciamérés) linked to the Életpálya (life-course) survey, decomposes the roughly one-standard-deviation Roma/non-Roma test score gap in grade 8; shows the gap is largely explained by social disadvantages (poverty, parental education, home environment, school quality) rather than ethnicity per se — comparable to the Black–White test score gap in the US of the 1980s.
- **URL used:** https://epa.oszk.hu/00000/00017/00193/pdf/EPA00017_Kozgazdasagi_szemle_2012_07-08_03%20Kertesi%20Kezdi.pdf
- **License basis:** Közgazdasági Szemle is archived with the publisher's permission in the Elektronikus Periodika Archívum (EPA) of the National Széchényi Library, where the full text is freely and publicly downloadable; the journal itself makes its archive freely available (kszemle.hu).
